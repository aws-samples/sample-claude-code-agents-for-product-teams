# UI/UX Designer in Iterate

Iterate is where continuous discovery pays off. You co-own the ongoing insights feed with Business Analyst, turning a cycle's worth of real-user behavior into the usability evidence the next Discover cycle needs. Your voice here shapes what "desirable" looks like going forward.

## What you do

- Co-own continuous discovery with Business Analyst — synthesize behavioral and qualitative signal.
- Turn usability patterns into design-direction inputs for the next cycle.
- Feed usability friction into the bug and opportunity backlogs as warranted.
- Maintain the research-and-observation muscle across the operating cadence.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [ongoing insights feed](artifacts/ongoing-insights-feed.md) — Business Analyst owns; you co-produce the usability and behavioral layer.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — bi-directional on continuous discovery and insights synthesis.
- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on roadmap and deprecation implications.
- **Customer Support** — bi-directional on qualitative customer signal.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on experimentation design where UX hypotheses are tested.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with a real-user research base, not a speculative one. Carry that into the next Discover cycle — user/problem validation, personas, and journey mapping are all sharper when they're refinements of live evidence rather than new-ground hypotheses. That compounding insight is how design de-risks future bets.
