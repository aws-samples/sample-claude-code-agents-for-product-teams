# scheduled benefits reviews

_Produced by: Benefits realization coordination_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when benefits review happens in the KPI cadence

## What this is

The calendar of post-launch benefits-review checkpoints the PM coordinates — at 30, 90, 180 days and beyond — with inputs required, attendees, and decisions expected. Holds the org to its benefits-realization commitments.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Benefits reviews slip off calendars first; teams are onto the next project and no one remembers to circle back. Scheduling them up front, with owners and escalation, is how you preserve PDLC loop integrity and keep the Sponsor accountable to the bet.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Review calendar lists dates and named owners
- [ ] Inputs required per review (KPIs, telemetry, BA analysis) are specified
- [ ] Attendees and decision rights are defined
- [ ] Escalation path when targets are missed is documented
- [ ] Exit criteria for the last scheduled review are stated

## What it contains

- Review calendar with dates and owners
- Inputs required per review (KPIs, telemetry, BA analysis)
- Attendees and decision rights
- Escalation path if targets are missed
- Archive of past reviews and decisions
- Exit criteria for the last scheduled review

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved benefits plan
- Ongoing benefits tracking from Operate
- Benefits-realization report
- Governance calendar from Plan phase
- Portfolio investment decision timing

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — attends the reviews and exercises decision rights
- Business Analyst — prepares inputs
- PMO — ensures discipline across projects
- Finance — calibrates reforecasting

## Common pitfalls

- Reviews scheduled but without required inputs; meeting wastes time
- Missing escalation path; shortfalls discussed but no action taken
- All reviews at 30/90; nothing tracks the long tail
- Calendar owned by PM alone; when PM rolls off, reviews die
