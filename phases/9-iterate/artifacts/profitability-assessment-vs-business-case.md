# profitability assessment vs. business case

_Produced by: Commercial performance review_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Business Analyst, Architect

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP isn't monetized

## What this is

The structured comparison of actual commercial performance — revenue, margin, CAC, LTV, payback — against the forecasts and assumptions baked into the approved business case at Discover/Define. Owned by Sponsor; authored with BA and Architect.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

This is where the original bet gets re-underwritten. It is the direct input to the kill/pivot/double-down decision; without it, the portfolio keeps paying for products whose economics have quietly turned negative. The honesty of this assessment is the integrity of the whole PDLC loop.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Revenue actual vs. forecast is broken down by segment and plan
- [ ] Margin actual vs. target is reported with COGS decomposition
- [ ] CAC, LTV, and payback are compared to business-case assumptions
- [ ] Commercial-model validity (pricing, packaging, channels) is assessed
- [ ] A scenario view and recommendation feeding the portfolio investment decision are included

## What it contains

- Revenue actual vs. forecast by segment and plan
- Margin actual vs. target; COGS decomposition
- CAC, LTV, payback vs. business-case assumptions
- Commercial-model validity (pricing, packaging, channels)
- Scenario view for next-period forecast
- Recommendation into the portfolio investment decision

## Inputs you rely on

- Revenue report and margin & CAC/LTV report from Operate
- Approved business case and any prior reforecast
- Benefits-realization report
- Win-loss insights
- Architectural health and cost/capacity trajectory

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — feeds portfolio investment decision
- CFO and Finance — reforecast
- Product Owner — updated roadmap priorities
- Board — investor-grade review

## Common pitfalls

- Comparing against a forecast that was already quietly revised
- Excluding fully-loaded costs (headcount, support, infra) to flatter margin
- Measuring before the ramp window closed
- Recommendation hedged; Sponsor can't make a decision from it
