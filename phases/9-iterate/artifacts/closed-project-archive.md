# closed project & archive

_Produced by: Project closure_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The formal closure package: final budget and schedule reconciliation, artifact archive, open-item handoff to steady-state owners, and the signed closure approval. The moment "the project" ends as an organizational entity.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Projects that don't close cleanly leave accounting ambiguity, unassigned accountability, and orphaned artifacts. Formal closure lets the PM and the org recognize completion, free budget and headcount for the next bet, and preserve the record for audit.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Final budget and schedule reconciliation is included
- [ ] Outstanding items are listed with named steady-state owners
- [ ] Artifact and repository archive location and retention are recorded
- [ ] Closure sign-off from Sponsor and PMO is captured
- [ ] Final status communication is attached

## What it contains

- Final budget and schedule reconciliation
- Outstanding items and their steady-state owners
- Artifact and repository archive with retention
- Contracts closed or transitioned
- Closure sign-off from Sponsor and PMO
- Final status communication

## Inputs you rely on

- Budget burn report and final project schedule
- Steady-state operating handoff
- RAID log resolution status
- Lessons-learned repository entries
- Retrospective report

## Who consumes it

- PMO and Finance — close the books
- Sponsor — formal mark-complete
- Future auditors — traceable project record
- Teams taking over residual work — clear ownership handoff

## Common pitfalls

- Closing the project while open items still exist without new owners
- Retention policy not applied; artifacts vanish or linger indefinitely
- No formal sign-off; project drifts in a half-closed state
- Contracts left in auto-renew when the project is done
