## What this is

An assessment of emerging or changing regulations that affect the product — AI rules, data-residency mandates, privacy changes, industry-specific obligations — and the work required to stay compliant.

## Why it matters for the Security & Compliance team

Regulatory change is the blindside risk — a single new mandate can wipe quarters of roadmap capacity. Tracking and assessing impact early lets you bake work into the plan, negotiate timelines, and avoid emergency re-architecture sprints when enforcement dates arrive.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each regulation or guidance is named with its effective date
- [ ] Jurisdiction and applicability to this product are assessed
- [ ] Impact on controls, data flows, and features is specified
- [ ] Required work is described with a rough effort estimate
- [ ] Options and a recommended posture are stated, with tracking until compliant

## Inputs you rely on

- Applicable-regulations list from Define
- Threat model and data inventory & classification

- External intelligence and industry bodies

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — prioritization vs. feature work
- Architect — feeds architecture and modernization plans
- Product Owner — slots regulatory work into roadmap
- Legal and Privacy — aligns customer-contract commitments

## Common pitfalls

- Spotting regulation only when enforcement starts — too late
- Assessing applicability in the abstract, without mapping to actual data flows
- No cost estimate — work can't compete for roadmap capacity
- Compliance theater without engineering work behind it
