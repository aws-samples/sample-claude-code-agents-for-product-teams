# next-cycle opportunity backlog

_Produced by: Hand off insights to Discover_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Product Owner

**Stakeholders:** Business Analyst

## What this is

The curated set of validated opportunities handed from Iterate back into Discover for the next cycle — each with enough evidence and framing to become a candidate opportunity brief. The live artifact that makes the PDLC an actual loop.

## Why it matters for the Product Owner

Without a clean handoff, the next Discover cycle starts cold and reverts to opinion-driven brainstorming. A tight opportunity backlog ensures Discover inherits the best evidence this product has produced, compounding learning across cycles instead of restarting.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each opportunity has a statement with supporting evidence attached
- [ ] Persona, segment, and estimated value are included
- [ ] Confidence in problem-solution fit is stated
- [ ] Prior experiments or user research are referenced
- [ ] A recommended Discover posture (investigate, fast-track, defer) is assigned per opportunity

## What it contains

- Opportunity statements with supporting evidence
- Persona, segment, and estimated value
- Confidence in problem-solution fit
- Prior experiments or user research referenced
- Dependencies on other backlog items
- Recommended Discover posture (investigate, fast-track, defer)

## Inputs you rely on

- Feedback insights report and ongoing insights feed
- Adoption metrics and experiment results
- Win-loss insights and support/VoC trend analysis
- Benefits-realization and shortfall reports
- Lessons-learned repository

## Who consumes it

- Next Discover cycle — becomes opportunity briefs
- Sponsor — portfolio-level bet shaping
- Product Owner and BA — triage and prioritization
- Design — upstream context for discovery research

## Common pitfalls

- Feature requests masquerading as opportunities
- No evidence attached; the next Discover has to re-validate from scratch
- Backlog copies every idea heard; noise buries signal
- Handoff to a different team without briefing; context is lost
