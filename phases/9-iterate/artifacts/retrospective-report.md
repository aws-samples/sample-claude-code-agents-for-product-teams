# retrospective report

_Produced by: Post-launch review / retrospective_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** Product Sponsor

## What this is

The post-launch retrospective write-up covering the whole initiative — what went well, what didn't, surprises, and specific actions to change next time. Facilitated by PM, participated by every role swim lane.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

The retrospective is the single most leveraged artifact PM produces at the end of a cycle. Honest, actionable retros convert painful experience into durable improvements; hollow ones waste the most teachable moments the team will ever have.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] What went well is documented with evidence
- [ ] What didn't work is documented with contributing causes
- [ ] Surprises and unknowns are captured
- [ ] Specific actions carry owners and delivery dates
- [ ] Lessons are contributed to the organization-wide lessons-learned repository

## What it contains

- What went well (evidence-backed)
- What didn't work and why (contributing causes)
- Surprises and unknowns
- Specific actions with owners and delivery dates
- Lessons contributed to the organization-wide repository
- Participation and psychological-safety assessment

## Inputs you rely on

- Benefits-realization report and KPI performance report
- RCAs and postmortems
- Budget burn and schedule reconciliation
- Stakeholder feedback from closure interviews
- Lessons-learned repository (prior cycles)

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — prioritizes cross-initiative improvements
- PMO — updates PDLC templates and playbooks
- Future project teams — avoid repeating failures
- Engineering/Design leaders — team-level actions

## Common pitfalls

- Politeness wins; real issues stay unspoken
- Actions phrased as aspirations, not commitments
- No follow-up on last retro's actions; same lessons recur
- Written by PM alone instead of the full team
