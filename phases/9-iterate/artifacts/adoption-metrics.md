# adoption metrics

_Produced by: Feature adoption analysis_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** Product Owner

**Accelerated MVP:** required — adoption is the MVP's signal

## What this is

Feature-level adoption — who uses what, how often, in what workflows, and with what stickiness. Sliced by persona, segment, and cohort so low adoption can be diagnosed (discovery, friction, fit) rather than just observed.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

A feature that ships but isn't adopted earns no benefits; a feature adopted broadly but shallowly signals a discovery or UX problem. Adoption metrics are how you move the post-launch conversation from "we shipped it" to "is it earning?"

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Active-user counts and trend are reported per feature
- [ ] Adoption funnel stages (discovery, first use, repeat use) are quantified
- [ ] Cohort curves and retention of adopters are included
- [ ] Actuals are compared to the adoption targets set in Define
- [ ] Each low-adoption feature has an attached hypothesis for the cause

## What it contains

- Active users per feature and trend
- Frequency and depth of use
- Adoption funnel (discovery → first use → repeat use)
- Cohort curves and retention of adopters
- Comparison to adoption targets set in Define
- Hypotheses for low-adoption features

## Inputs you rely on

- Telemetry plan and instrumented events
- Pre-launch metrics baseline and KPI definitions
- User personas and journey maps
- Support/VoC trend analysis
- Experiment results

## Who consumes it

- Product Owner — prune, promote, or promote-in-UI decisions
- UI/UX Designer — surfaces friction signals for redesign
- Marketing — adoption for messaging emphasis
- Iterate phase — input to updated roadmap and next-cycle backlog

## Common pitfalls

- Treating logins as adoption instead of meaningful use
- No cohort view; averaging hides stark differences
- Metrics without hypothesis; low adoption gets waved away
- Missing funnel; can't tell where users drop off
