# QA/Tester in Iterate

Iterate is where production-discovered defects get the rigor they deserve. You document bugs with reproduction details so the backlog is actionable, feed coverage gaps back into the regression suite, and translate a cycle of quality-in-prod signal into a sharper test strategy for next time.

## What you do

- Document bugs with reproduction details, severity, and reproducibility so fixes can be scoped.
- Feed coverage gaps surfaced in production into the regression suite.
- Contribute to prioritized bug list sequencing based on risk and reproducibility.
- Translate a cycle's quality signal into next-cycle test strategy recommendations.

## Artifacts you own

- [bug reports](artifacts/bug-reports.md) — the actionable, reproducible bug records that drive fix sequencing.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on bug prioritization.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on reproduction, fix design, and verification.
- **Customer Support** — bi-directional on customer-reported defects and reproduction signal.
- **Site Reliability Engineer** — bi-directional on incident-linked quality issues.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with a cycle of real-world defect evidence. Carry that into the next Discover cycle's feasibility and risk assessment — the bug patterns from this cycle are the highest-signal input you have into next-cycle test strategy, and into the Define-phase acceptance criteria that keep recurring classes of defects from shipping again.
