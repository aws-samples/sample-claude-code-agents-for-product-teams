# Developer in Iterate

Iterate is where you ship the fixes and improvements that keep the product honest. You push bug-fix patches, run experiments with Product Owner, and contribute to tech-debt grooming with Architect. The operate-what-you-build loop continues — and it informs the next build.

## What you do

- Ship bug-fix application updates so the quality backlog actually drains.
- Run A/B tests and experiments with Product Owner to validate hypotheses.
- Groom the tech-debt backlog with Architect so debt decisions are intentional.
- Feed code-level lessons into the next cycle's backlog and design decisions.

## Artifacts you own

- [patched application release](artifacts/patched-application-release.md) — the shipped bug-fix release.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [experiment results](artifacts/experiment-results.md) — Product Owner owns; you implement and run the experiments.
- [prioritized tech-debt list](artifacts/prioritized-tech-debt-list.md) — Architect owns; you contribute implementation-level debt grooming.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on experiments and bug-fix sequencing.
- **Architect** — bi-directional on tech-debt and modernization prioritization.
- **QA/Tester** — bi-directional on bug-report quality and fix verification.
- **Site Reliability Engineer** — bi-directional on reliability-debt lessons.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with a cycle's worth of code-level evidence — what broke, what paid off, what needs rework. Carry that into the next Discover cycle's feasibility memo and early technical inputs — the operate-what-you-build loop is how engineering signal compounds into sharper hypotheses next time around.
