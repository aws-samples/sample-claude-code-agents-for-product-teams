# Customer Support in Verify

You are here to bring real users onto the product before Launch — via UAT / beta — and to stress-test your support readiness against their actual experience. Verify is where you discover the gaps in your KB, runbooks, and escalation paths while the stakes are still low enough to fix them.

## What you do

- Recruit and onboard the UAT / beta cohort jointly with Sales & Marketing.
- Run real support on the UAT / beta cohort — this is your dress rehearsal.
- Tighten the knowledge base and runbooks against the issues actually arising.
- Align with Technical Writer on deflection-oriented content gaps.

## Artifacts you own

- [recruited UAT cohort](artifacts/recruited-uat-cohort.md) — co-owned with Sales & Marketing.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't formally contribute to other Verify-phase artifacts beyond the cohort.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Sales & Marketing** — co-owns cohort recruitment; shared ownership of the beta experience.
- **Technical Writer** — KB and deflection content gaps you spot.
- **Product Owner** — flags product issues that will create support load at Launch.
- **QA/Tester** — aligns on defects that surface through real support vs. UAT testing.

## Handoff into [Launch](../7-launch/README.md)

In Launch you cut over support processes, train alongside Sales, and go live. You know Verify is done when the cohort is active, your KB deflects the top questions they're asking, and you're confident support can absorb the Launch-day spike without losing service quality.
