# benefits-readiness report

_Produced by: Benefits-readiness assessment_

**Business outcome supported:** evidence-based readiness to launch — functional, non-functional, operational, and documentation — validated in production-like conditions.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when benefits are measured live in Operate

## What this is

Pre-launch check on whether the mechanics for realizing and measuring benefits are live — KPI telemetry wired, baselines captured, attribution method agreed, data owners named, and review cadence scheduled. Not the benefits themselves; the capability to track them.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

The worst benefits-tracking conversation happens 90 days post-launch: "We forgot to capture the baseline." Running a benefits-readiness check in Verify is how you make sure the benefit story can actually be told from day one and that the Sponsor inherits a measurable commitment, not vapor.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each benefit has a readiness state (KPI wired, baseline captured, reviews scheduled)
- [ ] Evidence of KPI telemetry emission is attached
- [ ] Pre-launch baseline values are captured
- [ ] Attribution method is confirmed with Finance and the named data owner
- [ ] Gaps with mitigation plan and an Operate cadence plan are documented

## What it contains

- Benefit-by-benefit readiness state (KPI wired, baseline captured, reviews scheduled)
- Evidence of KPI telemetry emission
- Pre-launch baseline values captured
- Attribution method confirmed with Finance and data owner
- Gaps and mitigation plan
- Cadence plan for ongoing benefits tracking in Operate

## Inputs you rely on

- Benefits plan and approved benefits plan
- KPI definitions and verified KPI telemetry
- Telemetry plan and connected KPI data pipeline (plans)
- Pre-launch metrics baseline (if already captured)
- Data owner agreements

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — confirms before approving launch
- Project Manager — closes out Verify-phase coordination
- Business Analyst — owns the execution into Operate
- Finance — confirms attribution and measurement method

## Common pitfalls

- Telemetry present but not validated — KPI events aren't actually emitting
- No baseline captured — post-launch numbers have nothing to compare
- Attribution method left ambiguous and settled later by whoever has bigger voice
- Review cadence not scheduled — measurement drifts immediately
