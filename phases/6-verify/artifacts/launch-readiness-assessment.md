# launch-readiness assessment

_Produced by: Review launch-readiness metrics_

**Business outcome supported:** evidence-based readiness to launch — functional, non-functional, operational, and documentation — validated in production-like conditions.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** slim — a one-pager of green, yellow, red is enough

## What this is

The Sponsor's consolidated view of whether the product is truly ready to launch — synthesizing functional, NFR, operational, commercial, security, and comms readiness against the business-case success conditions. Input to the committed launch date and eventual go/no-go.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

You own the commitment the organization is about to make to customers, the market, and the P&L. This assessment is how you test — with evidence — that the many readiness threads have actually converged, rather than trusting that silence means readiness.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Readiness is stated across functional, NFR, security, ops, docs, GTM, and support dimensions
- [ ] Each dimension has quantified status with source evidence cited
- [ ] Known issues and accepted risks are enumerated
- [ ] Open items list owners and required-by dates
- [ ] Launch-cohort and rollout strategy confidence is recorded alongside an explicit recommendation (proceed, adjust, or delay)

## What it contains

- Readiness across dimensions: functional, NFR, security, ops, docs, GTM, support
- Quantified status per dimension with source evidence
- Known issues and accepted risks
- Open items with owners and required-by dates
- Launch-cohort and rollout strategy confidence
- Recommendation: proceed, adjust, or delay

## Inputs you rely on

- Readiness assessment from QA and Security
- PRR sign-off from SRE and Release Manager
- UAT/beta sign-off
- Compliance go/no-go
- Performance and accessibility test reports
- Launch-readiness status pack from PM

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor peers and executive committee
- Product Owner and Release Manager — confirm the launch plan
- Board or steering forums — investor-visible readiness signal
- Sales & Marketing — alignment of external launch moves

## Common pitfalls

- Checklist checked without evidence — "ready" means someone said so
- One strong dimension papering over another that's weak
- Launch cohort defined but not segmented for risk (too broad too fast)
- Assessment produced once and not refreshed as issues land during Verify
