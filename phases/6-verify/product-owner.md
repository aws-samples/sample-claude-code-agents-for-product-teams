# Product Owner in Verify

You are here to close the loop on value. Verify is where UAT tells you whether the thing you accepted in Build is the thing users actually want, and where you help the Sponsor decide who gets it first and what risks are acceptable. Your job is to represent user value in go/no-go decisions.

## What you do

- Co-sign UAT/beta pre-launch validation with QA/Tester so user acceptance is evidence-based, not wishful.
- Co-own the approved launch cohort with the Sponsor so who gets it first matches risk appetite.
- Co-own the known-issues risk acceptance with the Sponsor and Security & Compliance — you represent user impact.
- Stay close to UAT defect triage so what ships is what you'd want to ship.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own Verify-phase artifacts formally — your output is outcomes co-signed with others.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to other-owned Verify-phase artifacts beyond your outcomes.

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved launch cohort](outcomes/approved-launch-cohort.md) — co-owned with Product Sponsor.
- [approved known-issues & risk acceptance](outcomes/approved-known-issues-risk-acceptance.md) — co-owned with Product Sponsor and Security & Compliance.
- [UAT/beta sign-off](outcomes/uat-beta-sign-off.md) — co-owned with QA/Tester.

## Who you work with

- **QA/Tester** — co-signs UAT/beta sign-off; you bring user value perspective to their evidence.
- **Product Sponsor** — co-owns cohort and risk-posture with you.
- **Business Analyst** — hands you the benefits-readiness report so you know the bet is still measurable.
- **Security & Compliance** — co-owns risk posture when compliance risk affects launch cohort decisions.

## Handoff into [Launch](../7-launch/README.md)

In Launch you co-own the go/no-go decision with Release Manager and Sponsor, and co-own the pricing & packaging plan. You know Verify is done when UAT is signed, the cohort is approved, known issues are accepted or fixed, and you're ready to sign go/no-go with confidence — not hope.
