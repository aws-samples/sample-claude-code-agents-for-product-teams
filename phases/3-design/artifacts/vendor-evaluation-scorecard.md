# vendor evaluation scorecard

_Produced by: Vendor / buy option evaluation_

**Business outcome supported:** produce a build-ready design package — UX, architecture, data model, APIs, threat model, and integration map — with engineering signed off to build.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** Architect

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when no vendor is in scope

## What this is

A structured scoring of candidate vendors for any buy / partner component of the solution — functional fit, NFR fit, commercial terms, security/compliance posture, and strategic fit. It is the evidence behind "we picked vendor X" that finance, procurement, and the Sponsor will scrutinize.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

You own this with Architect so vendor choice is not a single-person preference dressed up as analysis. A well-run scorecard is also how you get leverage in commercial negotiations — credible alternatives keep the chosen vendor honest on pricing and terms.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Candidate vendors are listed with shortlisting provenance.
- [ ] Weighted criteria (functional, NFR, security, compliance, commercial, support, viability) are set before scoring.
- [ ] Each score cites evidence (demo, reference, POC on your data).
- [ ] TCO is computed over a 3–5 year horizon including exit cost.
- [ ] Recommendation names a next-best alternative and any conditions.

## What it contains

- Candidate vendors with provenance (how they were shortlisted)
- Weighted criteria: functional, NFR, security, compliance, commercial, support, viability
- Scored comparison with evidence per score
- Demo, reference-customer, and POC outcomes
- TCO over a 3–5 year horizon
- Risks and exit costs per vendor
- Recommended vendor and next-best, with conditions

## Inputs you rely on

- [Technical options assessment](technical-options-assessment.md) and [NFR document](../../2-define/artifacts/nfr-document.md)
- [Security control requirements](../../2-define/artifacts/security-control-requirements.md) and [vendor risk assessment](vendor-risk-assessment.md) in progress
- Reference customers and analyst input
- Procurement's contract-term expectations

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — ratifies via [build-vs-buy decision](../outcomes/build-vs-buy-decision.md)
- Procurement / Finance — negotiates commercial terms
- Project Manager — sequences vendor activities via [vendor selection process outputs](vendor-selection-process-outputs.md)
- Security & Compliance — finalizes [vendor risk assessment](vendor-risk-assessment.md)

## Common pitfalls

- Weighted criteria chosen after trial scoring to justify the desired answer
- Demos treated as proof of fit without a POC on your data
- Exit cost not scored, so lock-in becomes free in the comparison
- Missing operational-cost reality (seats, integrations, services)
