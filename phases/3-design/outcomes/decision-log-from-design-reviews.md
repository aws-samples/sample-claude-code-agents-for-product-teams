# decision log from design reviews

_Produced by: Design review scheduling & minutes_

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

## What this is

A dated record of decisions made during design reviews: what was decided, who decided, what alternatives were considered, and what triggered the decision.

## Why it matters

Design reviews make dozens of small decisions that shape the product for years. Without a log, teams re-debate them endlessly and lose institutional memory.

## What it contains

- Decision ID, date, owner, and context
- Options considered and why the chosen option won
- Affected artifacts and follow-up actions

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each entry has a unique ID, date, owner, and context recorded.
- [ ] Options considered and why the chosen option won are documented.
- [ ] Affected artifacts and follow-up actions are linked.
- [ ] The log is kept as a single canonical source, not scattered across minutes.

## Entry criteria

- Design review cadence established
- Minutes template in use

## Exit signal

New team members and future phases can trust the log as the canonical 'why' for the design.
