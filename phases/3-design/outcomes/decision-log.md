# decision log

_Produced by: Architecture decision records_

**Primary owner:** Architect

**Accelerated MVP:** required — future-you needs to know why the MVP looks the way it does

## What this is

Architecture Decision Records — the narrative of why the architecture looks the way it does.

## Why it matters

ADRs capture the trade-offs behind architectural choices so future engineers can extend the system without accidentally violating its constraints.

## What it contains

- One ADR per significant decision, with context / decision / consequences
- Superseded ADRs retained for history

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every significant architecture decision has its own ADR entry.
- [ ] Each ADR captures context, decision, and consequences.
- [ ] Superseded ADRs are retained (not deleted) and marked as superseded.
- [ ] The log is discoverable from the architecture document.

## Entry criteria

- Architect has begun documenting solution architecture

## Exit signal

Every meaningful design choice is traceable to an ADR.
