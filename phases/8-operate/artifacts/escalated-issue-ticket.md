# escalated issue ticket

_Produced by: Escalate user issues_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Customer Support

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

A ticket Support has decided cannot be resolved at its tier and is handing to engineering, security, or leadership. Structured with reproduction steps, impact assessment, and the support context so the receiving team can act immediately.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support team

Escalations are expensive for engineering and slow for customers. Good escalation hygiene (clean reproduction steps, clear impact, right receiving team) keeps engineering willing to respond quickly; sloppy escalations get deprioritized and erode the partnership.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Customer and account context are captured, including revenue implication if any.
- [ ] Reproduction steps include environment and product version.
- [ ] Observed vs. expected behaviour is stated explicitly.
- [ ] Support steps already tried are listed so engineering does not repeat them.
- [ ] Recommended receiving team and severity are named.

## What it contains

- Customer and account context
- Reproduction steps with environment and version
- Observed vs. expected behavior
- Customer impact and any revenue implication
- Support steps already tried
- Recommended receiving team and severity

## Inputs you rely on

- Triaged ticket queue and support playbooks
- Release notes and known-issues list
- Customer health score (for impact weighting)
- Support process model escalation paths
- Product support-matrix and version data

## Who consumes it

- Developer and SRE — receive and investigate
- Product Owner — visibility into escalation pattern
- Security & Compliance — for security-flagged tickets
- Engineering management — tracks escalation load

## Common pitfalls

- Missing reproduction steps; engineering punts back to Support
- Every escalation marked critical — priority signal dies
- Wrong receiving team; ticket bounces across engineering
- No closure loop to Support, so customer follow-up is blind
