# revenue report

_Produced by: Revenue / ARR tracking_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Sales & Marketing

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP isn't monetized

## What this is

The recurring commercial view of the product — ARR/MRR, new/expansion/contraction/churn components, pipeline coverage, win rates, and billing vs. booked revenue reconciliation. The top-line the business case promised and the board tracks.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

This is the primary scorecard for whether the bet is earning its keep. You use it to hold sales accountable, defend or redirect funding, and feed the Iterate reforecast; without a trusted revenue report, every portfolio conversation is negotiable instead of evidence-driven.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] ARR/MRR, bookings, and revenue are broken down by segment and plan.
- [ ] Net retention is stated with both gross retention and expansion components.
- [ ] Pipeline coverage is reported against target.
- [ ] Billing-to-revenue reconciliation is documented.
- [ ] Variance is measured vs. both the business case and the current plan.

## What it contains

- ARR/MRR, bookings, and revenue by segment and plan
- Net retention (gross retention + expansion)
- Pipeline coverage vs. target
- Win/loss rate and average deal size
- Billing-to-revenue reconciliation
- Variance vs. business case and variance vs. plan

## Inputs you rely on

- Live sales processes CRM data
- Renewal & expansion bookings from Customer Support
- Pricing & packaging plan and actual realized discount
- Win/loss insights
- Finance and RevOps source systems

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor and CEO/CFO — portfolio and resource decisions
- Sales leadership — territory and comp conversations
- Business Analyst — feeds profitability assessment in Iterate
- Board — investor-level rollups

## Common pitfalls

- Bookings reported as revenue, inflating the picture
- Net-retention headline hides churn problems in specific segments
- Pipeline coverage without stage-weighting — false confidence
- Lagging data that disagrees with Finance's close — review credibility
