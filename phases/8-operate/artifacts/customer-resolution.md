**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The record of a completed customer case — problem, investigation steps, resolution delivered, time to resolve, and customer confirmation. One per closed ticket; feeds CSAT and knowledge-base mining.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support team

Closed tickets are not just closed work; they are the raw material for knowledge-base growth, deflection improvement, and product-feedback signal. Capturing resolution discipline consistently is what separates a cost-effective support org from one that keeps reinventing fixes.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] The problem is described in the customer's own terms.
- [ ] Diagnostic steps and findings are recorded, not just the final answer.
- [ ] Resolution (or workaround with limits) is documented.
- [ ] Time to first response and total resolution are captured.
- [ ] Customer confirmation, CSAT, and tags for KB/product-feedback are recorded.

## What it contains

- Problem description from customer's perspective
- Diagnostic steps and findings
- Resolution or workaround delivered
- Time to first response and total resolution
- Customer confirmation and CSAT
- Tags for knowledge-base and product feedback

## Inputs you rely on

- Triaged ticket queue entry
- Known-issues list
- Customer-facing knowledge base
- Product release notes and user guides
- Engineering / escalation responses

## Who consumes it

- Tech Writer — knowledge-base updates
- Product Owner — frequent issues feed Iterate
- Business Analyst — ticket dataset for VoC trend analysis
- New agents — past resolutions as training material

## Common pitfalls

- Resolution steps written for insiders; not reusable as KB content
- Tags inconsistent across agents — analytics muddy
- No confirmation from customer; ticket closed but issue persists
- Product feedback buried in resolution narrative; never mined
