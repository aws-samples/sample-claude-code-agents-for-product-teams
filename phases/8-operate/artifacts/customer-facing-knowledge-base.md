# customer-facing knowledge base

_Produced by: Curate documentation for customers_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Technical Writer

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP's user base is small enough for direct help

## What this is

The curated, searchable knowledge base customers hit first when they have questions — how-tos, troubleshooting articles, FAQs, video walkthroughs, and the embedded help that deflects tickets. Continuously fed from support resolutions and product changes.

## Why it matters for the Technical Writer

KB deflection is where your time-to-value and cost-to-serve metrics converge. Each well-written article avoids N tickets forever; stale or missing articles generate the same questions every week. Keeping the KB fresh is your highest-leverage steady-state work.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] How-to articles cover the common tasks for each persona or plan.
- [ ] Troubleshooting content is structured as decision trees, not flat prose.
- [ ] FAQs are grouped by topic or persona with a consistent taxonomy.
- [ ] Search and navigation are tuned against the content taxonomy.
- [ ] Per-article analytics (views, helpful-rating, deflection) are recorded.

## What it contains

- How-to articles for common tasks
- Troubleshooting decision trees
- FAQs grouped by topic or persona
- Embedded videos and quickstart clips
- Search tuning and content taxonomy
- Analytics per article (views, helpful-rating, deflection)

## Inputs you rely on

- Feature documentation and user guides
- Tagged support-ticket dataset and customer resolutions
- Release notes and known-issues list
- Support/VoC trend analysis
- In-product help content hooks

## Who consumes it

- Customers — self-serve resolution
- Support agents — quick-link to resolution articles
- Sales engineers — share with prospects in trials
- Customer Success — reference during onboarding

## Common pitfalls

- Article sprawl without taxonomy; search becomes useless
- No analytics per article; no signal on what to improve
- Articles reflect an older product version
- Separate internal and external KBs that diverge
