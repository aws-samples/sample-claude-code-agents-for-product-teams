# renewal & expansion bookings

_Produced by: Renewal & expansion management_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Customer Support

**Stakeholders:** Sales & Marketing

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when there is no commercial motion

## What this is

The actual booked renewals and expansions — closed-won records with realized ARR, contract length, and discount. The "did it happen" counterpart to the renewal & expansion pipeline.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support / Success team

Shared ownership between CS and Sales only works if the numbers are shared and tracked. Booking records close the loop on your save-plays and expansion work and feed net-retention reporting that proves CS impact to leadership.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Renewed contracts include realized amount and term length.
- [ ] Expansions and upsells record realized ARR.
- [ ] Discounts granted have a documented reason.
- [ ] Churn (non-renewals) has a reason code captured.
- [ ] Forecast vs. actual variance is reported by CSM and segment.

## What it contains

- Renewed contracts with amount and length
- Expansions and upsells with realized ARR
- Discounts granted and reasons
- Churn (non-renewals) with reason codes
- Per-CSM, per-segment performance
- Forecast vs. actual variance

## Inputs you rely on

- Renewal & expansion pipeline
- Live sales processes (CPQ and CRM)
- Health scores & at-risk list
- Customer feedback and win/loss data
- Finance billing data

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor and CEO/CFO — net retention and revenue forecast
- Sales & Marketing — co-owned compensation and credit
- Business Analyst — profitability assessment in Iterate
- CS leadership — coaching and performance management

## Common pitfalls

- Expansion attributed entirely to Sales, demotivating CS
- Churn without reason codes; no learning loop
- Renewals autobooked without confirming customer value realization
- Discount drift — heavy discounts absorb margin silently
