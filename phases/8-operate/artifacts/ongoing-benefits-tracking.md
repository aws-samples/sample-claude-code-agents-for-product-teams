# ongoing benefits tracking

_Produced by: Value-realization tracking_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** slim — the KPI dashboard is the benefits tracker

## What this is

The BA's continuous measurement of realized benefits against the approved benefits plan — each committed outcome tracked with actuals, attribution assumptions, and variance. Feeds benefits realization in Iterate.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Benefits realization is how BA earns its keep post-launch. Without continuous tracking, the business case becomes a historical document and the "did it work?" conversation in Iterate becomes a debate instead of an audit. Consistent tracking makes that conversation evidence-based.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every committed benefit has a baseline, target, and current actual.
- [ ] An attribution model is stated with known confounders called out.
- [ ] Variance analysis and trend are shown per benefit.
- [ ] An "assumptions still valid?" check is documented for the underlying premises.
- [ ] Risks to realization and the benefits-review schedule are recorded.

## What it contains

- Each committed benefit with baseline, target, and current actual
- Attribution model and known confounders
- Variance analysis and trend
- Assumptions still-valid check
- Risks to benefits realization
- Schedule for benefits reviews

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved benefits plan and business case
- Pre-launch metrics baseline
- Connected KPI data pipeline
- Rolled-out process changes (for internal-benefits tracking)
- Support/VoC trend analysis

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — input to operational performance review
- Iterate phase — feeds benefits-realization report and benefits-shortfall report
- Product Owner — signals which bets are earning, which aren't
- Finance — validates the business case forecast

## Common pitfalls

- Attribution not specified — any outcome can be claimed or dismissed
- Stopping measurement once numbers look good
- Ignoring confounding events (market change, pricing, other launches)
- No "assumption still valid?" check — benefits depend on crumbling premises
