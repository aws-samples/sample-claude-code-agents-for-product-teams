# support/VoC trend analysis

_Produced by: Support data analysis_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The analytical view over the tagged support-ticket dataset — trends by category, severity, persona, and release; emerging issues; deflection rate; themes extracted from verbatims. Turns the ticket stream into Voice of Customer signal for Product.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Product Owner and Support both ask "what are customers struggling with?" Without this analysis, each answers from anecdote. A structured VoC trend report grounds Iterate priorities in real data, validates (or challenges) the benefits story, and calibrates Product Marketing claims.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Ticket-volume trends are reported by category and release.
- [ ] Top drivers of new tickets are named with supporting numbers.
- [ ] Deflection rate and KB effectiveness are measured.
- [ ] Severity distribution and escalation rate are included.
- [ ] Thematic analysis of verbatims ends in recommendations with named owners.

## What it contains

- Ticket volume trends by category and release
- Top drivers of new tickets
- Deflection rate and KB effectiveness
- Severity distribution and escalation rate
- Thematic analysis of verbatims and NPS comments
- Recommendations with owners

## Inputs you rely on

- Tagged support-ticket dataset
- Customer resolutions and escalated tickets
- NPS and customer feedback
- Release notes (correlate releases to volume shifts)
- Health scores & at-risk list

## Who consumes it

- Product Owner — iteration priorities and next-cycle opportunity backlog
- Technical Writer — KB article gaps
- Customer Support leadership — staffing and training priorities
- Sales & Marketing — messaging and objection refinement

## Common pitfalls

- Analysis of volume without impact; loudest ≠ most valuable
- Ignoring the low-volume/high-severity long tail
- Tagging inconsistency makes trends noisy
- Report without recommendations; data for data's sake
