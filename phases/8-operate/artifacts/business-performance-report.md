# business performance report

_Produced by: Business-metric reporting cadence_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The recurring BA-authored rollup of business KPIs — adoption, activation, retention, revenue, and support metrics — measured against targets with commentary on drivers and anomalies. The analytical narrative that sits under Finance and Sponsor dashboards.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Your job is making the data make sense to decision-makers. A disciplined performance report turns numbers into causal stories, surfaces anomalies before they become crises, and ties metrics back to the business case commitments you'll audit in Iterate.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every KPI on the scorecard has an actual vs. target value.
- [ ] Trend analysis compares the current period to at least one prior period.
- [ ] Results are broken down by segment or cohort, not just headline aggregate.
- [ ] Anomalies are surfaced with a suspected driver.
- [ ] Data-quality caveats and open questions for leadership are stated explicitly.

## What it contains

- Scorecard of KPIs vs. target
- Period-over-period trend analysis
- Segment and cohort breakdowns
- Anomalies with suspected drivers
- Data-quality notes and caveats
- Open questions requiring leadership input

## Inputs you rely on

- Connected KPI data pipeline and dashboards
- Revenue report and renewal & expansion bookings
- Activated customers and adoption data
- Support/VoC trend analysis
- Approved business case for target comparison

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — input to operational performance review
- Product Owner — iteration priorities
- Finance and RevOps — cross-check commercial reports
- Iterate phase — inputs to profitability assessment

## Common pitfalls

- Dashboard dump without narrative; exec readers stop reading
- Trends without segmentation hide the real story
- Anomalies described but not investigated
- Data caveats omitted; single bad metric derails the whole report
