# renewal & expansion pipeline

_Produced by: QBRs / customer check-ins_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Customer Support

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when there is no commercial motion

## What this is

The forward-looking commercial pipeline of renewal and expansion opportunities across the customer base — whose contract is up when, which accounts are expansion candidates, and the work committed to close each one.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support / Success team

CS earns its cost center or revenue center distinction here. A well-maintained pipeline lets you forecast net retention, stage interventions, and prove value to leadership; an ad-hoc one turns renewals into last-minute fire drills.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every account lists renewal date and amount.
- [ ] Expansion opportunities have estimated ARR and a confidence weighting.
- [ ] QBR cadence and next QBR date are recorded per account.
- [ ] Executive sponsors on both sides are named.
- [ ] Blockers, product asks, and a single owner are captured per opportunity.

## What it contains

- Renewal dates and amounts by account
- Expansion opportunities with estimated ARR and confidence
- QBR cadence and next QBR date
- Executive sponsors on both sides
- Blockers and required product asks
- Owner for each opportunity

## Inputs you rely on

- Health scores & at-risk list
- Activated customers and adoption data
- Contract and renewal data from CRM
- Product usage telemetry
- Customer feedback and NPS

## Who consumes it

- Sales & Marketing — expansion bookings
- Sponsor — net retention forecast
- Business Analyst — retention cohort analysis
- Product Owner — product asks that unlock revenue

## Common pitfalls

- Pipeline entries without confidence weighting — false forecast
- Missing executive sponsor capture — deals stall at renewal
- Expansion asks to Product with no business case attached
- Renewals "assumed" until the month before; churn surprises leadership
