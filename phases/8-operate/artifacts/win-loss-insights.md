# win-loss insights

_Produced by: Win/loss analysis_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Sales & Marketing

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

Structured analysis of recent deals won and lost — why customers chose you or didn't, which competitors showed up, which features mattered, which objections killed deals. Sourced from interviews and closed-opportunity data.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

Pipeline is only as good as conversion. Win/loss turns the noisy signal of individual deals into actionable inputs for messaging, competitive positioning, and roadmap asks. It is one of the cheapest, highest-leverage insight engines the team has.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Win/loss rate trend is reported by segment and deal size.
- [ ] Top reasons to win and to lose are ranked with supporting evidence.
- [ ] Competitor presence and win rate against each is captured.
- [ ] Product gaps cited in losses are listed with frequency.
- [ ] Verbatim customer quotes are included, not just narrative summaries.

## What it contains

- Win/loss rate trend by segment and size
- Top reasons to win and reasons to lose, ranked
- Competitor presence and win rate against each
- Deal cycle length and pricing sensitivity
- Product gaps cited in losses
- Verbatim quotes from interviews

## Inputs you rely on

- CRM closed-opportunity data
- Win/loss interview program (internal or third-party)
- Competitive landscape report and battlecards
- Customer support / feedback data
- Pricing & packaging plan

## Who consumes it

- Sales & Marketing — refresh messaging and battlecards
- Product Owner — feeds Iterate next-cycle opportunity backlog
- Sponsor — portfolio positioning decisions
- Enablement — updated objection handlers

## Common pitfalls

- Only interviewing winners; loser signal is more valuable
- Sales-narrative bias on reason codes; no customer voice
- Insights published but never turn into messaging or product changes
- Competitor intel stale within a quarter if not refreshed
