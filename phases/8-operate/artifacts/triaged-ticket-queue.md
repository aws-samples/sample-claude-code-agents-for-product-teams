## What this is

The operational support queue — incoming tickets categorized, prioritized, and routed to the right tier or specialist. The living artifact that Support manages in real time against SLAs.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support team

Triage quality determines SLA attainment, CSAT, and cost-to-serve. Good triage moves easy tickets to self-serve or tier-1 quickly and routes complex ones to the right expert the first time — avoiding the dreaded ticket-bouncing that kills customer patience.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every ticket shows severity, priority, and SLA clock.
- [ ] Categorisation tags are applied against the canonical taxonomy.
- [ ] Each ticket is assigned to a tier or agent.
- [ ] Aging and escalation flags surface breaches before they age out.
- [ ] Known-issue matches and workarounds applied are linked per ticket.

## What it contains

- Live queue with severity, priority, and SLA clock
- Categorization tags per ticket
- Assignment to tier/agent
- Aging and escalation flags
- Known-issue matches and workarounds applied
- Volume and trend dashboards

## Inputs you rely on

- Support process model and live support processes
- Known-issues list and customer-facing knowledge base
- Customer health scores
- Product release notes (anticipate release-driven tickets)
- Trained support team and macros

## Who consumes it

- Support agents — work the queue
- Support leadership — SLA and staffing decisions
- Business Analyst — support/VoC trend analysis
- Product Owner — volume and category trends feed Iterate

## Common pitfalls

- Tag taxonomy allowed to sprawl — analytics unusable
- Severity assigned by customer, not by impact — distorts priorities
- Escalations disappear into engineering without tracking
- Aging tickets hidden under the fold; SLA breaches surprise leadership
