# audit report / attestation

_Produced by: Periodic compliance audit_

**Business outcome supported:** run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.

**Primary owner:** Security & Compliance

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when no formal audit applies

## What this is

The formal audit report or third-party attestation (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, PCI, HIPAA, FedRAMP) covering the product — scope, controls tested, exceptions found, management response, and any follow-up commitments.

## Why it matters for the Security & Compliance team

Attestations unlock market segments and shorten enterprise sales cycles; exceptions drag them on. Your goal is a clean opinion (or controlled, time-boxed exceptions with remediation paths) that sales can share without qualification and that Legal/Privacy can lean on in contract negotiations.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Scope (systems, services, locations, time period) is explicitly stated.
- [ ] Controls tested and the testing methodology are documented.
- [ ] Every exception has a management response and a dated remediation commitment.
- [ ] The auditor's opinion is clearly recorded.
- [ ] Period-over-period trend is shown against the prior attestation.

## What it contains

- Scope (systems, services, locations, time period)
- Controls tested and methodology
- Exceptions and management responses
- Remediation commitments with deadlines
- Auditor opinion
- Period-over-period trend

## Inputs you rely on

- Audit evidence pool
- Access review results
- Security monitoring and incident postmortems
- Patched dependencies and DR test results
- Applicable-regulations list

## Who consumes it

- Enterprise customers under NDA — vendor due diligence
- Sales and deal teams — security questionnaire responses
- Board and Sponsor — risk posture
- Next audit cycle — baseline

## Common pitfalls

- Exceptions without remediation dates become chronic
- Scope quietly shrinks between periods, hiding regressions
- Sales overclaims the certification's scope to prospects
- Report published but corrective actions not tracked to closure
