# Architect in Operate

Operate is where production teaches you what design could only hypothesize. You monitor architectural health, run capacity and scale reviews, and evolve the architecture from real operating learning. Your contributions to unit economics and root cause keep fit-for-enterprise real, not aspirational.

## What you do

- Monitor architectural health — structural metrics, coupling, drift from approved patterns.
- Review capacity and scale readiness so growth doesn't surprise the platform.
- Evolve the architecture from production learnings — what designs are paying off, which need rework.
- Contribute to root-cause analysis on significant incidents with SRE and Developer.
- Contribute to unit-economics review with the Sponsor so architectural cost drivers are visible.

## Artifacts you own

- [architectural health report](artifacts/architectural-health-report.md) — the recurring architectural-health readout.
- [scale-readiness report](artifacts/scale-readiness-report.md) — the capacity and scale architecture review.
- [architecture updates](artifacts/architecture-updates.md) — architectural evolution driven by production learning.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [margin & CAC/LTV report](artifacts/margin-cac-ltv-report.md) — Product Sponsor owns; you contribute cost-to-serve architecture input.
- [root cause analysis report](artifacts/root-cause-analysis-report.md) — SRE owns; you contribute architectural root-cause analysis.
- [cost & capacity report](artifacts/cost-capacity-report.md) — SRE owns; you contribute architectural cost/capacity input.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on unit economics and architectural cost drivers.
- **Site Reliability Engineer** — bi-directional on reliability, capacity, and root cause.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on architectural evolution and conformance.
- **Business Analyst** — bi-directional on cost-driver analysis for margin work.

## Handoff into [Iterate](../9-iterate/README.md)

You exit Operate with production-grounded architectural evidence. Carry that into Iterate's architecture roadmap refresh, modernization plan, tech-debt prioritization, and benefits-shortfall root-cause — the architecture evolves best when next-cycle investment is informed by what production actually taught you.
