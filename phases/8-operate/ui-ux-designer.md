# UI/UX Designer in Operate

Operate is a listening phase for you. You're not the lead on any Operate-phase artifact, but real-customer behavior is exactly the signal you need for the next design cycle — so you ride along with Customer Support, Business Analyst, and Product Owner, mining live usage for the usability evidence Iterate will act on.

## What you do

- Observe real-user behavior in production — heatmaps, session replays, drop-off points.
- Partner with Customer Support to understand where users get stuck and why.
- Stand by for urgent usability or accessibility fixes if signals warrant.
- Build the continuous-discovery muscle that feeds Iterate.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Customer Support** — bi-directional on usability friction surfaced in tickets.
- **Business Analyst** — bi-directional on VoC and adoption signal.
- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on prioritization of UX-driven issues.
- **Technical Writer** — bi-directional on journey gaps surfaced in docs analytics.

## Handoff into [Iterate](../9-iterate/README.md)

You exit Operate with a rich stream of real-user behavioral data. Carry that into Iterate's continuous discovery feed and feedback insights work — the ongoing insights feed you co-own with Business Analyst is where usability evidence translates into the next discover cycle.
