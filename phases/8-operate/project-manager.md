# Project Manager in Operate

Operate is where hypercare gives way to steady state. You own the transition, set the operating cadence, and make sure the coordination muscle that carried Launch doesn't evaporate once the intensity drops. Your job is to protect what was launched with predictable rhythms.

## What you do

- Drive the hypercare-to-steady-state transition so operational ownership is explicit.
- Set up the operating cadence — regular reviews, escalation paths, decision forums.
- Keep dependencies visible and escalation thresholds honest through the steady state.
- Maintain the cross-functional coordination spine that keeps Operate predictable.

## Artifacts you own

- [operating review meetings](artifacts/operating-review-meetings.md) — the recurring operating-cadence forums.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

- [steady-state operating handoff](outcomes/steady-state-operating-handoff.md) — the explicit transition from hypercare to steady state.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on operating cadence and executive reviews.
- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on business review coordination.
- **Release Manager** — bi-directional on handing release ownership to steady-state operations.
- **Site Reliability Engineer** — bi-directional on incident governance and operational health.

## Handoff into [Iterate](../9-iterate/README.md)

You exit Operate with a steady operating rhythm and clean ownership boundaries. Carry that into Iterate's lessons-learned facilitation, project closure, retrospective, and benefits-review coordination — closure discipline is how organizations actually learn, and Iterate is where that becomes institutional.
