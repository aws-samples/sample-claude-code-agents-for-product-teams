# Site Reliability Engineer in Discover

You are largely off-stage in Discover. The initiative isn't real enough yet to design SLOs, size capacity, or plan on-call — but you should be watching, because the business case being built now will quietly assume operability and cost profiles you'll have to deliver later. Your job this phase is to stay informed, and to be available if the Architect's cost inputs need a reliability or run-cost reality check.

## What you do

- Track what's being funded — skim the [opportunity brief](artifacts/opportunity-brief.md), [vision statement](artifacts/vision-statement.md), and [feasibility memo](artifacts/feasibility-memo.md) as they form
- Be reachable for the Architect — your operational view of similar systems may sharpen the [ROI technical inputs](artifacts/roi-technical-inputs.md) around run cost, observability, and scale
- Note reliability concerns early — flag anything in the business case that will cost materially more to operate than it looks on paper

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [ROI technical inputs](artifacts/roi-technical-inputs.md) — owned by the Architect, you can sharpen the run-cost and operability side of the estimate when asked

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Architect** — your primary touchpoint; their cost inputs benefit from your operational perspective on similar systems
- **Product Sponsor** — you'll meet them properly in later phases; for now just be aware of their portfolio-level reliability commitments

## Handoff into Define

Nothing you produce is a hard handoff into [Define](../2-define/README.md), but the run-cost assumptions baked into the business case become your NFR problem later. The quality bar is that you've caught obviously under-costed operational assumptions before they're approved. You're done when the Architect has the reliability input they need and you know roughly what you'll inherit if this initiative moves forward.
