# Product Sponsor in Discover

You are here to decide whether this opportunity deserves to exist on your portfolio. Discover is the cheapest moment to kill a bad bet or sharpen a good one, and your job is to force that honest conversation — align the initiative to corporate strategy, put real money behind it, and own the go/no-go that ends the phase. Nothing else in the lifecycle happens without your signature here.

## What you do

- Align to portfolio strategy — co-author the [strategic alignment statement](artifacts/strategic-alignment-statement.md) with the Architect so the initiative ladders up to a corporate bet
- Secure initiative funding — commit budget and staffing air-cover, producing the [approved budget & investment commitment](outcomes/approved-budget-investment-commitment.md)
- Run the Discover stage-gate review — issue the [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md) with recorded rationale
- Pressure-test the [business case](artifacts/business-case.md) and [feasibility memo](artifacts/feasibility-memo.md) with the Product Owner — the evidence has to convince governance, not just the team
- Validate the [stakeholder map](artifacts/stakeholder-map.md) and use your air-cover to unlock the executives the PO cannot reach

## Artifacts you own

- [strategic alignment statement](artifacts/strategic-alignment-statement.md) — the narrative tying this initiative to corporate strategy and portfolio priorities

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [business case](artifacts/business-case.md) — owned by the Product Owner, you co-author the financial/strategic justification and defend it in governance
- [stakeholder map](artifacts/stakeholder-map.md) — owned by the Product Owner, you name the executives and political realities
- [feasibility memo](artifacts/feasibility-memo.md) — owned by the Product Owner, you weigh in on risk appetite and portfolio fit

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved budget & investment commitment](outcomes/approved-budget-investment-commitment.md) — the money and headcount required to enter Define
- [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md) — go, no-go, or defer, with conditions

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — you need a crisp business case, vision, and hypothesis; they need your strategic framing, funding, and stakeholder access
- **Architect** — you need a candid read on feasibility and cost; they need to know how much strategic cover they have for bold technical bets
- **Project Manager** — they coordinate leadership alignment and pre-kickoff logistics; you need a clean path to the stage-gate
- **Business Analyst** — their ROI model and market analysis feed your business case; you need to trust their numbers

## Handoff into Define

Your exit decision is the single gate between Discover and [Define](../2-define/README.md). Define cannot start until funding is committed, leadership is aligned, and the Discover artifacts hang together as a defensible bet. The quality bar is governance-ready: if a board member asked for the one-pager tomorrow, you could send it without flinching. You're done when the PO can kick off Define with certainty about scope of ambition, not just permission to proceed.
