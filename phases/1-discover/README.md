# Phase 1: Discover

**Goal:** frame the opportunity, validate the problem, understand the market, and exit with a funded, strategically-aligned bet.

## Where this fits

Discover is the fuzzy front end of the lifecycle. Every later phase — [Define](../2-define/README.md), [Design](../3-design/README.md), and onward — assumes the problem is real, the customer is known, the market is understood, and the investment is funded. Get this phase wrong and the whole cycle produces something elegant that nobody asked for. Get it right and the team runs with clarity for months.

This is the **first phase**. It has no upstream predecessor on a new initiative, though insights from a prior cycle's [Iterate](../9-iterate/README.md) feed directly in as [next-cycle opportunity backlog](../9-iterate/artifacts/next-cycle-opportunity-backlog.md).

## What happens in this phase

Discover is led by the **Product Owner** framing the opportunity and vision, the **Business Analyst** building the market, problem-validation, and ROI evidence, and the **Product Sponsor** closing with a funded go/no-go decision. Around them, **Sales & Marketing** supplies competitive and pricing intelligence, **UI/UX** produces early personas, the **Architect** provides technical feasibility and cost inputs, **Security & Compliance** raises early regulatory and data-sensitivity flags, and the **Project Manager** coordinates the leadership team into alignment.

The phase ends at the [Discover stage-gate review](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md). Only opportunities that survive this gate move to Define.

## Deliverables

- **[`artifacts/`](artifacts/)** — 18 tangible outputs: opportunity brief, vision statement, business case, market analysis, validated problem statement, personas, competitive landscape, pricing benchmark, feasibility memo, ROI model, stakeholder map, and more.
- **[`outcomes/`](outcomes/)** — 3 decisions/state changes: [aligned leadership team](outcomes/aligned-leadership-team.md), [approved budget & investment commitment](outcomes/approved-budget-investment-commitment.md), [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md).

## Exit checklist

Ready to enter [Define](../2-define/README.md) when all of the following are true:

- [ ] [business case](artifacts/business-case.md) — NPV, payback, and at least one sensitivity range are computed.
- [ ] [competitive landscape report](artifacts/competitive-landscape-report.md) — At least one white-space opportunity is identified with defensibility rationale.
- [ ] [data-sensitivity flag](artifacts/data-sensitivity-flag.md) — Preliminary classification tier is assigned against the org's scheme.
- [ ] [feasibility memo](artifacts/feasibility-memo.md) — Each feasibility dimension carries a red/amber/green rating with supporting evidence.
- [ ] [initial project brief](artifacts/initial-project-brief.md) — Top 3-5 risks each have a named owner.
- [ ] [market analysis report](artifacts/market-analysis-report.md) — TAM/SAM/SOM figures include methodology and sources, not just totals.
- [ ] [opportunity brief](artifacts/opportunity-brief.md) — One-sentence statement identifies customer, pain, and desired outcome.
- [ ] [opportunity sizing](artifacts/opportunity-sizing.md) — Best, expected, and worst case sizings are each shown with their drivers.
- [ ] [outcome hypothesis](artifacts/outcome-hypothesis.md) — Hypothesis defines a measurable outcome and success threshold.
- [ ] [pricing benchmark report](artifacts/pricing-benchmark-report.md) — Report ends with a recommended pricing hypothesis expressed as a range.
- [ ] [regulatory risk flag](artifacts/regulatory-risk-flag.md) — Applicable regimes each carry a confidence rating (definitely, likely, possible).
- [ ] [ROI model](artifacts/roi-model.md) — NPV, IRR, and payback are computed under expected, best, and worst cases.
- [ ] [ROI technical inputs](artifacts/roi-technical-inputs.md) — Technical risks that could move the number by >30% are listed.
- [ ] [stakeholder map](artifacts/stakeholder-map.md) — Each stakeholder carries a current stance with evidence.
- [ ] [strategic alignment statement](artifacts/strategic-alignment-statement.md) — Statement links explicitly to specific corporate strategy pillars or OKRs.
- [ ] [user personas](artifacts/user-personas.md) — Document contains 3-5 primary personas with jobs-to-be-done per persona.
- [ ] [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md) — Evidence base is quantified (interview count, survey n, data sources).
- [ ] [vision statement](artifacts/vision-statement.md) — At least one explicit anti-vision (what the product will not do) is stated.
- [ ] [aligned leadership team](outcomes/aligned-leadership-team.md) — Shared problem framing and success definition are recorded.
- [ ] [approved budget & investment commitment](outcomes/approved-budget-investment-commitment.md) — Approved budget ceiling and funding horizon are recorded.
- [ ] [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md) — Go/no-go/defer decision is recorded with named rationale.
- [ ] All role-owned artifacts for this phase meet their artifact-doc DoD
