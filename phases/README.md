# Phases — AI-Powered Product Lifecycle

Each phase has a role-aware README that introduces what you do, what you produce, and who you work with. Start at your current phase, find your role, and follow the links into `artifacts/` and `outcomes/` for details.

- **[1. Discover](1-discover/README.md)** — frame the opportunity, validate the problem, understand the market, and exit with a funded, strategically-aligned bet.
- **[2. Define](2-define/README.md)** — turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.
- **[3. Design](3-design/README.md)** — produce a build-ready design package — UX, architecture, data model, APIs, threat model, and integration map — with engineering signed off to build.
- **[4. Plan](4-plan/README.md)** — stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.
- **[5. Build](5-build/README.md)** — produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.
- **[6. Verify](6-verify/README.md)** — evidence-based readiness to launch — functional, non-functional, operational, and documentation — validated in production-like conditions.
- **[7. Launch](7-launch/README.md)** — product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.
- **[8. Operate](8-operate/README.md)** — run a stable, SLO-meeting service while growing a profitable, retained customer base — the commercial engine of the product.
- **[9. Iterate](9-iterate/README.md)** — close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.
