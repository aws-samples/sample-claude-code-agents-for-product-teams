# UI/UX Designer in Define

You are quieter in Define than in Discover or Design — the team is working on requirements and scope, not pixels yet. But the requirements being approved now will dictate the journeys you design next, so your job is to keep the user frame present while the room gets technical. Make sure the approved requirements serve the personas you validated, not a parallel universe of assumed users.

## What you do

- Stay in the requirements reviews — sanity-check that the [requirements document](artifacts/requirements-document.md) and [testable acceptance criteria](artifacts/testable-acceptance-criteria.md) reflect your personas and validated problem
- Contribute to the [process models](artifacts/process-models.md) — flag where to-be flows will demand new UX patterns
- Feed [MVP scope statement](artifacts/mvp-scope-statement.md) conversations with UX risk signals — what can't be shipped without prototyping
- Begin scoping the journeys and IA work you'll own in Design

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [requirements document](artifacts/requirements-document.md) — owned by the Business Analyst, you make sure the functional requirements don't silently assume un-designed interactions
- [process models](artifacts/process-models.md) — owned by the Business Analyst, you contribute the user-facing flow where it's a UX problem rather than a process one

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the BA's [approved requirements](outcomes/approved-requirements.md) and the Sponsor's [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md) through UX risk visibility.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — they're drafting requirements that describe user behavior; you keep those grounded in persona reality
- **Product Owner** — consumes your UX risk reads into MVP scope decisions
- **Customer Support** — their support process model and your journeys will need to be consistent later

## Handoff into Design

Define ends and you become the busiest role in [Design](../3-design/README.md) — journey maps, IA, specs, mockups, prototypes, usability testing, accessibility review. The quality bar in Define is that when you start Design work, the requirements you're designing for feel like the personas you validated. You're done when you can sketch the journeys for the approved MVP without finding gaps in the requirements.
