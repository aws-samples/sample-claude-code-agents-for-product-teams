# draft GTM plan

_Produced by: GTM planning — initial_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Sales & Marketing

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP is a private beta or internal release

## What this is

The first pass at go-to-market strategy — positioning, target segments, channels, pricing posture, launch sequence, and sales-readiness plan. It is a draft so product-marketing decisions can shape (and be shaped by) Design, not arrive as a fait accompli before launch.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing owner

You produce this because GTM assumptions quietly drive product decisions — who the MVP serves, what must be in the first release, how the product is packaged. Drafting GTM in Define gives Product Owner and Architect a chance to align with commercial reality before expensive choices are locked in.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Draft includes a positioning statement and primary messaging pillars
- [ ] Target segments are named with sequencing (early adopters first)
- [ ] Channel strategy specifies direct / partner / self-serve / hybrid
- [ ] Pricing hypothesis and packaging approach are stated
- [ ] Assumptions and items to validate before final GTM are listed

## What it contains

- Positioning statement and primary messaging pillars
- Target segments and ICP with sequencing (early adopters first)
- Channel strategy (direct, partner, self-serve, hybrid)
- Pricing hypothesis and packaging approach
- Launch type (stealth, beta, GA) and cadence
- Sales enablement needs and certification plan
- Competitive response posture
- Assumptions and things to validate before final GTM

## Who consumes it

- Product Owner — shapes MVP and packaging choices around GTM
- Product Sponsor — tests commercial viability of the chosen sequence
- Customer Support — previews launch volume and training needs
- Sales & Marketing (future you) — evolves into launch [marketing collateral](../../../7-launch/artifacts/marketing-collateral.md) and [pricing & packaging plan](../../../7-launch/artifacts/pricing-packaging-plan.md)

## Common pitfalls

- Messaging written before talking to a single customer
- One target segment when early adopters and mainstream need different stories
- Pricing hypothesis incompatible with the packaging the product can actually ship
- No validation plan, so the draft becomes the committed plan by default
