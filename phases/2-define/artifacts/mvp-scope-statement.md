# MVP scope statement

_Produced by: MVP / scope decision_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Product Owner

**Stakeholders:** Product Sponsor

**Accelerated MVP:** required — this is the spec the accelerated path delivers against

## What this is

A written articulation of the minimum viable scope — the smallest coherent slice that can test the outcome hypothesis with real users. It is the line between "first release" and "nice-to-have," and the stake in the ground for scope discipline through Build.

## Why it matters for the Product Owner

You write this so every scope-expansion conversation has to argue against a concrete baseline rather than a vibe. Without an MVP statement, every stakeholder's favorite feature becomes "core," and the initiative's first launch slides by quarters.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Statement names the outcome hypothesis the MVP will test
- [ ] Statement identifies the primary persona and the one or two journeys that must work
- [ ] Included capabilities are distinguished from explicit exclusions with rationale
- [ ] Quality bar specifies performance, reliability, and accessibility thresholds
- [ ] Success criteria define the keep-building / pivot / kill decision

## What it contains

- The outcome hypothesis the MVP will test
- The primary persona and the one or two journeys that must work
- Included capabilities with just-enough depth
- Explicit exclusions with rationale and "next after MVP" landing spot
- Quality bar: performance, reliability, accessibility thresholds for the MVP
- Success criteria that decide "keep building / pivot / kill"

## Inputs you rely on

- [Signed product charter](signed-product-charter.md) and [outcome hypothesis](../../1-discover/artifacts/outcome-hypothesis.md)
- [User personas](../../1-discover/artifacts/user-personas.md) and [validated problem statement](../../1-discover/artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md)

## Who consumes it

- Business Analyst — authors requirements inside this scope, not beyond it
- Architect and Developer — plan to build only what the MVP demands
- QA/Tester — focuses risk-based coverage on the MVP journeys
- Sales & Marketing — sequences launch and enablement around MVP capabilities

## Common pitfalls

- Minimum-marketable-product masquerading as MVP — too big to test, too small to sell
- No exclusions, so everything drifts back in through acceptance criteria
- Quality bar set too low ("it's just an MVP") and launch becomes a trust destroyer
- No kill/pivot criteria, so even a failed MVP keeps getting funded
