# affected-audiences list

_Produced by: Identify affected internal audiences_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP's audience is a defined design-partner cohort

## What this is

The list of internal audiences whose work will be affected by the initiative — sales, support, finance, legal, operations, field teams — with the nature of the impact and the first-draft communication posture. It is the seed for the Plan-phase communication plan.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

You produce this so "who do we need to tell" does not happen the week before launch. Each affected audience has a ramp-up cost; naming them early lets you sequence enablement and avoid the "nobody told us" complaint cycle.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each audience entry includes name, function, and approximate size
- [ ] Every audience has a nature-of-impact label (tool / process / comp / policy change)
- [ ] Each audience carries a sentiment-risk rating (welcome / neutral / resist)
- [ ] Each audience has a first-draft cadence (inform / consult / involve / partner)
- [ ] Each audience names a specific liaison or sponsor, not a team placeholder

## What it contains

- Audience name, function, and approximate size
- Nature of impact (tool change, process change, comp change, policy change)
- Sentiment risk (welcome / neutral / resist)
- Required knowledge transfer and training needs
- First-draft cadence (inform / consult / involve / partner)
- Named liaison or sponsor in that audience

## Who consumes it

- Project Manager (future you) — develops the [stakeholder comms plan](../../4-plan/artifacts/stakeholder-comms-plan.md)
- Business Analyst — sizes change-management effort for the business case
- Sales & Marketing, Customer Support — plan internal enablement
- Product Sponsor — sees which audiences need air-cover

## Common pitfalls

- Missing adjacent teams (Finance ops, Legal, Procurement) that only show up at launch
- No sentiment column, so resistance is a surprise
- Treating "inform" as sufficient for audiences that need "involve"
- No refresh — audiences shift as scope evolves
