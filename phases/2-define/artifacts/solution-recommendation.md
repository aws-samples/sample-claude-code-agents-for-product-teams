# solution recommendation

_Produced by: Solution options evaluation_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** Architect

**Accelerated MVP:** slim — a one-paragraph "we chose X because Y" is enough

## What this is

A reasoned recommendation of one option from a set of viable solution approaches — build, buy, partner, extend existing, or do nothing — with the evidence and trade-offs that led there. It is a decision-support artifact: the Sponsor signs, the team builds.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

You author this — with Architect — so the org commits to the chosen approach with eyes open, not by default. A well-reasoned recommendation shortens governance conversations and makes later "why did we pick this?" questions answerable.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] At least three options (including "do nothing") are evaluated with elimination rationale for rejects
- [ ] Evaluation criteria and weights are stated
- [ ] Each option has evidence-backed scores against the criteria
- [ ] Total cost of ownership is estimated per viable option
- [ ] Recommendation states the reasoning, a dissenting view, and attached conditions or risks

## What it contains

- Options evaluated and the elimination rationale for rejected ones
- Evaluation criteria and weights (cost, time-to-value, fit, risk, strategic control)
- Scored comparison with evidence for each score
- Total cost of ownership estimate per viable option
- Recommended option with the reasoning and the dissenting view
- Conditions and risks attached to the recommendation

## Inputs you rely on

- [Capability gap report](capability-gap-report.md) and [technical feasibility findings](technical-feasibility-findings.md)
- [NFR document](nfr-document.md) — options that cannot meet NFRs are out
- [ROI technical inputs](../../1-discover/artifacts/roi-technical-inputs.md) and vendor indicative pricing
- [Commercial model decision](../outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md) framing

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — ratifies the [build-vs-buy decision](../../3-design/outcomes/build-vs-buy-decision.md) in Design
- Architect — refines the chosen approach through [technical options assessment](../../3-design/artifacts/technical-options-assessment.md)
- Project Manager — sequences dependencies around the chosen option
- Finance — uses TCO view for budget and depreciation treatment

## Common pitfalls

- Decision-theater — criteria and weights chosen after the answer is known
- Missing the "do nothing" option as a baseline
- Scoring without evidence so scores are opinions dressed up as data
- No dissenting view captured; the risk of group-think is unrecorded
