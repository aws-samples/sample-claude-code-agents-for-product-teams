# charter draft

_Produced by: Charter development facilitation_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The pre-approval working draft of the product charter — content substantially complete, stakeholders consulted, Sponsor walk-through staged. It becomes the [signed product charter](signed-product-charter.md) once it lands the Sponsor's signature.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

You produce this because charter signing is an event, not a surprise. Walking the draft through stakeholders beforehand surfaces objections in a form that can be resolved; arriving at a signing meeting with unseen content guarantees delay.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Draft names the initiative, sponsor, owner, and core team
- [ ] Scope statement includes explicit non-goals
- [ ] Success metrics and business outcome are stated
- [ ] Budget and schedule envelope are included
- [ ] Pre-sign stakeholder consultation log identifies who was walked through the draft

## What it contains

- Draft initiative name, sponsor, owner, and team
- Scope statement with explicit non-goals
- Success metrics and business outcome
- Budget and schedule envelope as currently understood
- Governance cadence proposal and decision rights
- Risks, assumptions, and open questions
- Pre-sign stakeholder consultation log

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — reviews and signs (or sends back with edits)
- Product Owner — co-signs and confirms ownership
- Architect and Security & Compliance — sanity-check scope and risk statements
- Project Manager (future you) — maintains the charter as the authoritative reference

## Common pitfalls

- "Charter by committee" — content softened to the point of meaninglessness
- Missing a pre-sign conversation with a key skeptic — signing becomes re-litigation
- Schedule and budget in the draft that the team has not actually committed to
- Draft circulated without a clear cutoff date and signing moment
