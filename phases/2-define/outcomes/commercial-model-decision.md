# commercial model decision

_Produced by: Commercial / delivery model decision_

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Product Owner, Architect

## What this is

The Sponsor, PO, and Architect have decided how the product will be sold and delivered: SaaS vs. on-prem vs. hybrid, usage-based vs. subscription, direct vs. partner channel.

## Why it matters

Commercial model shapes nearly every downstream decision — architecture, pricing, legal, sales, support. Deferring this decision past Define forces expensive re-architecture.

## What it contains

- Deployment model (SaaS, self-hosted, hybrid)
- Pricing model family (per-seat, per-unit, tiered, outcome-based)
- Channel strategy (direct, partner, marketplace)
- Commercial assumptions feeding the business case

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Deployment model is selected (SaaS / self-hosted / hybrid)
- [ ] Pricing model family is selected (per-seat / per-unit / tiered / outcome-based)
- [ ] Channel strategy is named (direct / partner / marketplace)
- [ ] Commercial assumptions feeding the business case are documented
- [ ] Sponsor, Product Owner, and Architect are recorded as aligned on the decision

## Entry criteria

- Pricing benchmark and competitive landscape reviewed
- Architect can articulate technical implications of each option

## Exit signal

Architecture, pricing, GTM, and legal all work against a single commercial target.
