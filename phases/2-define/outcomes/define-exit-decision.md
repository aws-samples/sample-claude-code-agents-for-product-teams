# Define exit decision

_Produced by: Define stage-gate review_

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Architect

**Accelerated MVP:** slim — an explicit "ready to build" is enough

## What this is

The Define stage-gate. Sponsor and Architect decide whether the initiative is ready to move into detailed design and planning, or whether more discovery or refinement is needed.

## Why it matters

The gate prevents rushing into Design with unclear scope, unratified economics, or unresolved regulatory questions — all of which are expensive to fix later.

## What it contains

- Go / no-go / defer with rationale
- Any pre-conditions for Design to begin
- Updated risk and dependency view

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Decision is recorded as go / no-go / defer with rationale
- [ ] Any pre-conditions for Design to begin are listed
- [ ] Risk and dependency view is refreshed as of the gate date
- [ ] Sponsor and Architect are recorded as decision participants

## Entry criteria

- All Define artifacts complete
- Cross-role requirements sign-off in hand
- Business case ratified and commercial model decided

## Exit signal

Design work is authorized with scope, budget, and constraints understood.
