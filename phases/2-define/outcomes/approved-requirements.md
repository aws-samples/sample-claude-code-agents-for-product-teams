# approved requirements

_Produced by: Review & iterate on requirements_

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Accelerated MVP:** slim — PO acceptance of the backlog is enough

## What this is

The requirements draft has been iterated with stakeholders and formally approved. It is now the authoritative description of what the product must do.

## Why it matters

Approved requirements are the baseline every later activity — design, acceptance criteria, testing, UAT — traces back to. Ambiguity here becomes defects later.

## What it contains

- Functional requirements with acceptance criteria
- Non-functional requirements linked from the NFR document
- Explicit out-of-scope list
- Change-control procedure for modifying requirements

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every functional requirement has at least one linked acceptance criterion
- [ ] NFRs are linked back to the NFR document and covered
- [ ] An explicit out-of-scope list is published alongside the approved set
- [ ] A change-control procedure specifies how requirements may be modified after approval
- [ ] Approval sign-off is recorded against a specific version of the document

## Entry criteria

- Requirements draft exists
- Stakeholders have reviewed and raised concerns
- BA has resolved or documented each concern

## Exit signal

Design can begin with confidence about what it must satisfy.
