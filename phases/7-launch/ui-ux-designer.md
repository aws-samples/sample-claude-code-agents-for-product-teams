# UI/UX Designer in Launch

Launch is a quiet phase for you by design — the UX work was done upstream, and your role now is to watch what real users do with what you shipped. You stay available for rapid visual or copy fixes during hypercare and start building the observation muscle you'll need in Operate and Iterate.

## What you do

- Observe real-user behavior against the journeys you designed and flag anything that diverges meaningfully.
- Stay on call for urgent visual, copy, or accessibility fixes that surface during hypercare.
- Partner with Technical Writer and Sales & Marketing on in-product copy and launch-day UI polish.
- Start collecting qualitative signal that will inform continuous discovery in Iterate.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [feature documentation](artifacts/feature-documentation.md) — Technical Writer owns; you validate UX alignment of screenshots and flows.
- [user guides](artifacts/user-guides.md) — Technical Writer owns; you validate walkthroughs match the shipped experience.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Technical Writer** — bi-directional on feature documentation and user guide accuracy.
- **Sales & Marketing** — bi-directional on in-product UI copy and launch collateral visuals.
- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on any launch-week UX trade-offs.
- **Customer Support** — bi-directional on usability friction surfaced in early tickets.

## Handoff into [Operate](../8-operate/README.md)

You exit Launch with early behavioral signal from real users. Carry that forward into Iterate's continuous discovery feed — the first weeks of live traffic are your best chance to spot journey breakpoints, so observe hard and log patterns that will feed the next discovery cycle.
