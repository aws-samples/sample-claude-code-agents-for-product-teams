# launch announcement

_Produced by: Launch event_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Sales & Marketing

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP is private

## What this is

The external launch moment — press release, blog post, keynote, webinar, or event that publicly declares the product is live. The single piece of content the market points to when it says "this launched."

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

You get one launch headline; it sets category positioning, analyst narrative, and pipeline velocity for the quarter. A sharp announcement compounds into coverage and inbound; a muddled one forces months of remediation through paid demand-gen.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Hero message is stated with its supporting proof points listed.
- [ ] Press release includes quotes from customer, partner, and internal spokespeople.
- [ ] Blog post or hub page links out to deeper assets.
- [ ] Launch event plan names the format (live stream, webinar, keynote) and schedule.
- [ ] Every claim carries Legal and Security & Compliance review status.

## What it contains

- Hero message and supporting proof points
- Press release with quotes (customer, partner, internal)
- Blog post or hub page linking to deeper assets
- Launch event plan (live stream, webinar, keynote)
- Social and partner syndication package
- Legal- and Security & Compliance-reviewed claims

## Inputs you rely on

- Pricing & packaging plan (analysts will ask)
- Marketing collateral and demo assets
- Analyst/press coverage coordination and embargoes
- Board/investor launch narrative for message consistency
- Customer quotes from design partners and beta users

## Who consumes it

- Prospective customers — top-of-funnel demand signal
- Analysts and press — quote and amplify
- Partners and community — share in their channels
- Sales — use in outbound campaigns and nurture

## Common pitfalls

- Shipping the announcement before production validation completes
- Claims that legal hasn't cleared or that over-index on unreleased capabilities
- No customer proof — announcement reads like marketing boilerplate
- Launching into a news cycle dominated by bigger stories — poor timing
