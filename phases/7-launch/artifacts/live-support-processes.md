# live support processes

_Produced by: Execute support process cutover_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Customer Support

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when there is no formal support motion

## What this is

The support operation running live for the new product on launch day — staffed queues, routing rules, SLAs, escalation paths, tagging taxonomy, and knowledge-base links. Proof that support is open for business.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support lead

Cost-to-serve and retention start here. A shaky cutover means the first cohort of customers hits a wall and your CSAT and churn metrics start underwater. A clean one converts early-customer friction into resolved tickets and VoC insight instead of escalation incidents.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Routing and priority rules for the new product are configured in the ticketing system.
- [ ] Tagging taxonomy is documented so tickets can be analyzed later.
- [ ] SLA targets are stated by severity and channel.
- [ ] Escalation paths into engineering and the hypercare war room are named.
- [ ] Knowledge base articles and macros are published and linked from day one.

## What it contains

- Routing and priority rules for the new product
- Tagging taxonomy so tickets can be analyzed later
- SLA targets by severity and channel
- Escalation paths into engineering and the hypercare war room
- Shift coverage and on-call rotations during launch
- Knowledge base and macros ready from day one

## Inputs you rely on

- Support process model from Define
- Trained sales & support teams
- Customer-facing knowledge base and user guides
- Known-issues list with workarounds
- IR plan & runbook for security incidents

## Who consumes it

- Support agents — execute triage and resolution
- Customer Success — coordinate on at-risk accounts
- Product Owner and Engineering — consume escalated-issue tickets
- Business Analyst — tagged tickets feed VoC analysis in Operate

## Common pitfalls

- Tagging taxonomy improvised per agent — analytics unusable later
- Hypercare escalation path undefined; everything goes to engineering
- Macros reference docs that aren't published yet
- Cutover with no parallel run or fallback for the legacy product
