# rolled-out process changes

_Produced by: Process change rollout to impacted business teams_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP doesn't change business processes

## What this is

Evidence that the to-be business processes documented in Define have actually been rolled out to the internal teams affected — billing, legal, ops, support, finance. The product changed; the company's operating procedures followed.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Benefits realization rests on the assumption that internal teams actually work the new way. If the product ships but Ops still fulfills the old way, or Finance still books revenue under the old SKU, the business case KPIs never materialize and benefits tracking shows unexplained drift.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each impacted function has an updated SOP with a change summary.
- [ ] Training completion is recorded per team and role.
- [ ] New system access and permissions are verified in place.
- [ ] Cutover dates and any parallel-run windows are documented.
- [ ] Steady-state owner is named for every rolled-out process.

## What it contains

- Updated SOPs per impacted function with change summaries
- Training completion per team and role
- New system access and permissions in place
- Cutover dates and any parallel-run windows
- Exception handling for edge cases the new process doesn't yet cover
- Owners for steady-state enforcement

## Inputs you rely on

- Process models (as-is / to-be) from Define
- Change impact assessment and affected-audiences list
- Support process model and live support processes
- Pricing & packaging plan (billing/finance implications)
- Stakeholder comms plan and executed comms per cadence

## Who consumes it

- Impacted internal teams — operate the new way from day one
- Customer Support and Success — coordinate handoffs under new process
- Finance and RevOps — reconcile bookings and revenue correctly
- Iterate-phase benefits review — attributes outcomes to process or product

## Common pitfalls

- Process docs updated but teams not trained or given system access
- Cutover date set but no parallel-run safety net
- Exception paths undefined; the first weird case breaks the process
- No steady-state owner, so process decays back to old habits
