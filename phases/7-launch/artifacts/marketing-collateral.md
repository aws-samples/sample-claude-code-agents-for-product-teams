## What this is

The kit of sales-ready assets launching with the product — pitch deck, one-pagers, battlecards, demo scripts, objection handlers, website updates, and partner-facing collateral. Everything a seller or partner needs to explain and close.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

Collateral is the lever between product capability and pipeline. If sales can't articulate positioning, differentiation, and pricing on day one, the window of launch attention converts into generic interest instead of qualified opportunities.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Positioning and messaging framework answers who, what, and why us in writing.
- [ ] Pitch deck and modular slide library are published and accessible to the field.
- [ ] Customer-facing one-pagers exist for each target persona or use case.
- [ ] Battlecards cover each named key competitor.
- [ ] Objection handlers and pricing-pushback responses are documented.

## What it contains

- Positioning and messaging framework (who, what, why us)
- Pitch deck and modular slide library
- Customer-facing one-pagers by persona or use case
- Battlecards vs. key competitors
- Demo script and environment
- Objection and pricing-pushback handlers
- Website and paid-media updates

## Inputs you rely on

- Pricing & packaging plan
- Competitive landscape report from Discover
- Feature documentation and user guides
- Demo assets from Developer
- Analyst/press coverage quotes (if cleared)

## Who consumes it

- Account executives and SEs — run the sales motion
- Partners and resellers — co-sell in their ecosystems
- Customer Support and Success — share language when discussing value
- Product Marketing — evolves pieces as messaging lands in the field

## Common pitfalls

- Collateral shipped without sales enablement (assets live, no training)
- Messaging that disagrees with the website or analyst briefings
- Single massive deck instead of a modular library for different motions
- No feedback loop from field reps to refresh materials post-launch
