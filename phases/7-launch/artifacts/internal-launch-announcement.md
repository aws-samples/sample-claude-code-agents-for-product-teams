# internal launch announcement

_Produced by: Internal all-hands launch communication_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The Sponsor's all-hands message announcing launch to the company: what shipped, who built it, why it matters, and what every team should do next. It is equal parts recognition, alignment, and call-to-action.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

Launch only succeeds if the rest of the company shows up — support answers tickets, sales runs the new motion, finance closes the books correctly. A crisp internal message signals executive commitment and prevents the launch from being treated as "that team's project."

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Plain-language summary names what is now live and the audiences it serves.
- [ ] Teams and individuals who delivered the launch are named in the recognition section.
- [ ] Each affected function has a stated action to take starting today.
- [ ] Links to customer-facing assets, release notes, and support playbooks are embedded.
- [ ] KPIs the company will watch and where to follow them are identified.

## What it contains

- Plain-language summary of what is now live and for whom
- Recognition of the teams and individuals who delivered it
- What each affected function needs to do starting today
- Links to customer-facing assets, release notes, and support playbooks
- KPIs the company will watch and where to follow them
- Escalation paths during hypercare

## Inputs you rely on

- Released product in production and production validation result
- Release notes and marketing collateral
- Trained sales & support teams confirmation
- Launch run-of-show from Project Manager
- Board/investor launch narrative for message consistency

## Who consumes it

- All employees — know what launched and what they owe the product
- Sales and Customer Support leaders — cascade into team standups
- Finance, Legal, and HR — reconcile internal systems and policies
- Future hires — read this as part of onboarding context

## Common pitfalls

- Sending before production validation passes — claims collapse on first customer report
- Celebrating without naming the teams; morale benefit evaporates
- Ambiguous "what to do next" leaves support and sales improvising
- Treating it as a press release instead of an operational activation
