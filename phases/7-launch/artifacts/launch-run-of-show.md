# launch run-of-show

_Produced by: Launch day coordination / command center_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The minute-by-minute choreography of launch day — who does what, in what order, with which decision points, from pre-launch checks to hypercare standdown. It is the script for the command center.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Launch day has too many moving parts (deploy, comms, analyst embargoes, support staffing, partner notifications) for improvisation. A published run-of-show turns the day into a coordinated operation, makes dependencies visible, and gives you a clean trigger for go/no-go and rollback.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Timeline lists T-minus and T-plus steps with a named owner for each.
- [ ] Command center roster documents shift coverage and communication channels.
- [ ] Gated decision points (go/no-go, continue/rollback) are called out with the decider named.
- [ ] Rollback procedure is documented and the authorizing role is identified.
- [ ] Exit criteria for hypercare standdown are stated.

## What it contains

- Timeline (T-minus and T-plus) with named owners per step
- Command center roster, shift coverage, and communication channels
- Pre-launch readiness checks and their owners
- Gated decision points (go/no-go, continue/rollback)
- Comms triggers (internal, customer, press, analyst)
- Rollback procedure and who authorizes it
- Exit criteria for hypercare standdown

## Inputs you rely on

- Deployment runbook and rollback plan from Release Manager
- Go/no-go decision package
- Executed launch comms schedule
- Trained sales & support team rosters and shift plans
- IR plan & runbook for incident handling

## Who consumes it

- Release Manager — executes deploy steps per the timeline
- Sponsor, PO, and Release Manager — exercise the go/no-go and continue/rollback gates
- Sales, Support, and PMM — time comms and availability against it
- SRE — aligns on-call and war-room coverage

## Common pitfalls

- Timeline in local time with a global audience — timezones cause missed cues
- Owners listed by role, not by named person on shift
- No rehearsal; owners see the run-of-show for the first time on launch day
- Missing the hypercare exit criteria, so the war room never stands down
