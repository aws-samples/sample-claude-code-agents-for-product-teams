# trained sales & support teams

_Produced by: Internal enablement / training_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Sales & Marketing

**Stakeholders:** Customer Support

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when no sales or support motion exists yet

## What this is

The evidence that the sales and support workforce is certified and ready to sell and service the new product — training completion records, certification scores, demo pass-offs, and role-specific readiness checklists.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

Pipeline and activation collapse if the front-line teams can't articulate or operate the product on day one. Certification is how you prove to the Sponsor and Sales VP that the first customer conversation won't be the team's first time hearing about the feature.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Curriculum is defined by role (AE, SE, SDR, CSM, support tiers).
- [ ] Training completion rosters with scores are recorded.
- [ ] Demo certification pass-offs are captured for every seller required to demo.
- [ ] Battlecard and objection-handling quiz results are logged.
- [ ] Remediation plan is documented for anyone not yet certified.

## What it contains

- Curriculum by role (AE, SE, SDR, CSM, support tiers)
- Training completion rosters and scores
- Demo certification pass-offs per seller
- Battlecard and objection-handling quizzes
- Support playbook walkthroughs and shadowing records
- Remediation plan for anyone not yet certified

## Inputs you rely on

- Marketing collateral and demo assets
- Pricing & packaging plan
- Feature documentation and user guides
- Support process model and live support processes
- Known-issues list and escalation paths

## Who consumes it

- Sales leadership — confirm bench readiness before launch gate
- Customer Support leadership — validate tier-1 and tier-2 coverage
- Sponsor and Product Owner — input to go/no-go decision
- Enablement team — refresh content as field feedback arrives

## Common pitfalls

- Training happens but certification doesn't — no proof of readiness
- Reps certified on the product but not on pricing or compliance language
- One-time training with no refresh as the product changes
- Support training lags sales, leading to tickets that can't be resolved at tier 1
