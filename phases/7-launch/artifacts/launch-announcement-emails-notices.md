# launch announcement emails/notices

_Produced by: Customer communications_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Sales & Marketing

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The segmented customer-facing messages announcing the launch — email campaigns, in-product banners, status page notices, and partner notifications. Targeted by segment (existing customer, trial, prospect, partner) and personalized where it matters.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

Existing customers are the easiest source of expansion revenue if you tell them something useful launched. They are also the fastest source of churn if a breaking change arrives unannounced. These notices set the tone for how the install base experiences the launch.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Segmented email templates exist with variants per persona or plan.
- [ ] In-product announcement banners or modals are authored and ready to publish.
- [ ] Status page and change-window notices are drafted for each affected surface.
- [ ] Migration or action-required messages name the affected customer segments and the required action.
- [ ] Send schedule and suppression rules are specified per audience.

## What it contains

- Segmented email templates with variants by persona or plan
- In-product announcement banners or modals
- Status page and change-window notices
- Partner and reseller notifications
- Migration or action-required messages for affected customers
- Send schedule and suppression rules

## Inputs you rely on

- Release notes and known-issues list
- Pricing & packaging plan (any billing-relevant changes)
- Stakeholder comms plan
- Legal approval on claims and required disclosures
- Segmentation from CRM and product analytics

## Who consumes it

- Existing customers — discover and adopt new capability
- Prospects on nurture — move from awareness to pipeline
- Partners — relay to their end customers
- Support — anticipate questions triggered by the message

## Common pitfalls

- Broadcast to the whole list instead of segmented by relevance
- Calls-to-action that point to docs that aren't published yet
- No action-required flag for customers who must migrate or reconfigure
- Sending before production validation — breakage mid-campaign
