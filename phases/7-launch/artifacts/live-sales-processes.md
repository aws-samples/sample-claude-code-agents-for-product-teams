## What this is

The sales motion running live on launch day — CRM updated with new products and SKUs, quoting/CPQ configured, deal-desk rules in place, compensation plans signed, and the pipeline engine pointing the right opportunities at the right reps.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

Pipeline targets from the business case only land if reps can actually quote, discount, and close the new thing. A half-configured CPQ or a missing comp plan causes deal slippage that compounds across the quarter and forces painful mid-quarter fixes.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Product, SKU, and pricing catalog entries exist and are live in CRM/CPQ.
- [ ] Quote templates and approval workflows are configured end-to-end.
- [ ] Deal-desk rules and the discount authority matrix are published.
- [ ] Compensation plan covering the new SKUs is signed and in effect.
- [ ] Pipeline reports and dashboards isolate the new product.

## What it contains

- Product, SKU, and pricing catalog live in CRM/CPQ
- Quote templates and approval workflows configured
- Deal-desk rules and discount authority matrix
- Comp plan in effect for new SKUs
- Pipeline reports and dashboards for the new product
- Partner/channel configurations where applicable

## Inputs you rely on

- Pricing & packaging plan
- Marketing collateral and battlecards
- Trained sales & support teams confirmation
- Commercial model decision from Define
- Finance and RevOps configuration of revenue recognition

## Who consumes it

- Account executives and managers — run the new motion
- Finance and RevOps — forecast and book revenue correctly
- Partners and resellers — quote through their channels
- Product Sponsor — validates pipeline health in operate reviews

## Common pitfalls

- CPQ goes live but quote approvals route to the wrong approvers
- Comp plan not finalized — reps de-prioritize selling the new SKU
- Missing partner configuration — channel deals stall
- Reporting doesn't separate the new product, so pipeline signal is noisy
