# Product Owner in Launch

Launch is where you convert months of sequencing decisions into a live product and the first real customers. You own the go/no-go call on launch readiness, sequence the value going out the door, and stay close to the baseline metrics that will tell you whether the increments actually earn value.

## What you do

- Chair the launch readiness review and make the go/no-go call with the Release Manager and Product Sponsor.
- Align with Business Analyst on the pre-launch metrics baseline so value delivery can be measured.
- Ratify pricing & packaging with Sales & Marketing and Product Sponsor so commercial terms match product value.
- Confirm legal & compliance sign-off is in hand before release.
- Stay reachable on launch day for trade-off calls that emerge during hypercare.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [pre-launch metrics baseline](artifacts/pre-launch-metrics-baseline.md) — Business Analyst owns; you validate the KPI sequencing.
- [pricing & packaging plan](artifacts/pricing-packaging-plan.md) — Sales & Marketing owns; you align to product value and scope.

## Outcomes you drive

- [go/no-go decision](outcomes/go-no-go-decision.md) — your call, made with Release Manager and Product Sponsor.
- [legal approval](outcomes/legal-approval.md) — Security & Compliance owns; you contribute product scope and commitments.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on go/no-go ratification and pricing posture.
- **Release Manager** — bi-directional on launch readiness and release sequencing.
- **Business Analyst** — bi-directional on baseline metrics and KPI wiring.
- **Security & Compliance** — bi-directional on legal sign-off and launch risk posture.
- **Sales & Marketing** — bi-directional on pricing, packaging, and launch narrative.

## Handoff into [Operate](../8-operate/README.md)

You exit Launch with the product live, a baselined scorecard, and a backlog ready to absorb real-customer signal. Point the team at KPI performance and support trends so the next sequencing decisions are grounded in Operate-phase evidence, not launch-week enthusiasm.
