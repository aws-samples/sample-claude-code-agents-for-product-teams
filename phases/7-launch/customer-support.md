# Customer Support in Launch

Launch is where your readiness becomes real. You cut over support processes, you're certified on the product, and the first real tickets start landing. Your job this phase is to make sure nothing customer-facing falls through the cracks on day one and that the support motion is live, not theoretical.

## What you do

- Execute the support process cutover so the new product is fully supported from go-live.
- Participate in internal enablement and training with Sales & Marketing — you're certified at GA.
- Staff the hypercare ticket queue and escalate launch-window issues cleanly.
- Start capturing early user feedback and known issues for the Iterate feedback loop.

## Artifacts you own

- [live support processes](artifacts/live-support-processes.md) — the cutover that makes the support motion real at GA.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [trained sales & support teams](artifacts/trained-sales-support-teams.md) — Sales & Marketing owns; you certify the support side.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Sales & Marketing** — bi-directional on enablement, training, and customer comms.
- **Technical Writer** — bi-directional on docs gaps surfaced by early tickets.
- **Release Manager** — bi-directional on hypercare escalations.
- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on early customer signal and launch-window trade-offs.

## Handoff into [Operate](../8-operate/README.md)

You exit Launch with support live and the first tickets flowing. Carry that forward into Operate's customer onboarding/activation, health scoring, renewal & expansion management, and triage discipline — retention is earned one ticket and one QBR at a time, and Operate is where cost-to-serve is managed against retention goals.
