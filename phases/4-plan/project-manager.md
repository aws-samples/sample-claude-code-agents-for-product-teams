# Project Manager in Plan

You are here to make delivery predictable. Plan is where you stand up the team, the cadence, and the instruments — iterations, RAID, governance, comms — that you'll run on for the rest of Build and Verify. Your baseline here is what everything gets measured against later; getting it right is the difference between transparent delivery and constant firefighting.

## What you do

- Onboard and ramp the team so people know who they are, what they're on, and how the team works.
- Plan iterations / milestones and assign work items so every day has a clear "what are we doing."
- Track features/epics and resource schedule so progress and staffing are visible.
- Set up the RAID log, risk register, governance calendar, and comms cadence — the instrumentation of delivery.
- Execute the stakeholder communication cadence so the org hears progress on rhythm.
- Partner with the Sponsor on the delivery baseline so what you commit to is what you can deliver.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's baseline and escalation policy by providing the plan and risk data they approve.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — co-owns baseline and risk register; you feed them schedule, budget, and top risks; they give you escalation thresholds.
- **Product Owner** — gives you the backlog your iteration plan pulls from.
- **Architect** — hands you the milestone plan that anchors your iteration sequencing.
- **All role leads** — you onboard, cadence, and track them.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

In Build your job shifts from setup to steady-state delivery management — weekly/biweekly status, scope-change impact coordination, RAID maintenance, budget burn tracking, cross-team sync, and vendor management. You know Plan is done when the first iteration is planned and running, RAID is live, the governance calendar is on every leader's calendar, and the baseline is signed.
