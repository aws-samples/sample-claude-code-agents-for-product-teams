# assigned workitems & daily status

_Produced by: Assign & manage workitems_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the squad uses a standup and a board directly

## What this is

The live view of who owns what right now — work items assigned to people, their current state, and the daily standup notes that capture progress and blockers. It's the signal you watch every day to keep the iteration on track.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Your job is early detection of drift, and drift shows up at the work-item level long before it shows up in status reports. Maintaining clean assignment and daily state is how you spot stuck items, surface hidden dependencies, and unblock before a slip becomes a miss.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every in-flight item lists exactly one owner
- [ ] Each item records a current state (not started, in progress, blocked, in review, done)
- [ ] Items exceeding expected time in a state carry an aging indicator
- [ ] Daily standup notes capture blockers and help-needed items
- [ ] Each item links to associated PRs, designs, and test artifacts

## What it contains

- Assignment map: each in-flight item has exactly one owner
- Current state per item (not started, in progress, blocked, in review, done)
- Aging indicators — items stuck in a state longer than expected
- Daily standup notes capturing blockers and help-needed
- Handoff notes when items change owners or cross roles
- Links to associated PRs, designs, and test artifacts

## Inputs you rely on

- Iteration plan and delivery-ready work items
- Onboarded team and resource schedule
- RAID log for known blockers and dependencies
- Governance calendar for standup and review timing

## Who consumes it

- Developers and QA — see their queue and surface blockers
- Product Owner — tracks feature progress without interrupting engineers
- Engineering Manager — spots load imbalance and flight-risk items
- Project Manager — rolls up to the feature/epic status report

## Common pitfalls

- Stale states — items "in progress" for the whole iteration with no daily update
- Multiple owners per item so accountability is diffuse
- Standup used as status read-out rather than blocker surfacing
- Tracking work on a separate spreadsheet that diverges from the tool of record
