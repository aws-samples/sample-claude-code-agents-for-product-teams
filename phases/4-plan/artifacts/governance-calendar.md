# governance calendar

_Produced by: Governance cadence setup_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the squad's own cadence is the only cadence

## What this is

The schedule of governance and delivery ceremonies — standups, iteration reviews, stage gates, steering, architecture/security reviews, release windows — with attendees, purpose, and artifacts produced by each.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Ad-hoc governance is chaos governance. A calendar of named ceremonies with clear purposes prevents duplicate meetings, ensures the right decisions happen at the right altitude, and gives the team a predictable rhythm they can plan around.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every ceremony lists cadence and duration
- [ ] Attendees, chair, and decision authority are named per ceremony
- [ ] Standing agenda and required pre-reads are specified
- [ ] Input and output artifacts are listed per ceremony
- [ ] Gates and approval checkpoints are called out explicitly
- [ ] Calendar integration and timezone handling are documented

## What it contains

- Ceremony list with cadence and duration
- Attendees, chair, and decision authority per ceremony
- Standing agenda and required pre-reads
- Artifacts that feed each ceremony and those produced out
- Gates and approval checkpoints called out explicitly
- Calendar integration and timezone handling for distributed teams

## Inputs you rely on

- Baselined schedule and iteration plan
- Stakeholder comms plan
- Escalation policy and change-control process
- Security/architecture review cadences already in use enterprise-wide
- Release Manager's release-window constraints

## Who consumes it

- All delivery roles — schedule their work around the cadence
- Product Sponsor — knows when decisions are requested of them
- Governance/PMO functions — track ceremony completion
- Joiners to the initiative — onboard to the rhythm quickly

## Common pitfalls

- Meeting count that consumes all of engineering's time
- Ceremonies with overlapping purpose (three forums for the same decision)
- No pre-read discipline — meetings become status catch-ups
- Calendar set once and never pruned as the initiative shifts
