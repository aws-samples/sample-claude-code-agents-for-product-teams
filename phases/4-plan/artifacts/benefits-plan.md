# benefits plan

_Produced by: Benefits realization plan_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when benefits are measured directly from KPI telemetry

## What this is

The plan for how the benefits claimed in the business case will be measured, attributed, and reviewed after launch — metric definitions, baselines, cadence, accountable owners, and the decision mechanism if benefits fall short.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Benefits without a plan to realize them are just sales material. Your value to the org is evidence-based: you produce this so the ROI model survives contact with reality and so the Sponsor has something concrete to review against during Operate and Iterate.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each benefit has a measurement definition with baseline and target values
- [ ] Attribution method is stated per benefit
- [ ] Measurement source and data owner are named per benefit
- [ ] Review cadence and sign-off rubric are defined
- [ ] A shortfall trigger and path to business-case reforecast are specified

## What it contains

- Benefit-by-benefit measurement definitions
- Baseline and target values with realization timing
- Attribution method (how do we know this initiative caused the change)
- Measurement source and data owner per benefit
- Review cadence and sign-off rubric
- Shortfall trigger and path to business-case reforecast

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved business case and ROI model
- KPI definitions
- Telemetry plan and KPI instrumentation
- Data classification and access constraints
- Stakeholder commitments on what "success" looks like

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — approves the plan and uses it in portfolio reviews
- Product Owner — aligns roadmap to benefit drivers
- Business Analyst — produces the ongoing benefits tracking in Operate
- Finance — reconciles claimed benefits to ledger impact

## Common pitfalls

- Benefits defined so loosely they can't be measured
- No baseline — any post-launch number sounds like success
- Attribution ignored — the business moves and this initiative takes credit or blame by proximity
- Plan handed off at launch and never reviewed
