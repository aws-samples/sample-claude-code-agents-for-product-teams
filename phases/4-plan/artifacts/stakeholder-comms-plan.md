# stakeholder comms plan

_Produced by: Stakeholder communication plan_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when stakeholders are inside the squad

## What this is

The plan for who needs to know what, when, and through which channel across the initiative. Each audience has message goals, cadence, format, and an owner. Drives the actual comms the PM executes.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Stakeholders who feel informed stay aligned; stakeholders who feel ignored escalate. Producing a thoughtful comms plan is how you convert the stakeholder map into predictable, proportional communication that prevents surprise and preserves the scope commitments you worked to secure.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Audience segments list interest, influence, and information needs
- [ ] Message objectives are stated per audience
- [ ] Channel, format, and frequency are specified per audience
- [ ] Each comms thread has an owner and backup
- [ ] Escalation triggers that change cadence or audience are defined
- [ ] Templates for recurring comms are referenced

## What it contains

- Audience segments with interest, influence, and info needs
- Message objectives per audience (inform, engage, decide)
- Channel, format, and frequency per audience
- Owner and backup per comms thread
- Escalation triggers that change cadence or audience
- Template references for recurring comms

## Inputs you rely on

- Stakeholder map and affected-audiences list
- Governance calendar
- Benefits plan and KPI definitions (what you'll brief)
- Risk register (what you'll disclose)
- Change-control and escalation policy

## Who consumes it

- Project Manager — executes the cadence and logs executed comms
- Product Owner and Sponsor — use it to shape their own stakeholder messaging
- Business Analyst — refines the plan as scope and risks evolve
- Sales, Support, and Marketing — know how and when product info reaches them

## Common pitfalls

- One-size-fits-all cadence — executives and SMEs get the same firehose
- Missing escalation paths so surprises still blindside senior stakeholders
- Comms plan written once and never updated as the stakeholder set shifts
- Plan exists but the PM is never handed the template or ownership
