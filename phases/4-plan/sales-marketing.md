# Sales & Marketing in Plan

Plan is a quiet phase for you — the draft GTM plan from Define is still the working document, and your real work ramps in Build (design-partner engagement) and peaks at Launch. Your job here is to stay aligned with the baselined schedule so GTM readiness tracks to the committed launch window, and to start warming early customers.

## What you do

- Track the baselined schedule so GTM milestones (analyst briefings, battlecards, enablement) align to the launch window.
- Begin identifying design partners / early-access candidates you'll formally engage in Build.
- Refresh positioning and messaging against any scope changes that emerged in Design / Plan.
- Align with Customer Support on joint onboarding readiness for the eventual beta/GA cohort.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't formally contribute to a Plan-phase artifact — your work is schedule alignment and early customer identification.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — confirms commercial model and launch window commitment.
- **Product Owner** — keeps you informed of scope changes that affect positioning.
- **Customer Support** — joint planning for beta / early-access onboarding.
- **Project Manager** — you're on the governance calendar so GTM prep stays in sync with delivery.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

In Build you formally commit design partners with the Sponsor and begin producing launch assets. In Verify you recruit the UAT / beta cohort with Customer Support. In Launch you execute. You know Plan is done when GTM milestones are on the governance calendar, early-partner candidates are identified, and positioning is current.
