# Product Owner in Plan

You are here to make sure the first thing the team picks up is the most valuable thing they could pick up — and to keep that true sprint after sprint. Plan is where the roadmap becomes a backlog, features become work items, and value sequence gets pressure-tested against SLOs, enablers, and team capacity. Engineering cannot start productively on day one without your ordered, refined, delivery-ready backlog.

## What you do

- Manage the product backlog so it is clear, ordered, and reflective of current strategy and constraints.
- Decompose features into work items with the Business Analyst so stories are sized, testable, and ready to pull.
- Partner with the SRE on SLA/SLO definition so reliability targets are a product decision, not just a platform one.
- Sequence enablers against features so technical runway and user value move together.
- Hold the line on "delivery-ready" as the entry criteria for Build.

## Artifacts you own

- [prioritized backlog](artifacts/prioritized-backlog.md) — ordered, refined, ready to pull from.
- [delivery-ready work items](artifacts/delivery-ready-work-items.md) — co-owned with Business Analyst; decomposed and acceptance-criteria-ready.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [service-level objectives](artifacts/service-level-objectives.md) — SRE owns; you set the product-side reliability expectation.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'. The baseline, resource commitment, and kickoff are owned by the Sponsor; your contribution is a backlog worth committing to.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — decomposes features into work items with you and carries acceptance criteria; you provide the value order.
- **Site Reliability Engineer** — co-defines SLOs; you bring user expectation, they bring operational reality.
- **Architect** — surfaces technical enablers that compete with features; you decide sequence.
- **Project Manager** — takes your backlog into iteration planning; you keep it refined ahead of their horizon.
- **Product Sponsor** — needs confidence your sequence matches the business case; you keep them informed of trade-offs.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

Build starts with the top of your backlog. You are done with Plan when the first two iterations' worth of work is delivery-ready, SLOs are signed, and the team knows what "next" looks like. In Build you'll switch from planning value to accepting it — progress review and increment acceptance become your primary lever.
