# assumptions & benefits-tracking log

_Produced by: In-flight benefits & assumptions tracking_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The running check on the business-case assumptions and expected benefits as Build reveals new information — which assumptions still hold, which have broken, and what that means for the ROI commitment. Sits under the benefits plan but gets active maintenance through Build.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

The business case is a prediction; Build produces data that should update it. Keeping assumptions visible and testing them against reality is how you preempt the post-launch "benefits shortfall investigation" — you flag the drift while there's still time to adjust scope, approach, or expectation.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every assumption shows a current status of holds, weakened, or broken
- [ ] Each status change cites trigger evidence
- [ ] Benefits at risk are named with magnitude
- [ ] Recommended actions are listed for every weakened or broken assumption
- [ ] Benefit re-baselining entries carry sign-off where needed

## What it contains

- Assumption inventory with current status (holds / weakened / broken)
- Trigger evidence for each status change
- Benefits at risk and magnitude
- Recommended actions to re-validate or adjust
- Sign-off on benefit re-baselining where needed
- Forward-look items to validate before launch

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved benefits plan and ROI model
- Integration test results and telemetry emerging in test
- Market/customer intel from Sales & Marketing
- Technical reality checks from Architect and Developer
- Scope-change decisions

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — triggers business-case reconfirmation or reset
- Product Owner — adjusts backlog where benefits drivers have shifted
- Architect — reassesses technical choices driving economics
- Steering forum — uses the log in governance reviews

## Common pitfalls

- Assumptions listed once and never re-reviewed
- Bad news suppressed to protect optimism of the plan
- Confusing "delivery is on track" with "benefits are on track"
- Adjustments made informally without updating the record
