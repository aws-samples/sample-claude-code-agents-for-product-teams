# budget burn report

_Produced by: Budget & burn tracking_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when the MVP has no separate budget line

## What this is

A period view of actuals vs. baseline spend — people, cloud/infra, vendor, tooling — with forecast to completion and variance commentary. The financial companion to the feature/epic status report.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Budget surprise is never welcome. Keeping burn visible and forecast honest is how you protect the initiative from late "actually you're 30% over" conversations that damage trust and trigger unhelpful freezes right when the team needs air-cover.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Spend to date is shown by category against plan
- [ ] Forecast to completion includes a stated confidence level
- [ ] Material variances carry a written explanation
- [ ] Commitments and actuals are separated (POs issued vs. invoices received)
- [ ] Reserve drawdown status is current

## What it contains

- Spend to date by category vs. plan
- Forecast to completion with confidence
- Variance explanation for material swings
- Commitments vs. actuals (POs issued, invoices received)
- Reserve drawdown status
- Budget-change requests in flight

## Inputs you rely on

- Baselined budget and resource schedule
- Actuals from finance systems, cloud billing, vendor invoices
- Change control package outcomes
- Resource schedule changes
- Vendor SOW and partner commitments

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — uses it in executive updates and steering
- Finance — reconciles against authorization and accruals
- Product Owner — trades scope vs. spend where forecast moves
- Procurement — aligns vendor commitments to actual need

## Common pitfalls

- Reporting commitments as actuals (or vice versa) — forecasting blind
- Cloud spend treated as noise and hidden in "other"
- Forecast ignores known upcoming commitments
- Report produced but never matched against the official P&L — reconciliation debt
