# governance briefing

_Produced by: Steering / board progress briefing_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

**Accelerated MVP:** optional when there is no governance forum

## What this is

A formal briefing artifact for steering committees, boards, or portfolio reviews — deeper than the executive status update, with the strategic narrative, investment posture, and any portfolio-level decisions the initiative needs.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

Steering and board forums shape whether the bet keeps its funding, its mandate, and its runway. Producing a well-framed briefing is how you keep the initiative correctly anchored to strategy, preempt political questions, and secure decisions that unblock the team.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Strategic recap states the bet, why now, and expected outcome
- [ ] Milestones hit and milestones at risk are listed with reasoning
- [ ] Investment posture compares burn to commitment
- [ ] Each material risk names the decision-owner and the ask
- [ ] Explicit recommendations and decisions sought are stated

## What it contains

- Strategic recap: the bet, why now, expected outcome
- Progress, milestones hit, milestones at risk with reasoning
- Investment posture (burn vs. commitment) and portfolio implications
- Material risks with decision-owner and ask
- Market or competitive intelligence that affects the bet
- Explicit recommendations and decisions sought

## Inputs you rely on

- Executive status update
- Business-case reconfirmation or reset
- Budget burn report and feature/epic status report
- Risk register and RAID log
- Commercial signals from Sales & Marketing (design partners, early pipeline)

## Who consumes it

- Board and steering committee — portfolio-level decisions
- CFO and Finance partners — investment oversight
- Other Sponsors in the portfolio — coordinate overlapping bets
- PMO — ensures the record reflects decisions taken

## Common pitfalls

- Briefing packed with delivery detail the forum can't act on
- No decision frame — meeting absorbs time and produces nothing actionable
- Strategic narrative drifts from the business case without acknowledging it
- Risks that were in last quarter's briefing still there with no mitigation update
