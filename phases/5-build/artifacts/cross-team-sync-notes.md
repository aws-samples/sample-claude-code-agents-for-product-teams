# cross-team sync notes

_Produced by: Cross-team sync facilitation_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

Running notes from dependency and cross-team sync meetings — commitments made, dates exchanged, blockers surfaced, risks raised. Captures the inter-team operating contract that the RAID log and status depend on.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Cross-team work is where most delivery slips live. Documented syncs with explicit commitments force other teams to own their dates in writing and give you evidence when commitments change — protecting the schedule without relying on memory or good faith.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Meeting date, attendees, and agenda are recorded
- [ ] Each commitment names the party, date, and confidence
- [ ] Decisions list the alternatives considered
- [ ] Every action item carries an owner and due date
- [ ] Dependency changes to record in the RAID log are flagged

## What it contains

- Meeting date, attendees, agenda
- Commitments made by each party, with dates and confidence
- Decisions taken and alternatives considered
- Action items with owners and due dates
- Dependency changes to record in RAID
- Escalations needed

## Inputs you rely on

- Dependency list and integration map
- RAID log and risk register
- Feature/epic status and technical milestone plan
- Other teams' schedules and published commitments

## Who consumes it

- Project Manager — updates RAID and status based on sync outcomes
- Architect and Developers — execute to the technical commitments
- Product Owner — sees scope implications of dependency slips
- Other teams — confirm or dispute the record before it hardens

## Common pitfalls

- Notes capture discussion but not decisions or commitments
- Action items without due dates — everything is "soon"
- Multiple parallel syncs with overlapping scope and contradictory outcomes
- Notes kept but never cross-linked to the RAID log, so nothing is tracked
