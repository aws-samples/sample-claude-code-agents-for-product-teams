# managed vendor deliverables

_Produced by: Vendor & contract management_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The tracked set of vendor and partner deliverables on the critical path — SOW milestones, acceptance state, invoice and payment status, escalation history, and risks. Makes external work as visible and managed as internal work.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Vendor slips are among the most common hidden risks — they live on someone else's schedule until they become your blocker. Active vendor management keeps their commitments dated, accepted, paid, and escalable; it's how you convert "them" back into "us" for delivery purposes.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each vendor entry references the SOW and names a primary contact
- [ ] Milestone table shows committed date, current forecast, and actuals
- [ ] Acceptance status and sign-off evidence are recorded per milestone
- [ ] Invoice and PO status is tied to milestones
- [ ] Escalation history is current and contractual remedies are identified

## What it contains

- Vendor list with SOW references and primary contacts
- Milestone table: committed date, current forecast, actuals
- Acceptance status and sign-off evidence per milestone
- Invoice/PO status against milestones
- Escalation history and current concerns
- Relationship health signals and contractual remedies available

## Inputs you rely on

- Vendor SOWs and master agreements
- Procurement records and budget burn
- Vendor selection process outputs
- RAID log entries for vendor risks
- Security & Compliance vendor risk assessment

## Who consumes it

- Project Manager — decides when to escalate or replace
- Product Sponsor — steps in when executive pressure is required
- Architect and Security — validate deliverable acceptance technically
- Procurement and Finance — handle commercial consequences

## Common pitfalls

- Accepting deliverables verbally, without written sign-off — disputes later
- Paying on schedule rather than on acceptance — loses commercial leverage
- Vendor status hidden in emails — no team visibility when the PM is out
- Contractual remedies unknown so escalation is bluff, not leverage
