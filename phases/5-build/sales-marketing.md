# Sales & Marketing in Build

Build is when you stop waiting and start engaging early customers. Your job is to commit design partners with the Sponsor, start real feedback loops, refine positioning against the product that's actually emerging, and prep launch assets so Launch is execution, not scramble.

## What you do

- Commit design partners / early-access customers with the Sponsor so feedback is real.
- Refine positioning and messaging against what's actually being built.
- Begin drafting battlecards, demo scripts, launch comms, and enablement material.
- Stay close to the Product Owner so you know which features will and won't be in the launch cohort.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own Build-phase artifacts formally — your outputs feed Verify's warmed briefings and Launch's collateral and comms.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [committed design partners](outcomes/committed-design-partners.md) — Product Sponsor owns; you identify, qualify, and close.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — co-owns design-partner commitments; uses executive relationships to close.
- **Product Owner** — tells you what's in/out so positioning stays honest.
- **Customer Support** — joint planning so design partners get a real onboarding experience.
- **Project Manager** — keeps GTM prep visible on the governance calendar.

## Handoff into [Verify](../6-verify/README.md)

In Verify you co-recruit the UAT / beta cohort with Customer Support and pre-brief analysts/press with the Sponsor. You know Build is done when design partners are committed, positioning is current against the committed scope, and the Launch asset backlog is in flight.
