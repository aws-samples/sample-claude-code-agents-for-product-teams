# Project Manager in Build

You are here to make Build legible. Build is where plan meets reality every single day — your status reports, RAID maintenance, budget tracking, cross-team syncs, and change-control coordination are what let the Sponsor see the truth and the team stay coordinated. If you disappear in Build, nobody notices until Verify, and by then it's too late.

## What you do

- Facilitate process improvement reviews so the team actually learns as it goes.
- Prepare the weekly/biweekly status report so Sponsor and PO have a current, honest read.
- Coordinate scope-change impact so decisions carry a complete package (cost, schedule, risk, dependencies).
- Maintain the RAID log continuously — new risks, assumptions, issues, dependencies as they emerge.
- Facilitate cross-team syncs so dependencies resolve before they become escalations.
- Track budget & burn so the Sponsor isn't surprised.
- Manage vendor & contract deliverables so external dependencies don't quietly slip.
- Co-own increment acceptance cadence with the Product Owner.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — receives your status, takes unblocks and scope-change packages you prepare.
- **Product Owner** — co-owns increment acceptance with you.
- **Architect / SRE / Security & Compliance / QA/Tester** — feed your RAID and status with current-reality signals.
- **Developer** — you track work items and flag burndown / dependency risks early.

## Handoff into [Verify](../6-verify/README.md)

In Verify you shift to verify-phase coordination, launch-readiness status reporting, and defect governance. You know Build is done when the RAID is cleared to baseline, budget burn is on plan (or deltas are documented), and you've got an honest status pack that says "ready for Verify" — or doesn't.
