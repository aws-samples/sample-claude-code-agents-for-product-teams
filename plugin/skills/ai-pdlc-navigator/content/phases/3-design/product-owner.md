# Product Owner in Design

Design is where the detail explodes — journeys, architecture, APIs, data model — and your job is to hold the scope line while signing off on the design package your team will build. You co-own the approved design package with the UI/UX Designer and the business-value alignment with the Sponsor and BA. Every design choice you approve is a choice to spend time and money you won't get back.

## What you do

- Sign off on the [approved design package](outcomes/approved-design-package.md) with the UI/UX Designer — the formal gate for the design pack
- Co-own the [validated business-value alignment](artifacts/validated-business-value-alignment.md) with the Sponsor and BA
- Co-own the [confirmed scope/budget or change request](outcomes/confirmed-scope-budget-or-change-request.md) with the Sponsor and PM
- Hold the MVP scope line as architecture and UX land — push back on scope creep
- Stay engaged with usability findings, vendor evaluation, and integration decisions that shape what users actually get

## Artifacts you own

- [validated business-value alignment](artifacts/validated-business-value-alignment.md) — the explicit "this design still delivers the business case" check (co-owned with Sponsor and BA)

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved design package](outcomes/approved-design-package.md) — the formal design gate, co-owned with the UI/UX Designer
- [confirmed scope/budget or change request](outcomes/confirmed-scope-budget-or-change-request.md) — co-owned with Sponsor and PM; the scope either holds or you raise the flag

## Who you work with

- **UI/UX Designer** — your co-signer on the design package; you trust their usability findings and accessibility review
- **Product Sponsor** — co-owner of the business-value alignment and scope/budget checks
- **Business Analyst** — co-owns the business-value alignment review and updates the traceability matrix
- **Architect** — you approve the architecture at the business-scope level; they own the technical side
- **Project Manager** — coordinates the design reviews and the scope/budget reality check with you

## Handoff into Plan

Your approved design package and scope-confirmation feed directly into [Plan](../4-plan/README.md), where you own the prioritized backlog and help set SLOs. The quality bar is that the design package is unambiguous enough for Plan to decompose into work items without you relitigating decisions. You're done when the team can begin backlog decomposition against a design you've personally approved, without scope being re-opened.
