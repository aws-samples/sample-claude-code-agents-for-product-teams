# UI/UX Designer in Design

This is your phase. Every artifact of substance is yours: journey maps, IA, specs, visuals, prototypes, usability findings, accessibility. You co-own the approved design package with the PO, and you co-own the build-ready handoff with Architect and Developer. If the design leaves Design ambiguous, incomplete, or untested against users, the whole build phase inherits the risk.

## What you do

- Customer journey mapping — produce the [journey map / service blueprint](artifacts/journey-map-service-blueprint.md)
- Information architecture — produce the [IA spec](artifacts/ia-spec.md)
- Create design specifications — own the [design specification](artifacts/design-specification.md)
- Create visual design — produce the [visual design mockups](artifacts/visual-design-mockups.md)
- Prototyping — build the [interactive prototype](artifacts/interactive-prototype.md)
- Usability testing (pre-build) — publish the [usability findings](artifacts/usability-findings.md)
- Accessibility design review — publish the [accessibility design report](artifacts/accessibility-design-report.md)
- Design review & sign-off — co-own the [approved design package](outcomes/approved-design-package.md) with the PO
- Design → engineering handoff review — co-own the [build-ready package](artifacts/build-ready-package.md) with Architect and Developer

## Artifacts you own

- [journey map / service blueprint](artifacts/journey-map-service-blueprint.md) — end-to-end user and service flows
- [IA spec](artifacts/ia-spec.md) — information architecture and navigation
- [design specification](artifacts/design-specification.md) — component-level design with states and behavior
- [visual design mockups](artifacts/visual-design-mockups.md) — high-fidelity visuals
- [interactive prototype](artifacts/interactive-prototype.md) — clickable flows for testing and handoff
- [usability findings](artifacts/usability-findings.md) — pre-build usability test results with changes made
- [accessibility design report](artifacts/accessibility-design-report.md) — WCAG-level review of designs before build
- [build-ready package](artifacts/build-ready-package.md) — the packaged handoff to engineering (co-owned with Architect and Developer)

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved design package](outcomes/approved-design-package.md) — formal sign-off on the UX side of the design (co-owned with PO)

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — your co-signer on the approved design package; scope questions surface here
- **Architect** — co-owns the build-ready package with you; your design must align with architecture and data model
- **Developer** — co-owns the build-ready package with you; they're your handoff audience
- **Business Analyst** — their requirements and business rules shape what you're designing

## Handoff into Plan

Your approved design package and build-ready handoff feed directly into [Plan](../4-plan/README.md) — backlog decomposition, delivery-ready work items, and the architecture runway. The quality bar is that a developer reading your specs and prototype can build the right thing without guessing, and that usability risks have been tested pre-build rather than discovered in Verify. You're done when Plan can slice the design into work items and Build can pick them up without UX ambiguity.
