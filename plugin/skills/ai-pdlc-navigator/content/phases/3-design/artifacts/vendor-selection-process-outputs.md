# vendor selection process outputs

_Produced by: Vendor selection process coordination_

**Business outcome supported:** produce a build-ready design package — UX, architecture, data model, APIs, threat model, and integration map — with engineering signed off to build.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The record of how the vendor-selection process was run — shortlisting, RFPs, demos, POCs, scoring, and decision. It is the process-governance artifact that makes the vendor choice defensible to procurement, finance, and (if things go wrong) legal.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

You produce this because vendor decisions draw scrutiny — sunk cost, lock-in, reference checks, rival vendors complaining. A clean process record turns "why did you pick them?" from a political question into a documented answer.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Scope of the decision and success criteria are captured at the start.
- [ ] Long-list and shortlisting rationale are recorded.
- [ ] RFP/RFI documents and responses are attached along with demo and POC notes.
- [ ] Reference-call outcomes are recorded, including any independent back-channels.
- [ ] Final decision captures rationale and any dissents.

## What it contains

- Scope of the decision and success criteria
- Long-list and shortlisting rationale
- RFP / RFI documents sent and responses received
- Demo and POC outcomes with evaluation notes
- Scoring against [vendor evaluation scorecard](vendor-evaluation-scorecard.md)
- Reference calls and their outcomes
- Final decision with rationale and dissents
- Contract negotiation log

## Inputs you rely on

- [Vendor evaluation scorecard](vendor-evaluation-scorecard.md) and [vendor risk assessment](vendor-risk-assessment.md)
- [Technical options assessment](technical-options-assessment.md)
- Procurement process standards
- Legal input on contract expectations

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — uses it for [vendor partnership agreement](../outcomes/vendor-partnership-agreement.md)
- Procurement / Legal — contract negotiation and record-keeping
- Internal auditor — when vendor spend crosses policy thresholds
- Project Manager (future you) — references in Build for vendor-deliverable management

## Common pitfalls

- Incomplete record — demos evaluated informally, no notes
- Reference calls with references the vendor selected, no independent backdoor checks
- Missing dissent capture, so "we all agreed" is unverifiable
- No cutoff date on the process — "one more demo" becomes a months-long delay
