# Customer Support / Success in Design

You are off-stage in Design but paying attention to what will land in your queue later. The journeys, information architecture, and design specs emerging this phase determine what customers will struggle with, what will generate tickets, and what will drive activation. Read the design package and refine your support process model in response.

## What you do

- Read the [journey map / service blueprint](artifacts/journey-map-service-blueprint.md), [IA spec](artifacts/ia-spec.md), and [design specification](artifacts/design-specification.md) — predict where support tickets will come from
- Refine your Define-era support process model in light of the approved design
- Flag friction visible in the design or usability findings so it gets fixed pre-build

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **UI/UX Designer** — their journey maps and usability findings tell you where trouble will be
- **Technical Writer** — customer-facing docs and your knowledge base have to align
- **Sales & Marketing** — their GTM plan and your support model must be consistent on expectations

## Handoff into Plan

You become materially active in Verify (UAT cohort recruitment) and Launch (support cutover). The quality bar in Design is that your support process model anticipates the friction visible in the approved design. You're done when you know what the ticket shape will likely look like and you've flagged high-friction flows for the designer while it's still cheap to change them.
