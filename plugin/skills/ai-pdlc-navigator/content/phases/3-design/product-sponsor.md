# Product Sponsor in Design

Design is where decisions get expensive. Build-vs-buy, vendor partnerships, architecture strategy, risk appetite — these are calls that lock the initiative into a shape it won't easily escape. You own the executive layer of those decisions, you ratify the architecture at the strategic level, and you run the steering cadence. Your signatures here are what Plan builds against.

## What you do

- Approve build-vs-buy & vendor decisions — produce the [build-vs-buy decision](outcomes/build-vs-buy-decision.md) with the Architect
- Design-to-business-case alignment review — co-own the [validated business-value alignment](artifacts/validated-business-value-alignment.md) with the PO and BA
- Architecture strategic review — issue the [architecture executive approval](outcomes/architecture-executive-approval.md) with the Architect
- Budget & scope reality check — produce the [confirmed scope/budget or change request](outcomes/confirmed-scope-budget-or-change-request.md) with the PO and PM
- Risk appetite ratification — produce the [ratified risk decisions](outcomes/ratified-risk-decisions.md) with Security & Compliance
- Vendor / partner executive alignment — produce the [vendor partnership agreement](outcomes/vendor-partnership-agreement.md)
- Steering committee review — chair the [steering checkpoint](outcomes/steering-checkpoint.md)

## Artifacts you own

- [validated business-value alignment](artifacts/validated-business-value-alignment.md) — explicit check that the design still delivers the business case (co-owned with PO and BA)

## Outcomes you drive

- [build-vs-buy decision](outcomes/build-vs-buy-decision.md) — make or acquire, with the Architect
- [architecture executive approval](outcomes/architecture-executive-approval.md) — strategic ratification of the architecture
- [confirmed scope/budget or change request](outcomes/confirmed-scope-budget-or-change-request.md) — either scope and budget hold or change is filed
- [ratified risk decisions](outcomes/ratified-risk-decisions.md) — explicit acceptance of residual risk with Security
- [vendor partnership agreement](outcomes/vendor-partnership-agreement.md) — executive-level vendor commitment
- [steering checkpoint](outcomes/steering-checkpoint.md) — periodic governance review

## Who you work with

- **Architect** — your primary partner this phase; they bring the technical options, you bring the strategic lens
- **Product Owner** — co-owns the business-value alignment review and the scope/budget reality check
- **Security & Compliance** — co-owns the ratified risk decisions with you
- **Project Manager** — packages the steering material and coordinates vendor selection with you
- **Business Analyst** — co-owns the business-value alignment review with you and the PO

## Handoff into Plan

Your Design-phase signatures are the foundation for [Plan](../4-plan/README.md) — approved resourcing, baselined schedule and budget, portfolio priority decisions, escalation policy, and initiative kickoff. The quality bar is that no major decision requires re-opening in Plan; vendor is chosen, scope is confirmed, risk is accepted, architecture is approved. You're done when Plan can stand up the team, pipeline, and backlog against a design package you've personally ratified.
