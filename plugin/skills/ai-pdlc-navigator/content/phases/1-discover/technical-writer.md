# Technical Writer in Discover

You are off-stage in Discover. There is no product to document, no API to reference, and no user guide to draft. Your job this phase is to stay aware of the opportunity so that when the team starts building something real, you already understand who the users are and what language will resonate with them.

## What you do

- Stay aware of the opportunity — skim the [vision statement](artifacts/vision-statement.md), [user personas](artifacts/user-personas.md), and [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md) as they form
- Notice the language — how the PO and BA talk about users, jobs, and pains in Discover will shape how your docs should read later

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **UI/UX Designer** — you'll share user-empathy ground with them throughout the lifecycle; start tracking their persona work
- **Business Analyst** — their validated problem statement is the richest source of real user language you'll have

## Handoff into Define

Nothing you produce is a hard handoff into [Define](../2-define/README.md), and you won't be heavily active until Verify and Launch when docs land for real. The quality bar is that you're never a stranger to the product when it's time to document it. You're done when you could sketch a docs IA based on the personas and vision alone.
