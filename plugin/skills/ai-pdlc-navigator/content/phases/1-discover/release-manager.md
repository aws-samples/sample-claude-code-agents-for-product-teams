# Release Manager in Discover

You are off-stage in Discover. There is no release to plan, no environment to stage, and no runbook to write — the initiative may not even be funded. Your job this phase is to stay informed so that when the team starts to build and ship, you already know the shape of the release problem.

## What you do

- Stay aware of the opportunity — skim the [opportunity brief](artifacts/opportunity-brief.md) and [vision statement](artifacts/vision-statement.md) if they're in your release train's scope
- Note release-shaping signals — commercial model hints in the [business case](artifacts/business-case.md), regulatory posture in the flags, integration complexity in the feasibility memo — anything that will change your deployment approach later

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Architect** — your primary touchpoint later; for now, just track where they're leaning on build-vs-buy and integration shape
- **Product Sponsor** — you'll meet them properly in Verify and Launch; for now just know the initiative exists

## Handoff into Define

Nothing you produce is a hard handoff into [Define](../2-define/README.md); you become materially active in Plan when the deployment runbook and release cadence need to be set. The quality bar is awareness — no surprises later about what this initiative is, who it's for, or how regulated it is. You're done when you could describe the initiative in one sentence to your release-train peers.
