# QA / Tester in Discover

You are off-stage in Discover. There is nothing to test yet, and no requirements to map tests against — writing test strategy now would be fiction. Your job this phase is to stay aware of the initiative and its risk posture so that when Define opens you can immediately start thinking about testability and coverage.

## What you do

- Stay aware of the opportunity — skim the [opportunity brief](artifacts/opportunity-brief.md), [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md), and [regulatory risk flag](artifacts/regulatory-risk-flag.md) as they form
- Note early risk signals — regulated data, high-stakes workflows, or novel technology choices will shape your Define-phase test strategy

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — you'll be their partner in Define; start tracking their research so requirements-to-tests tracing isn't a cold start
- **Security & Compliance** — their regulatory and data flags will drive your security test partnership in later phases

## Handoff into Define

Nothing you produce is a hard handoff into [Define](../2-define/README.md), but as soon as requirements start to take shape you'll be pairing on acceptance criteria and mapping tests to requirements. The quality bar is that you arrive in Define already thinking about risk-based coverage rather than volume. You're done when you know the shape of the bet well enough to propose a testing posture on day one of Define.
