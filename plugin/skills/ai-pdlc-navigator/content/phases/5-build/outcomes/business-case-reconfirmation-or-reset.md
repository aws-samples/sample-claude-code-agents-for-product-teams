# business-case reconfirmation or reset

_Produced by: Re-validate business case vs. emerging reality_

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Business Analyst

## What this is

Mid-build, the Sponsor — informed by the BA's in-flight benefits tracking — either re-confirms that the business case remains valid, or resets it to reflect what reality now shows.

## Why it matters

Plans decay. Re-confirming forces the team to either trust the baseline or update it before launch commits them to a fantasy.

## What it contains

- Updated assumptions vs. Define-phase assumptions
- Revised benefits forecast if reset
- Decision to continue, adjust scope, or escalate

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Updated assumptions are compared line-by-line to Define-phase assumptions
- [ ] A revised benefits forecast is recorded when the case is reset
- [ ] The decision explicitly states continue, adjust scope, or escalate

## Entry criteria

- Assumptions & benefits-tracking log current
- Actual costs and timelines visible

## Exit signal

The team is either executing the original plan or an explicitly updated one — never a ghost plan.
