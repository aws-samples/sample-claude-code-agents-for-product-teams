# process improvement actions

_Produced by: Process improvement review_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The running list of delivery-process improvements generated from retrospectives and team observations during Build — specific, owned actions to reduce friction, not a retro-notes dump. Each item has an owner, a target date, and a success signal.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

A team that retros without acting regresses. Converting insight to owned action — and closing the loop visibly — is how the team's speed and quality compound iteration over iteration instead of re-discussing the same pain every sprint.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every action item carries an owner, target date, and success signal
- [ ] Each entry cites the source retro or observation
- [ ] Status and changes since last review are recorded
- [ ] Experiments in progress state what they are measuring
- [ ] Retired actions capture the outcome

## What it contains

- Action items with owner, target date, and success signal
- Source retro or observation that generated each
- Status and changes since last review
- Experiments in progress and what they're measuring
- Retired actions with outcome captured
- Cross-team actions flagged for coordination

## Inputs you rely on

- Retrospective notes from iteration ceremonies
- Cross-team sync notes
- Updated RAID log entries
- Budget burn and velocity trends
- Voice-of-the-team signals (surveys, 1:1 themes)

## Who consumes it

- Delivery team — executes the actions
- Engineering Manager — supports actions needing investment
- Product Owner — sees where process pain affects throughput
- Business Analyst — captures which actions later feed lessons learned

## Common pitfalls

- Actions with no owner and no due date — theater
- Same item appearing retro after retro without decomposition or escalation
- Team-level actions that need org-level sponsorship and stall silently
- No visible closure — team stops trusting the process
