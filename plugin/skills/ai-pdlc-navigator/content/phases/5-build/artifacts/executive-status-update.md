# executive status update

_Produced by: Executive checkpoint review_

**Business outcome supported:** produce working, tested, instrumented increments that meet the Definition of Done, with stakeholder-accepted quality and documented lessons for continuous improvement.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The Sponsor's concise, recurring briefing to executive peers — board, executive staff, portfolio steering — on what's happening in Build. Progress against baseline, emerging risks, decisions needed, and asks for air-cover. Shorter than the PM's status report, pitched one level higher.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

Executives don't read PM status reports; they read yours. This artifact is your instrument for keeping the initiative correctly positioned in their minds — defending the runway when it's on track and surfacing help-needed before issues become political.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Headline sentence states on track / at risk / off track with the why
- [ ] Progress against committed baseline and major milestones is shown
- [ ] Top 2-3 risks are listed with mitigations in flight
- [ ] Decisions or air-cover requests are explicitly named
- [ ] Material scope, budget, or timeline changes since last update are listed

## What it contains

- Headline sentence: on track / at risk / off track — with the why
- Progress against committed baseline and major milestones
- Top 2–3 risks with mitigations in flight
- Decisions or air-cover requested from executives
- Material scope, budget, or timeline changes since last update
- Forward look for the next period

## Inputs you rely on

- Weekly/biweekly status report and feature/epic status report
- Risk register and RAID log
- Budget burn report
- Change control package and scope-change decisions
- Business-case reconfirmation signals

## Who consumes it

- Board and executive staff — portfolio-level governance
- Peer Sponsors — coordinate cross-initiative impacts
- Finance — tracks commitment against authorization
- PMO — rolls up to portfolio dashboards

## Common pitfalls

- Too long — executives don't read it and it becomes a PM artifact in disguise
- Consistently green until the cliff — no graduated signal
- Asking for nothing — if nothing needs a decision, the forum is wasted
- Omitting known-bad news hoping it resolves quietly before next update
