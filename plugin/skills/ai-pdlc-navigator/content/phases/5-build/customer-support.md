# Customer Support in Build

Build is when you start turning your Plan-phase prep into real readiness. As features land, you begin seeding the knowledge base, drafting support runbooks, and staying close to design-partner feedback so you see the bugs and confusion customers will hit at launch.

## What you do

- Seed the knowledge base with draft articles against merged features.
- Draft support runbooks for the product as it takes shape.
- Absorb design-partner feedback that Sales & Marketing is collecting, so you know what's coming.
- Refine the UAT / beta recruitment pipeline with Sales & Marketing so Verify can move fast.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any Build-phase artifacts — your outputs feed Verify (UAT cohort) and Launch (live support processes).

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't formally contribute to a Build-phase artifact — your contribution is readiness preparation.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Sales & Marketing** — joint UAT / beta recruitment pipeline; shared visibility on design-partner feedback.
- **Technical Writer** — aligns on deflection-oriented content you need in the KB.
- **Product Owner** — you flag product gaps or UX friction design partners are hitting.
- **Project Manager** — keeps your readiness milestones tracked.

## Handoff into [Verify](../6-verify/README.md)

In Verify you recruit and onboard the UAT / beta cohort with Sales & Marketing — your first producer moment. You know Build is done when KB drafts exist for every accepted increment, cohort pipeline is healthy, and you're confident you can onboard a real user without surprises.
