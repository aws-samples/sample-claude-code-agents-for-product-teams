# UI/UX Designer in Build

Build is a consulting role for you. The approved design package is the source of truth; your job is to make sure implementation stays faithful to it, answer questions fast, and absorb reality feedback from developers without letting scope creep dress itself up as "design changes."

## What you do

- Answer developer clarifications against the design spec and interactive prototype.
- Review in-flight UI work for fidelity — catch drift before it becomes merged code.
- Absorb build-time reality (platform constraints, technical trade-offs) and decide when to adapt the design vs. hold the line.
- Prepare for Verify — your visual QA, accessibility testing, and documentation-review cycles are coming.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't formally contribute to a Build-phase artifact — your contribution is live design guidance and fidelity oversight.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Developer** — your primary counterpart; you answer their questions, they implement your designs.
- **Product Owner** — flags UX-affecting scope questions to you before accepting increments.
- **Architect** — you escalate when an architectural constraint forces a design deviation.
- **Technical Writer** — aligns on in-product copy and terminology as content gets wired into the UI.

## Handoff into [Verify](../6-verify/README.md)

In Verify you own the visual QA report and co-own accessibility testing with QA. You know Build is done when merged increments match the approved design, no design clarification is outstanding, and you have a clean scope for visual and accessibility verification.
