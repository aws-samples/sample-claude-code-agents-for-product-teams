# Product Sponsor in Build

You are here to hold scope, hold the business case, and clear the path. Build is where reality collides with the plan — velocity disappoints, dependencies surface, early customers react. Your job is to re-validate the bet as evidence accumulates, arbitrate scope changes before they metastasize, and unblock cross-org obstacles the team can't clear themselves.

## What you do

- Run the executive checkpoint review so leadership has a current, honest read on progress.
- Unblock cross-org dependencies with the Project Manager — use your authority, not theirs.
- Hold scope discipline jointly with the Product Owner so every change gets an explicit yes/no.
- Re-validate the business case with the Business Analyst against emerging build reality — confirm, reset, or call it.
- Commit design partners / early customers with Sales & Marketing so feedback starts flowing before Launch.
- Brief steering / board on progress so governance stays warm and supportive.

## Artifacts you own

- [executive status update](artifacts/executive-status-update.md) — honest read for leadership.
- [governance briefing](artifacts/governance-briefing.md) — steering / board progress.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to other-owned artifacts this phase beyond your outcomes.

## Outcomes you drive

- [unblocked dependencies](outcomes/unblocked-dependencies.md) — co-owned with Project Manager.
- [scope-change decisions](outcomes/scope-change-decisions.md) — co-owned with Product Owner.
- [business-case reconfirmation or reset](outcomes/business-case-reconfirmation-or-reset.md) — co-owned with Business Analyst.
- [committed design partners](outcomes/committed-design-partners.md) — co-owned with Sales & Marketing.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — co-owns scope-change decisions with you; they surface, you arbitrate.
- **Project Manager** — escalates unblocks, tracks dependencies, and produces your status pack.
- **Business Analyst** — tracks benefits assumptions and flags business-case drift.
- **Sales & Marketing** — lines up design partners whose feedback you sanity-check the business case against.

## Handoff into [Verify](../6-verify/README.md)

In Verify you move from "is this still worth building?" to "is this safe to ship?" — launch-readiness assessment, cohort approval, launch-window commitment, and risk-posture sign-off. You know Build is done when accepted increments meet the Definition of Done, the business case still stands (or has been formally reset), and the team can point at working, tested, instrumented software.
