# Project Manager in Launch

Launch is where your coordination muscle is on full display. You run the command center, execute the communications cadence, and package the go/no-go inputs so the decision-makers can move fast with confidence. Your job is to make a complex cross-org launch look predictable.

## What you do

- Run the launch-day command center and own the run-of-show across all contributors.
- Execute the launch communications cadence internally and externally per plan.
- Package the go/no-go inputs so Product Owner, Release Manager, and Sponsor can decide on evidence.
- Track launch-day risks and escalate decisively when thresholds are crossed.

## Artifacts you own

- [launch run-of-show](artifacts/launch-run-of-show.md) — the minute-by-minute coordination plan for launch day.
- [executed launch comms](artifacts/executed-launch-comms.md) — confirmation that the comms cadence landed as planned.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

- [go/no-go decision package](outcomes/go-no-go-decision-package.md) — the integrated evidence pack feeding the decision.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on go/no-go inputs and command-center cadence.
- **Release Manager** — bi-directional on launch-day execution and war-room handoffs.
- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on executive updates through the launch window.
- **Sales & Marketing** — bi-directional on external comms coordination.

## Handoff into [Operate](../8-operate/README.md)

You exit Launch with a cleanly executed release and a run-of-show that teams can learn from. Hand the team into Operate's steady-state handoff: convert hypercare energy into an operating cadence with predictable reviews, escalation paths, and ownership — closure discipline protects everything you just launched.
