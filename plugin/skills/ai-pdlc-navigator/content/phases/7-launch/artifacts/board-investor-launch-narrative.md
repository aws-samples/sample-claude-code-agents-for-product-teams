# board/investor launch narrative

_Produced by: Board / investor launch briefing_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The narrative the Sponsor delivers to the board, investors, or executive committee at launch — what shipped, why it matters to the portfolio, and what performance to expect over the next one to four quarters. It frames the bet in strategic terms, not feature terms.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

You staked reputation and capital on this initiative; the board needs to hear the story from you, grounded in the business case and the KPIs you committed to. A clear narrative locks in air-cover for the operate period and pre-positions the reforecast conversation that will happen in Iterate.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Thesis restatement names the problem, the bet, and the strategic fit in plain language.
- [ ] Launch scope summary separates what is live, what is deferred, and what is next.
- [ ] KPIs to watch are listed with the cadence of future board updates.
- [ ] Commercial model, pricing, and ramp expectations are stated with reference to business-case assumptions.
- [ ] Top risks and mitigations — including rollback posture — are documented.

## What it contains

- Thesis restatement: the problem, the bet, and the strategic fit
- Launch scope summary — what is live, what is deferred, what is next
- KPIs to watch and the cadence of future board updates
- Commercial model, pricing, and ramp expectations
- Top risks and mitigations (including rollback posture)
- Asks of the board (funding, intros, patience on ramp)

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved business case and any in-flight reforecast
- Pre-launch metrics baseline and launch KPI dashboard design
- Go/no-go decision and rollback safety sign-off
- Pricing & packaging plan and GTM readiness from Sales & Marketing
- Executive-level talking points from Product Owner

## Who consumes it

- Board members and investors — approve continued investment posture
- CEO and executive peers — align messaging across the portfolio
- Product Owner and Sales & Marketing — borrow the language for downstream comms
- Finance — reconcile narrative against forecast and budget

## Common pitfalls

- Pitching feature lists instead of strategic outcomes the board can track
- Over-promising ramp without referencing the business case's own assumptions
- Hiding known issues or the rollback posture — the board finds out anyway
- No stated metrics, so there is nothing to hold the product to next quarter
