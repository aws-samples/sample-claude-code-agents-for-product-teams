# pricing & packaging plan

_Produced by: Pricing & packaging decision_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Sales & Marketing

**Stakeholders:** Product Owner, Product Sponsor

## What this is

The approved commercial model at launch: what bundles/tiers exist, list and floor prices, unit of value, add-ons, trial and discount policy, and how billing reconciles to usage. Owned jointly by PMM, PO, and Sponsor.

## Why it matters for the Sales & Marketing team

Pricing is the single biggest lever on revenue and on deal cycle length. A clean packaging plan lets sellers quote confidently, keeps margin honest, and ties the commercial model back to the business case assumptions the Sponsor committed to.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Tiers and bundles list included capabilities and limits for each.
- [ ] List price, floor price, and discount matrix by segment are documented.
- [ ] Billing unit and metering approach are specified for every SKU.
- [ ] Trial, PLG, and freemium rules are written down.
- [ ] Legal review status is recorded for terms and disclosures.

## What it contains

- Tiers/bundles with included capabilities and limits
- List price, floor price, and discount matrix by segment
- Billing unit (seats, usage, consumption) and metering approach
- Trial, PLG, and freemium rules
- Promotions and launch incentives
- Legal review of terms and disclosures
- Migration path for existing customers (if applicable)

## Inputs you rely on

- Pricing benchmark report from Discover
- ROI model and business case assumptions
- Commercial model decision from Define
- Win/loss insights from earlier products (if comparable)
- Feasibility review from Finance/Revenue Ops on billing

## Who consumes it

- Sales — quote and negotiate with confidence
- Finance and RevOps — billing, invoicing, revenue recognition
- Customer Success — conversations about renewals and expansion
- Product Owner — informs roadmap of monetized vs. table-stakes features

## Common pitfalls

- Pricing set without validating billing system can actually meter it
- Discount floors undocumented — deal-desk chaos in week one
- Packaging that hides the flagship capability behind the top tier
- Forgetting the migration story for existing customers on legacy plans
