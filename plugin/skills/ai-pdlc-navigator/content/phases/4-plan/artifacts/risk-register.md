# risk register

_Produced by: Risk identification & escalation_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** Product Sponsor

## What this is

The living inventory of identified delivery risks — probability, impact, owner, mitigation, trigger, and status. Feeds into the RAID log as the "R" but is owned distinctly because it needs Sponsor visibility and active management.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Risks that are tracked and reviewed are risks you can buy down; risks that live in one person's head are risks that become issues. This register, paired with the escalation policy, is the mechanism by which you surface the things that could blow up the baseline before they do.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each risk is stated in "because X, there's a chance of Y, leading to Z" form
- [ ] Probability and impact are scored per risk
- [ ] Owner and review cadence are assigned per risk
- [ ] Mitigation plan and escalation trigger are specified
- [ ] Status history is preserved so the trend is visible
- [ ] Risks tie to contingency budget or schedule reserve where applicable

## What it contains

- Risk statement in "because X, there's a chance of Y, leading to Z" form
- Probability × impact scoring
- Owner and review cadence per risk
- Mitigation plan and trigger condition for escalation
- Status history so the trend is visible
- Link to contingency budget or schedule reserve where applicable

## Inputs you rely on

- Feasibility memo and technical feasibility findings
- Dependency list and RAID log
- Security & compliance risk items
- Lessons learned from comparable initiatives
- Daily standups and cross-team sync notes

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — uses top risks in executive updates and steering reviews
- Architect and SRE — own technical risks and drive mitigations
- Security & Compliance — cross-reference compliance and security risks
- Project Manager — threads risks into status and change-control

## Common pitfalls

- Register dumps with 80 risks, none reviewed — becomes checkbox theater
- Risks with no owner, no trigger, and no mitigation — just a worry list
- Failing to retire risks that no longer apply so the signal drowns
- Keeping spicy risks out of sight of the Sponsor until they become issues
