**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The time-phased allocation plan for people and specialist resources across iterations — who is on, at what percentage, when specialists rotate in, and where there are planned troughs or peaks.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Your schedule is only as real as the capacity behind it. Maintaining an honest resource schedule is how you catch over-allocation, time-off collisions, and vendor misalignment early — and how you defend the baseline when someone asks to pull a person "just for a few weeks."

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Allocation matrix covers each person across each iteration
- [ ] Specialist and shared-resource bookings with other programs are captured
- [ ] Time-off and holiday calendar is merged in
- [ ] Ramp-up/ramp-down curves are noted for new hires and contractors
- [ ] Gaps vs. skills requirement list a planned remedy
- [ ] Vendor and partner resource commitments include dates

## What it contains

- Person-by-iteration allocation matrix
- Specialist/shared-resource bookings with other programs
- Time-off and holiday calendar
- Ramp-up/ramp-down curves for new hires or contractors
- Gaps vs. skills requirement with planned remedies
- Vendor/partner resource commitments and dates

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved resource commitment and skills requirement input
- Onboarded team
- Iteration plan and baselined schedule
- HR systems for time-off
- Vendor SOWs and partner commitments

## Who consumes it

- Engineering Manager — sees their team's committed load
- Product Sponsor — approves when another initiative asks to borrow someone
- Release Manager and SRE — confirm on-call and launch-window coverage
- Project Manager — inputs to every schedule forecast

## Common pitfalls

- Allocating at 100% — leaves zero slack for support, reviews, or interviews
- Treating part-time specialists as full-time when scheduling their deliverables
- Updating only when something breaks instead of with iteration cadence
- Hiding the schedule in a spreadsheet only the PM can read
