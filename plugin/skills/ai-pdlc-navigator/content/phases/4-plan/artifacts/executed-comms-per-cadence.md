# executed comms per cadence

_Produced by: Execute stakeholder communication cadence_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The archive of communications actually delivered against the stakeholder comms plan — meeting invites and minutes, status emails, briefing decks, memos — tied to the cadence they were intended to satisfy.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Plans aren't comms; comms are comms. Logging executed comms is how you prove the cadence is running, catch dropped threads, and have a trail when a stakeholder claims they "weren't told" — which always happens sooner or later.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Log entries record date, audience, channel, topic, and artifact link for each comm
- [ ] Meeting minutes capture decisions and follow-ups
- [ ] Deck and email snapshots are retained in a central location
- [ ] Gaps against planned comms are marked with a recovery note
- [ ] Stakeholder feedback is captured against the relevant entries
- [ ] Each entry links back to the stakeholder comms plan item it fulfills

## What it contains

- Log of comms sent: date, audience, channel, topic, link to artifact
- Meeting minutes with decisions and follow-ups
- Deck/email snapshots retained in a central location
- Gaps where planned comms were missed, with a recovery note
- Feedback captured from stakeholders
- Links back to the stakeholder comms plan entries they fulfill

## Inputs you rely on

- Stakeholder comms plan
- Governance calendar
- Feature/epic status report and weekly status content
- RAID log updates needing disclosure
- Source comms artifacts from PO, Sponsor, and BA

## Who consumes it

- Business Analyst — refines the comms plan based on what worked
- Product Sponsor — knows the organization is informed at the right altitude
- Auditors and governance reviewers — trace disclosures to dates
- Incoming PMs — onboard faster with an auditable comms trail

## Common pitfalls

- Executed comms live in 15 inboxes with no central record
- No gap-tracking so a missed exec update goes unnoticed for weeks
- Confusing meeting invites with actual delivery of message
- Logging comms but not capturing the decisions or follow-ups they produced
