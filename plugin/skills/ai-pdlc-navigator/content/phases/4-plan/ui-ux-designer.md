# UI/UX Designer in Plan

Plan is a quiet phase for you by design — the approved design package from Design is what the team builds against, and Plan is about standing up delivery machinery, not redesigning. Your job here is to stay available for clarification, make sure design assets are accessible to engineering, and keep an eye on the backlog so stories don't drift from the approved UX.

## What you do

- Stay available for developer clarifications on the approved design package.
- Confirm design assets and tokens are wired into the environment/pipeline so implementation stays faithful.
- Review decomposed work items as they land to catch UX drift before it becomes code.
- Begin scoping the visual QA and accessibility tests you'll own in Verify.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [delivery-ready work items](artifacts/delivery-ready-work-items.md) — Product Owner owns; you sanity-check UX fidelity of decomposed stories.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — you flag work items where decomposition is drifting from approved design.
- **Developer** — you answer implementation questions against the design spec and interactive prototype.
- **Site Reliability Engineer** — you confirm design assets / tokens are deployable through the new pipeline.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

You enter Build as a consultant to engineering, not a producer of new designs. You know Plan is done when decomposed stories trace cleanly back to the approved design package and your Verify-phase test plans (visual QA, accessibility) are scoped. In Build you'll defend fidelity; in Verify you'll validate it.
