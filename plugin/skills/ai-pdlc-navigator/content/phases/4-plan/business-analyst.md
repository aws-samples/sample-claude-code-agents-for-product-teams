# Business Analyst in Plan

You are here to keep the thread from strategy to work item intact and to make benefits a promise the org can actually measure. Plan turns approved requirements into decomposed work items, a benefits realization plan, and a stakeholder comms plan so that Build delivers against evidence, not memory.

## What you do

- Partner with the Product Owner to decompose features into delivery-ready work items with acceptance criteria and traceability intact.
- Author the benefits realization plan so that every KPI has an owner, a baseline, and a measurement mechanism.
- Draft the stakeholder communication plan so affected audiences hear the right message at the right cadence.
- Preserve the traceability thread (requirements → design → work item → test) as decomposition happens.

## Artifacts you own

- [benefits plan](artifacts/benefits-plan.md) — how benefits get measured and when.
- [stakeholder comms plan](artifacts/stakeholder-comms-plan.md) — who hears what, when, and from whom.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [delivery-ready work items](artifacts/delivery-ready-work-items.md) — Product Owner owns; you carry acceptance criteria and traceability.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'. The Sponsor approves your benefits plan ([approved benefits plan](outcomes/approved-benefits-plan.md)); the PM executes the comms cadence you authored.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — you decompose features together; they provide value order, you provide acceptance clarity and traceability.
- **Product Sponsor** — approves your benefits plan; you make sure the benefits are measurable and owned.
- **Project Manager** — executes your comms plan on cadence; you provide the content and audiences.
- **QA/Tester** — will map test cases to your acceptance criteria in Build; you hand them clean, testable criteria.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

Your decomposed work items feed directly into the first iteration. In Build you shift from authoring to defending — clarifying requirements mid-flight, tracking benefits assumptions against emerging reality, and impact-analyzing scope changes. You know Plan is done when work items are delivery-ready, the benefits plan is approved, and the comms cadence is running.
