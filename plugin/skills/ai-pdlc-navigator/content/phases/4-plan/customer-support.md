# Customer Support in Plan

Plan is a quiet phase for you — your ramp-up for onboarding, health scoring, and ticket handling happens largely in Verify and Launch. Your job here is to stay looped into the delivery baseline so you know when training and process cutover have to be ready, and to begin scoping the recruitment pipeline for the UAT / beta cohort.

## What you do

- Track the baselined schedule so support readiness (training, KB seeding, runbooks) tracks to the launch window.
- Begin identifying UAT / beta recruitment candidates with Sales & Marketing.
- Refresh the support process model from Define against any scope changes.
- Align with the Technical Writer on what user-facing docs need to exist for support to be effective.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't formally contribute to a Plan-phase artifact — your work is schedule alignment and cohort prep.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Sales & Marketing** — joint planning for UAT / beta cohort recruitment.
- **Technical Writer** — align on what docs you need to deflect tickets at launch.
- **Project Manager** — you're on the governance calendar so support readiness stays tracked.
- **Product Owner** — you flag known product gaps that will hit support at launch.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

In Build you stay aligned as the product takes shape. In Verify you recruit and onboard the UAT / beta cohort with Sales & Marketing — your first real producer moment. In Launch you cut over support processes. You know Plan is done when cohort candidates are identified, the support process model is current, and training scope is understood.
