# Technical Writer in Define

You are still off-stage but should start listening more actively. The approved requirements and process models in Define describe how the product will work — your docs IA will need to cover those same surfaces. Pay attention especially to external-facing concepts and APIs, because those become your reference-doc backbone later.

## What you do

- Read the [requirements document](artifacts/requirements-document.md) and [process models](artifacts/process-models.md) — these become your docs IA skeleton
- Note user-visible terminology and concepts in the [signed product charter](artifacts/signed-product-charter.md) and [MVP scope statement](artifacts/mvp-scope-statement.md)
- Start a terminology list — the vocabulary being chosen now will leak everywhere

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — their requirements document and process models are your primary reading
- **Product Owner** — their MVP scope statement tells you what must have docs at launch

## Handoff into Design

Nothing you produce is a hard handoff into [Design](../3-design/README.md), and your volume picks up in Verify and Launch when docs land for real. The quality bar is that by the time the UI/UX Designer produces journey maps in Design, you can align a docs IA to them quickly. You're done when you know the product's user-visible surfaces and have a rough idea of the docs scope for MVP launch.
