# Project Manager in Define

You run the operating rhythm that turns Discover artifacts into approved requirements, a signed charter, and a first-pass schedule. Define has a lot of moving parts — requirements, NFRs, regulations, GTM, estimation, dependencies — and the Sponsor's stage-gate arrives faster than anyone expects. Your job is to make that convergence happen on time with no surprises.

## What you do

- Identify affected internal audiences — publish the [affected-audiences list](artifacts/affected-audiences-list.md)
- Charter development facilitation — produce the [charter draft](artifacts/charter-draft.md) the Sponsor and PO sign
- Define-phase stakeholder workshops — run the sessions that produce [stakeholder workshop outcomes](artifacts/stakeholder-workshop-outcomes.md)
- Baseline schedule draft — put out the [draft project schedule](artifacts/draft-project-schedule.md)
- Co-own dependency mapping with the Architect and estimation timing with the Developer

## Artifacts you own

- [affected-audiences list](artifacts/affected-audiences-list.md) — who inside the org is changed by this initiative
- [charter draft](artifacts/charter-draft.md) — the document that becomes the signed product charter
- [stakeholder workshop outcomes](artifacts/stakeholder-workshop-outcomes.md) — decisions and actions from cross-functional workshops
- [draft project schedule](artifacts/draft-project-schedule.md) — the first-pass timeline that feeds the Plan-phase baseline

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [dependency list](artifacts/dependency-list.md) — owned by the Architect, you contribute delivery-side dependencies and feed your schedule
- [effort estimates](artifacts/effort-estimates.md) — owned by the Developer, you co-own the estimation process and roll up into the schedule

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input heavily into the Sponsor's [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md) through workshop outcomes, schedule, and dependency visibility.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — you package the gate pack; they sign
- **Product Owner** — you drive the charter draft and schedule to completion with them
- **Architect** — co-owns dependency mapping with you
- **Developer** — co-owns estimation with you; their numbers flow into your schedule
- **Business Analyst** — drives the requirements workshops you're facilitating

## Handoff into Design

Your Define outputs become the inputs to the [Design](../3-design/README.md)-phase plan you'll own there — design reviews, vendor selection coordination, design-phase schedule. The quality bar is that Design opens with a calendar, a dependency list, and a stakeholder map that everyone trusts. You're done when the exit decision runs on time, the charter gets signed, and Design starts without scheduling friction.
