# approved business case

_Produced by: Ratify business case_

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

## What this is

The business case drafted in Discover is now formally ratified by the Product Sponsor as the financial contract for the initiative. All future decisions (scope, pricing, sunset) will be judged against this.

## Why it matters

A ratified business case is what you hold every stakeholder accountable to. Without it, scope creep and pivoting happen without anyone noticing the economics have changed.

## What it contains

- Ratified ROI model with assumptions and sensitivity ranges
- Agreed success thresholds for continue/pivot/kill
- Named owner for tracking actuals vs. plan

## Definition of Done

- [ ] ROI model lists its assumptions and sensitivity ranges
- [ ] Continue / pivot / kill thresholds are stated with numeric values
- [ ] A named owner is assigned for tracking actuals against the plan
- [ ] Sponsor ratification is recorded against the ratified version

## Entry criteria

- ROI model refined with Discover-era evidence
- Unit economics assumptions documented
- Finance has signed off on the method

## Exit signal

Benefits realization tracking can begin, and any scope change now triggers business-case impact review.
