# Developer in Define

This is your first real engagement. You're not building yet, but you are sizing — and the estimates you produce now become the Plan-phase baseline the PM will track against. Your job is to estimate honestly on what you know, flag the unknowns loudly, and make sure the requirements and NFRs the BA and Architect are approving are actually sized into the budget.

## What you do

- Estimation / sizing — produce the [effort estimates](artifacts/effort-estimates.md) with the Project Manager
- Read the [requirements document](artifacts/requirements-document.md), [testable acceptance criteria](artifacts/testable-acceptance-criteria.md), and [NFR document](artifacts/nfr-document.md) carefully — challenge anything unsizable
- Flag missing enablers, unknown integrations, and risky NFRs early so they surface in Architect's feasibility findings

## Artifacts you own

- [effort estimates](artifacts/effort-estimates.md) — sized work with named assumptions and confidence (co-owned with Project Manager)

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md) through estimation realism.

## Who you work with

- **Project Manager** — co-owns estimation with you; their schedule rides on your numbers
- **Business Analyst** — you size their requirements; ambiguity becomes your risk
- **Architect** — their NFRs and technical feasibility findings shape the scope you're estimating
- **QA/Tester** — their acceptance criteria and test plan hooks inform the work you estimate

## Handoff into Design

In [Design](../3-design/README.md) you partner with the Architect on API specs and sign off on the build-ready package. Your Define estimates are what Plan baselines against and what you'll be held to. The quality bar is that your estimates have honest confidence intervals and that every unknown is flagged rather than guessed. You're done when the PM has a schedule that stands up to Sponsor scrutiny and you know what you'll be building.
