# Customer Support / Success in Define

You are here to make sure the support and success motion is designed into the product, not bolted on after launch. The commercial model and MVP scope being decided in Define will shape what onboarding, ticketing, and renewal look like — your support process model is how you make those implications explicit.

## What you do

- Support process definition — own the [support process model](artifacts/support-process-model.md)
- Watch the [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md) — pricing and packaging dictate support tier and SLA expectations
- Stay in sync with Sales & Marketing on the [draft GTM plan](artifacts/draft-gtm-plan.md) — launch posture sets activation expectations

## Artifacts you own

- [support process model](artifacts/support-process-model.md) — how tickets flow, how CS engages, how activation and renewal work

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md) through a realistic cost-to-serve model.

## Who you work with

- **Sales & Marketing** — their commercial model and GTM plan set support tiers and handoffs
- **Product Owner** — needs to know what support workload this scope implies
- **Business Analyst** — their process models and your support process model have to be internally consistent

## Handoff into Design

You don't own artifacts in [Design](../3-design/README.md), and you become materially active in Verify when you recruit UAT cohorts and in Launch when support processes go live. The quality bar is that the support process model is costed into the business case rather than discovered at launch. You're done when someone could estimate support staffing for MVP launch from your document.
