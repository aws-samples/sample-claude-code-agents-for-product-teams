# Product Sponsor in Define

You are here to turn the funded opportunity into a signed commitment. Define is where the business case gets ratified, the charter signed, and the commercial model chosen — these are your calls to make, ideally with enough pressure-testing that nothing ambiguous leaves the phase. You also run the next stage-gate, and this one costs real money to get wrong.

## What you do

- Ratify business case — convert the Discover draft into the [approved business case](outcomes/approved-business-case.md)
- Approve product charter — sign the [signed product charter](artifacts/signed-product-charter.md) with the Product Owner
- Commercial / delivery model decision — make the call on pricing, packaging, and delivery with the Product Owner and Architect, producing the [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md)
- Co-author KPI definitions and MVP scope — make sure the definitions of success and of "enough to ship" are ambitious and honest
- Run the Define stage-gate review — issue the [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md) with the Architect

## Artifacts you own

- [signed product charter](artifacts/signed-product-charter.md) — the formal commitment to what, why, and within what envelope (co-owned with Product Owner)

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [KPI definitions](artifacts/kpi-definitions.md) — owned by the Product Owner, you ensure the metrics ladder up to portfolio KPIs
- [MVP scope statement](artifacts/mvp-scope-statement.md) — owned by the Product Owner, you hold the line on what must and must not be in MVP

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved business case](outcomes/approved-business-case.md) — the ratified numbers the whole initiative is now measured against
- [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md) — pricing, packaging, and delivery model
- [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md) — go to Design, with conditions if any

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — co-signs the charter and sets KPIs and MVP scope with you; your primary partner this phase
- **Architect** — partners on the commercial/delivery model and co-owns the exit decision; you need their read on enterprise fit
- **Business Analyst** — ratified business case rides on their numbers
- **Project Manager** — runs the workshops, charter draft, and schedule you sign off on

## Handoff into Design

Your ratified business case, signed charter, and commercial model decision are the inputs every [Design](../3-design/README.md) artifact will reference. The quality bar is that when Design proposes architecture, the team can measure trade-offs against KPIs and scope that you've explicitly approved. You're done when the exit decision is logged, Design can start without re-litigating scope, and every downstream commitment can be traced back to a number or decision you personally own.
