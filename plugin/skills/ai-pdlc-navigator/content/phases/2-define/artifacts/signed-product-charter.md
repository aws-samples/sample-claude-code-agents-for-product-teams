# signed product charter

_Produced by: Approve product charter_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Product Owner

## What this is

A short, formally approved document that names the initiative, its sponsor, its scope boundaries, its decision rights, and its success criteria. It is the authorizing document the org recognizes — "this is a real initiative; these people can spend this money to do this work."

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

You sign this so authority, accountability, and boundaries are unambiguous from day one. Without a charter, every cross-team conflict escalates to you; with one, the PO and PM can point to named decision rights and resolve most issues without you.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Charter names the initiative, sponsor, product owner, and core team
- [ ] Business outcome, success metrics, and explicit non-goals are stated
- [ ] Scope boundaries and out-of-scope items are listed
- [ ] Decision rights for scope, budget, and trade-offs are assigned to named roles
- [ ] Budget envelope, schedule envelope, and governance cadence are specified

## What it contains

- Initiative name, sponsor, product owner, and core team
- Business outcome, success metrics, and explicit non-goals
- Scope boundaries and known out-of-scope items
- Decision rights (who approves scope, budget, trade-offs)
- Budget envelope and schedule envelope
- Governance cadence and escalation path

## Who consumes it

- Product Owner and Project Manager — the daily authority reference
- Architect and Security & Compliance — use it to understand scope boundaries
- Finance and the PMO — use it for portfolio and budget tracking

## Common pitfalls

- Wall-of-text charters — 20 pages nobody reads defeats the purpose
- Missing "non-goals" — scope creep hides in the absence of explicit exclusions
- Vague decision rights ("we will decide together") — defaults to escalating to Sponsor
- Signed and then shelved — revisit at phase gates and when scope changes materially
