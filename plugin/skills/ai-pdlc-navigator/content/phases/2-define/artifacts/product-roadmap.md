# product roadmap

_Produced by: Create & manage product roadmap_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Product Owner

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

A time-bounded sequence of outcome-oriented bets the product will pursue over the next 3–4 quarters, grouped by theme. It is not a dated feature list; it is a communication of intent with enough concreteness to plan around.

## Why it matters for the Product Owner

You own the roadmap because it is how the org understands what is coming and what is not, and how you hold the line against every ad-hoc request. A good roadmap makes prioritization conversations cheap; a bad one makes them continuous.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Roadmap is organized into Now / Next / Later (or named quarters) by theme
- [ ] Each theme states a problem, a hypothesis, and a target KPI movement
- [ ] Each theme carries a confidence level and what would change its sequence
- [ ] An explicit "not on the roadmap" list with rationale is included
- [ ] Document carries a version number and last-updated stamp

## What it contains

- Themes or outcomes for Now / Next / Later (or named quarters)
- For each theme: the problem, the hypothesis, the target KPI movement
- Confidence level and what would change the sequence
- Dependencies on other initiatives or platform work
- Explicit "not on the roadmap" list with rationale
- Version / last-updated stamp

## Inputs you rely on

- [Signed product charter](signed-product-charter.md) and [MVP scope statement](mvp-scope-statement.md)
- [KPI definitions](kpi-definitions.md) to ensure themes map to measurable movement
- [Capability gap report](capability-gap-report.md) and dependency view from Architect
- Sponsor's portfolio context and competitor moves

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — uses it for portfolio-level sequencing conversations
- Business Analyst — aligns requirements and benefits tracking to roadmap themes
- Sales & Marketing — previews to customers and builds GTM pipeline around it
- Architect — sequences platform and enabler work against it

## Common pitfalls

- Dated feature commitments that become liabilities the minute they slip
- Theme soup — vague headings with no hypotheses or metrics
- No "not on the roadmap" — every unscoped ask drifts back in
- Never versioned, so stale versions circulate as current
