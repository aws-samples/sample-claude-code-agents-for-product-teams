# Site Reliability Engineer in Iterate

Iterate is a reflection phase for you. You're not the lead on any Iterate-phase artifact, but your Operate-phase reliability, capacity, and cost evidence is the single highest-leverage input into the next cycle's SLO posture, architectural roadmap, and platform investment. Your job is to make sure that signal lands.

## What you do

- Synthesize Operate-phase reliability, capacity, and cost evidence into next-cycle recommendations.
- Feed SLO performance, error-budget patterns, and incident learnings into the retrospective.
- Contribute to architecture roadmap refresh and modernization planning with Architect.
- Contribute to tech-debt prioritization where reliability debt is material.
- Identify the reliability and observability investments the next cycle needs.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Architect** — bi-directional on architecture roadmap, capacity, and modernization.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on reliability-debt prioritization.
- **Project Manager** — bi-directional on retrospective and lessons-learned around incidents.
- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional when reliability economics affect the portfolio decision.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with a cycle's worth of reliability-economics evidence. Carry that into the next Discover cycle's feasibility memo and ROI technical inputs — real operating signal beats estimates every time, and your job is to make sure the next bet is scoped with that evidence, not against it.
