# benefits-realization report

_Produced by: Benefits realization review_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Business Analyst

## What this is

The evidence-based answer to "did the promised benefits happen?" Each benefit from the approved benefits plan gets a realized/partial/shortfall verdict with attribution, confounders, and sustainability assessment.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

Promises made at funding have to be closed out. A published realization report keeps the PDLC honest, sets the pattern for future business-case credibility, and is the primary input to the board conversation about continued investment.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Every committed benefit from the approved benefits plan has a realized/partial/shortfall verdict with cited evidence
- [ ] Attribution model and confidence are stated for each verdict
- [ ] Confounders (positive and negative) affecting outcomes are identified
- [ ] Sustainability of realized benefits is assessed
- [ ] Shortfall items link to the benefits-shortfall analysis

## What it contains

- Each committed benefit with verdict and evidence
- Attribution model and confidence
- Confounders that affected outcomes (positive and negative)
- Sustainability of realized benefits
- Lessons for future forecasting
- Link to benefits-shortfall analysis where applicable

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved benefits plan and ongoing benefits tracking
- Pre-launch metrics baseline and current KPIs
- Connected KPI data pipeline and business performance report
- Support/VoC trend analysis
- Customer and user feedback

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — input to portfolio investment decision
- Business Analyst — feeds updated business case and reforecast
- PMO and Finance — credibility check on future business cases
- Next Discover cycle — calibrated expectations

## Common pitfalls

- Soft benefits (brand, satisfaction) claimed without measurement
- Ignoring confounders, flattering the product's contribution
- Partial realization quietly reported as full
- No "is this durable?" check; short-term spikes counted as wins
