# updated business case

_Produced by: Business-case reforecast_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The reforecast business case — updated with actuals from Operate and Iterate, revised assumptions, and a go-forward projection. Replaces the original approved business case as the reference for ongoing investment decisions.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

The original business case was a best estimate; the updated one is evidence-grounded. Your reforecast is the artifact Sponsor and Finance will use for the portfolio investment decision and subsequent board conversations — rigor here determines whether future planning is calibrated or optimistic.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Revenue and margin projections are revised with actuals-grounded inputs
- [ ] CAC, LTV, and payback are updated
- [ ] Cost and resource plan, and the benefits plan, are refreshed consistently
- [ ] Sensitivity analysis and scenarios are included
- [ ] A change log captures what moved since the prior business case and why

## What it contains

- Revised revenue and margin projections
- Updated CAC, LTV, and payback
- Revised cost and resource plan
- Updated benefits plan
- Sensitivity analysis and scenarios
- What changed and why since the prior business case

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved (prior) business case
- Profitability assessment vs. business case
- Benefits-realization and shortfall reports
- KPI performance and adoption metrics
- Updated architecture roadmap and modernization plan (cost inputs)

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — feeds reforecast decision and portfolio investment decision
- Finance — updates budget and forecast
- Product Owner — baseline for updated roadmap
- Next Discover cycle — historical reality-check

## Common pitfalls

- Reforecast to hit a palatable number rather than to match reality
- Not showing sensitivity; single-point forecast looks overconfident
- Change log missing; reviewers can't trace what moved
- Updates benefits without updating costs, or vice versa
