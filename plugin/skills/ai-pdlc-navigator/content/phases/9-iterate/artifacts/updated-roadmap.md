# updated roadmap

_Produced by: Roadmap refresh_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Product Owner

**Stakeholders:** Product Sponsor

## What this is

The revised product roadmap informed by what actually happened post-launch — what to continue, accelerate, pivot, or kill, and the sequencing of the next one to three cycles. Aligned with the Sponsor's portfolio investment decision.

## Why it matters for the Product Owner

Your roadmap is the contract between Product, engineering, and the rest of the company. Refreshing it with post-launch evidence — adoption, retention, VoC, architecture signals — is how you avoid building on anecdote and instead keep the team working on the highest-value items the real world has revealed.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Themes and outcomes for the next 1-3 cycles are articulated
- [ ] Bets are sequenced with dependencies and fit within the per-quarter budget
- [ ] Killed or deferred items are listed with rationale
- [ ] Items reference the benefits-realization and feedback-insights evidence that drove changes
- [ ] A communication plan and target review cadence are included

## What it contains

- Themes and outcomes for the next 1–3 cycles
- Sequenced bets with dependencies and bets-per-quarter budget
- Items killed or deferred with rationale
- Links to benefits realization and feedback insights that drove changes
- Communication plan for the refresh
- Target review cadence

## Inputs you rely on

- KPI performance report and adoption metrics
- Feedback insights report and NPS & health scores
- Benefits-realization and profitability assessments
- Updated architecture roadmap and modernization plan
- Portfolio investment decision

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — alignment with portfolio posture
- Engineering and Design — future capacity planning
- Sales and Customer Success — customer conversations on what's coming
- Next Discover cycle — seeds the opportunity backlog

## Common pitfalls

- Roadmap driven by loudest voices, not the data in Iterate
- No kills; backlog grows indefinitely and velocity drops
- Detail too fine for the horizon; commits to quarters that should be themes
- Published but not communicated — stakeholders learn what's next from rumor
