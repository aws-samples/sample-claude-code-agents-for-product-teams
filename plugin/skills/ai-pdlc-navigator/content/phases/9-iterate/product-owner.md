# Product Owner in Iterate

Iterate is where you translate evidence into the next sequencing decisions. You refresh the roadmap with the Sponsor, track KPI performance, run experiments, groom bug and deprecation backlogs, and hand insights off to Discover so the next cycle starts with real signal, not fresh opinion.

## What you do

- Refresh the roadmap with the Product Sponsor so next-cycle investment is grounded in real performance.
- Track KPI performance with Business Analyst so sequencing decisions are evidence-driven.
- Run A/B tests and experiments with Developer to validate hypotheses cheaply.
- Hand off insights to Discover with Business Analyst so the next cycle inherits signal.
- Prioritize bug fixes so the quality backlog stays honest.
- Decide sunset / deprecation with the Product Sponsor when that's the right call.
- Contribute to adoption analysis and feedback insights with Business Analyst.

## Artifacts you own

- [updated roadmap](artifacts/updated-roadmap.md) — the refreshed roadmap, produced with the Product Sponsor.
- [KPI performance report](artifacts/kpi-performance-report.md) — the KPI readout, produced with Business Analyst.
- [experiment results](artifacts/experiment-results.md) — the A/B and experiment output, produced with Developer.
- [next-cycle opportunity backlog](artifacts/next-cycle-opportunity-backlog.md) — the hand-off to Discover, with Business Analyst.
- [prioritized bug list](artifacts/prioritized-bug-list.md) — your sequencing of bug-fix investment.
- [deprecation plan](artifacts/deprecation-plan.md) — the sunset decision, with Product Sponsor.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [adoption metrics](artifacts/adoption-metrics.md) — Business Analyst owns; you contribute sequencing implications.
- [feedback insights report](artifacts/feedback-insights-report.md) — Business Analyst owns; you contribute roadmap implications.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on roadmap, deprecation, and portfolio posture.
- **Business Analyst** — bi-directional on KPI, adoption, feedback, and next-cycle hand-off.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on experiments and bug-fix sequencing.
- **Architect** — bi-directional on roadmap architectural implications and tech-debt posture.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with a refreshed roadmap and a next-cycle opportunity backlog on record. Carry that into the next Discover cycle — your job there is to re-frame opportunity and re-validate problem-solution fit with the benefit of one full cycle of real-customer evidence. That's how each loop gets cheaper and sharper than the last.
