# UI/UX Designer in Verify

Verify is where you validate the product matches what you designed — pixel by pixel, interaction by interaction, accessibility check by accessibility check. Your job is to catch fidelity and accessibility drift before users do, and to sign off that what ships is what you approved.

## What you do

- Validate visual fidelity to the approved design — layout, type, color, spacing, motion, edge cases.
- Co-own accessibility testing with QA/Tester against WCAG and your accessibility design report.
- Sanity-check in-product copy alongside the Technical Writer as documentation is verified.
- Flag any UX debt that should be a known issue rather than a blocker.

## Artifacts you own

- [visual QA report](artifacts/visual-qa-report.md) — fidelity findings against approved design.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [accessibility audit report](artifacts/accessibility-audit-report.md) — QA/Tester owns; you provide design-side expertise.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **QA/Tester** — co-owns accessibility testing; primary counterpart on product validation.
- **Technical Writer** — aligns on in-product copy as docs get verified.
- **Developer** — receives your visual QA findings; fixes drift.
- **Product Owner** — decides when a UX gap is a known issue vs. a fix-before-ship.

## Handoff into [Launch](../7-launch/README.md)

Launch is a quiet phase for you — you observe, support the team, and start scoping Iterate-phase continuous-discovery inputs. You know Verify is done when the visual QA report is clean or triaged, accessibility audit is signed, and your sign-off is on the design fidelity of what's about to ship.
