# Business Analyst in Verify

You are here to prove the bet is still measurable. Verify is where your benefits plan meets reality — are the KPIs actually wired, is baseline data captured, is UAT confirming the value you promised? Your job is to coordinate UAT with business users and produce the benefits-readiness evidence the Sponsor needs to approve launch.

## What you do

- Coordinate UAT with business users so real workflows get tested against the product, not just scripted cases.
- Assess benefits readiness — are KPI instruments live, baselines captured, owners named, thresholds set.
- Keep the traceability matrix current so "what shipped" still maps back to "why we built it."
- Flag any late-breaking benefits gap so the Sponsor can decide before Launch, not after.

## Artifacts you own

- [UAT coordination & results](artifacts/uat-coordination-results.md) — business-user validation.
- [benefits-readiness report](artifacts/benefits-readiness-report.md) — is the bet measurable at Launch.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't formally contribute to other Verify-phase artifacts.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'. Your benefits-readiness report underwrites the Sponsor's launch-readiness assessment.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — uses your benefits-readiness report to sign UAT/beta.
- **Product Sponsor** — consumes your benefits-readiness assessment for the launch decision.
- **QA/Tester** — aligns on UAT orchestration so business and QA testing don't collide.
- **Site Reliability Engineer / Developer** — confirm KPI telemetry is actually flowing to dashboards.

## Handoff into [Launch](../7-launch/README.md)

In Launch you own the pre-launch metrics baseline and the live launch KPI dashboard, and coordinate process changes rolling out to impacted business teams. You know Verify is done when UAT is signed by business, benefits instrumentation is proven, and the traceability matrix is current.
