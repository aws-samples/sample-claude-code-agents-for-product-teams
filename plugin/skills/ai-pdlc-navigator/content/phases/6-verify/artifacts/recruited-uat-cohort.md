# recruited UAT cohort

_Produced by: Recruit & onboard UAT / beta users_

**Business outcome supported:** evidence-based readiness to launch — functional, non-functional, operational, and documentation — validated in production-like conditions.

**Primary owner:** Customer Support

**Stakeholders:** Sales & Marketing

## What this is

The identified, agreed, and onboarded set of users participating in UAT and beta — with their profile mix, agreements signed, access provisioned, and onboarding complete. Customer Support leads the recruit; Sales & Marketing often brings the accounts.

## Why it matters for the Customer Support owner

Launch goes better when the first customers through were pre-loved. A carefully recruited cohort gives you real signal before GA, shapes the KB with the questions actual users ask, and converts beta participants into early references for Sales and advocacy stories later.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Cohort roster shows segment/persona mix
- [ ] Legal agreements (beta terms, NDA, data handling) are signed
- [ ] Access and provisioning status is recorded per participant
- [ ] Onboarding completion evidence (training, docs, channel invites) is attached
- [ ] Expectations-setting summary and escalation channels/support coverage for beta issues are documented

## What it contains

- Cohort roster with segment/persona mix
- Legal agreements (beta terms, NDA, data handling)
- Access and provisioning status per participant
- Onboarding completion evidence (training, docs, channel invites)
- Expectations-setting summary shared with the cohort
- Escalation channels and support coverage for beta issues

## Inputs you rely on

- Approved launch cohort direction from Sponsor/PO
- User personas and affected-audiences list
- Customer Support process model
- Legal approval for beta participation terms
- Sales & Marketing account coordination

## Who consumes it

- Business Analyst — plans UAT coordination with this cohort
- Customer Support — covers their issues during the window
- Sales & Marketing — nurtures beta users as launch-day references
- Product Owner — weighs cohort feedback in scope decisions

## Common pitfalls

- Recruit dominated by friendly accounts — low-quality signal
- Onboarding skipped so participants can't actually use the product
- No feedback mechanism — cohort works in silence
- Beta lingers indefinitely with no exit and no GA date
