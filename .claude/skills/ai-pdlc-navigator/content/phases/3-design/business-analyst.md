# Business Analyst in Design

Design is where your requirements meet reality. As the UX lands, the architecture takes shape, and vendors come into the picture, you keep the traceability honest — making sure the design actually answers the approved requirements, updating the traceability matrix when designs change, and refreshing the ROI model with real design-era data. You're also the primary author for business rules and vendor evaluation.

## What you do

- Validate design against requirements — produce the [design-requirements validation](artifacts/design-requirements-validation.md) with Security & Compliance
- Update traceability (requirements → design) — maintain the [updated traceability matrix](artifacts/updated-traceability-matrix.md)
- Vendor / buy option evaluation — produce the [vendor evaluation scorecard](artifacts/vendor-evaluation-scorecard.md) with the Architect
- Business rules documentation — publish the [business-rules catalog](artifacts/business-rules-catalog.md)
- Refine cost-benefit model with design-era data — publish the [updated ROI model](artifacts/updated-roi-model.md)
- Co-own the [validated business-value alignment](artifacts/validated-business-value-alignment.md) with the Sponsor and PO

## Artifacts you own

- [design-requirements validation](artifacts/design-requirements-validation.md) — explicit confirmation each requirement is addressed by design (co-owned with Security)
- [updated traceability matrix](artifacts/updated-traceability-matrix.md) — requirements → design → test → control linkage refreshed for design
- [vendor evaluation scorecard](artifacts/vendor-evaluation-scorecard.md) — weighted comparison of vendors on functional, NFR, commercial, and risk axes (co-owned with Architect)
- [business-rules catalog](artifacts/business-rules-catalog.md) — the rules the product enforces, named and owned
- [updated ROI model](artifacts/updated-roi-model.md) — refreshed numbers reflecting design-era cost and benefit clarity

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [validated business-value alignment](artifacts/validated-business-value-alignment.md) — owned by the Sponsor with the PO; you supply the evidence that design still delivers

## Who you work with

- **Architect** — co-owns vendor evaluation with you; you hold the functional and business-rule side, they hold the technical fit
- **Security & Compliance** — co-owns design-requirements validation with you; you cover business rules, they cover controls
- **Product Owner** — consumes your validation and updated ROI into the approved design package
- **Product Sponsor** — consumes your updated ROI and validation into the business-value alignment review
- **UI/UX Designer** — their design specs and journeys are what you validate against requirements

## Handoff into Plan

Your updated traceability matrix and business rules catalog become reference material for the [Plan](../4-plan/README.md)-phase backlog decomposition and benefits plan you'll own there. The quality bar is that no requirement is orphaned, no business rule is ambiguous, and the ROI model reflects what the team actually plans to build. You're done when the approved design package can be decomposed into delivery-ready work items without rediscovering requirements or rules.
