# Project Manager in Design

Design has more moving pieces than any phase so far — parallel UX, architecture, security, and vendor tracks, each with their own reviews and approvals. Your job is to coordinate those tracks to land on one build-ready package, keep the steering and executive reviews fed, and run the vendor-selection process if the Sponsor's build-vs-buy decision needs one. When Design runs over, it's almost always a coordination problem.

## What you do

- Design-phase schedule & resource coordination — own the [design-phase plan](artifacts/design-phase-plan.md)
- Vendor selection process coordination — manage the [vendor selection process outputs](artifacts/vendor-selection-process-outputs.md)
- Design review scheduling & minutes — capture the [decision log from design reviews](outcomes/decision-log-from-design-reviews.md)
- Co-own the [confirmed scope/budget or change request](outcomes/confirmed-scope-budget-or-change-request.md) with the Sponsor and PO

## Artifacts you own

- [design-phase plan](artifacts/design-phase-plan.md) — the schedule and resource view for Design
- [vendor selection process outputs](artifacts/vendor-selection-process-outputs.md) — RFI/RFP outputs, evaluation materials, and selection records

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [confirmed scope/budget or change request](outcomes/confirmed-scope-budget-or-change-request.md) — owned by the Sponsor with the PO; you bring the coordination reality check and change-control discipline

## Outcomes you drive

- [decision log from design reviews](outcomes/decision-log-from-design-reviews.md) — the decisions and actions from every design review

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — you package steering material and run vendor selection for them
- **Product Owner** — co-owns the scope/budget reality check with you
- **Architect** — you schedule their reviews and coordinate vendor-technical evaluation
- **UI/UX Designer** — you coordinate their review cadence so the approved design package lands on time
- **Security & Compliance** — you schedule their threat model, vendor risk, and approval reviews

## Handoff into Plan

In [Plan](../4-plan/README.md) you become the operating heart — onboarded team, iteration plan, RAID log, governance calendar, communications cadence. Your Design-phase plan and decision log are the starting inventory. The quality bar is that every Design decision is captured, every review has minutes with owned actions, and no scope drift leaves the phase unlogged. You're done when Plan can kick off with a calendar, RAID log, and clean handoff from vendor selection.
