# Sales & Marketing in Design

You are off-stage in Design but following it closely. The approved design package defines the product you'll eventually sell, launch, and position — and the vendor decisions that land this phase may change the commercial story materially. Refine your draft GTM plan as the design sharpens; don't wait for Verify to start thinking about launch and enablement.

## What you do

- Read the [approved design package](outcomes/approved-design-package.md) as it comes together — positioning and demo design start here
- Track the [build-vs-buy decision](outcomes/build-vs-buy-decision.md) and [vendor partnership agreement](outcomes/vendor-partnership-agreement.md) — vendor co-branding and commercial terms change messaging
- Update your draft GTM plan with the design-era clarity

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others' (see below).

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — approved design package tells you what you'll be selling
- **Product Sponsor** — their build-vs-buy and vendor decisions change the commercial narrative
- **Customer Support** — your evolving GTM plan and their support process model have to stay aligned

## Handoff into Plan

You're largely off-stage in [Plan](../4-plan/README.md) as well; your heavy work is in Launch. The quality bar is that when Launch starts, the GTM plan reflects the real product rather than the Discover-era framing. You're done when you have a clear view of what will ship and a refreshed GTM direction to work from.
