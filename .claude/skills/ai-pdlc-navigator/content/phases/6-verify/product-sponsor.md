# Product Sponsor in Verify

You are here to convert evidence into commitments. Verify produces launch-readiness data — functional, non-functional, operational, compliance — and your job is to read it honestly and decide: is this safe to ship, to whom, and when? Your sign-offs here are the decisions that Launch executes against.

## What you do

- Review launch-readiness metrics so you're making decisions on evidence, not sentiment.
- Approve the beta / early-access cohort jointly with the Product Owner so launch scope matches risk posture.
- Approve the launch window / date jointly with the Release Manager so commitments are credible.
- Approve launch risk posture — what known issues and risks you'll accept — jointly with PO and Security & Compliance.
- Pre-brief analysts, press, and reference customers with Sales & Marketing so launch-day lands warm.

## Artifacts you own

- [launch-readiness assessment](artifacts/launch-readiness-assessment.md) — your read on readiness.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to other-owned artifacts this phase beyond your outcomes.

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved launch cohort](outcomes/approved-launch-cohort.md) — co-owned with Product Owner.
- [committed launch date](outcomes/committed-launch-date.md) — co-owned with Release Manager.
- [approved known-issues & risk acceptance](outcomes/approved-known-issues-risk-acceptance.md) — co-owned with Product Owner and Security & Compliance.
- [warmed market & analyst briefings](outcomes/warmed-market-analyst-briefings.md) — co-owned with Sales & Marketing.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — co-owns cohort and risk-posture decisions; they bring value perspective, you bring portfolio risk.
- **Release Manager** — co-owns the launch-date commitment; you validate the readiness evidence that date stands on.
- **Security & Compliance** — co-owns risk-posture with you; brings the compliance go/no-go.
- **Sales & Marketing** — co-owns analyst briefings; you set the narrative altitude.
- **Project Manager** — produces the launch-readiness status pack you review.

## Handoff into [Launch](../7-launch/README.md)

In Launch you own the board/investor narrative, live launch-day monitoring, continue/rollback authority, and celebration. You know Verify is done when readiness evidence is green (or known-issue acceptance is signed), the cohort and date are committed, and analysts are warmed. Any surprise on Launch day at this point is a Verify failure.
