# RAID log

_Produced by: Dependency / RAID management setup_

**Business outcome supported:** stand up the team, environments, pipeline, test strategy, SLOs, and work-ready backlog so engineering can begin productively on day one.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The consolidated Risks, Assumptions, Issues, and Dependencies log — one place where anything that could alter the plan is tracked with owner, date, and state. Sits alongside the risk register but captures the broader set.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

The RAID log is the single view that lets you run governance without panic. Keeping it honest and current is how you force assumptions into the open, convert dependencies into real commitments, and escalate issues before they eat the critical path.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Risks section summarizes entries traced to the risk register
- [ ] Assumptions list validation status and owner per entry
- [ ] Issues record remediation plan and ETA
- [ ] Dependencies capture direction, counterparty, committed date, and confidence
- [ ] Each entry has a change history
- [ ] Entries link to the decisions, meetings, or artifacts they tie to

## What it contains

- Risks (summary; full detail lives in the risk register)
- Assumptions with validation status and owner
- Issues (items that have already materialized) with remediation and ETA
- Dependencies with direction, counterparty, committed date, confidence
- Change history per entry
- Links to the decisions, meetings, or artifacts each entry ties to

## Inputs you rely on

- Risk register and dependency list
- Stakeholder workshop outcomes
- Cross-team sync notes and status updates
- RAID entries surfaced in daily standups
- Change-control package inputs

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — sees top issues and cross-team dependencies
- Architect and Security — track technical/compliance items they own
- PMO or governance forums — review cadence
- Incoming PMs — onboard from the RAID log as their primary handover artifact

## Common pitfalls

- Using RAID as an inbox, never closing entries — log becomes untrusted
- Risks duplicated in two places (register + RAID) with diverging states
- Dependencies listed with "TBD" owners — no one is actually committed
- Issues treated as risks to soften the message — escalation delayed
