# Product Sponsor in Plan

You are here to convert an approved design into a funded, staffed, baselined commitment — and then get out of the way. Plan is the last chance to resolve portfolio conflicts, set the risk appetite, and publicly signal that this bet is real. Your sign-offs here are what let engineering begin productively on day one of Build.

## What you do

- Approve the staffing & resourcing plan so the team exists on paper and in reality.
- Resolve cross-portfolio priority conflicts with the Architect so nothing critical is fighting for the same scarce capacity.
- Approve the delivery baseline (schedule & budget) jointly with the Project Manager — this is what you will later measure Build against.
- Set risk appetite and escalation thresholds so the team knows what to handle locally and what must come up to you.
- Kick the initiative off internally and make sure the broader org knows it's funded and moving.
- Approve the benefits realization plan so there is a clear definition of "this worked."

## Artifacts you own

You don't own conventional artifacts this phase — your outputs are outcomes (decisions) rather than documents.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [risk register](artifacts/risk-register.md) — Project Manager owns; you feed the executive-level risks and escalation thresholds.

## Outcomes you drive

- [approved resource commitment](outcomes/approved-resource-commitment.md) — the team is staffed and funded.
- [portfolio priority decision](outcomes/portfolio-priority-decision.md) — cross-initiative conflicts resolved.
- [baselined schedule & budget commitment](outcomes/baselined-schedule-budget-commitment.md) — the number you will measure against.
- [escalation policy](outcomes/escalation-policy.md) — risk appetite and when things come to you.
- [internal kickoff announcement](outcomes/internal-kickoff-announcement.md) — the initiative is real and visible.
- [approved benefits plan](outcomes/approved-benefits-plan.md) — how you will know it delivered.

## Who you work with

- **Project Manager** — baselines the schedule & budget with you and surfaces risks for the register; you give them escalation thresholds and authority.
- **Architect** — brings portfolio-level technical conflicts and skills gaps; you arbitrate priorities and approve the fix.
- **Product Owner** — feeds you the sequenced backlog; you ensure value sequencing matches the business case.
- **Business Analyst** — authors the benefits plan you approve; you confirm benefits are measurable and owned.

## Handoff into [Build](../5-build/README.md)

With staffing, baseline, risk appetite, and benefits plan approved, the team has air cover to start building. Your next job is holding scope discipline and re-validating the business case as reality emerges. You know Plan is done when the baseline is signed, the kickoff has landed, and the team is executing against a work-ready backlog.
