# Architect in Iterate

Iterate is where architectural evolution becomes intentional. You refresh the architecture roadmap, plan modernization work, prioritize tech debt with Developer, and contribute architectural root-cause to benefits shortfall analysis. Your output is a platform that fits the next cycle, not just the last one.

## What you do

- Refresh the architecture roadmap so next-cycle architectural investment is explicit.
- Plan technology refresh and modernization work grounded in Operate-phase reality.
- Groom the tech-debt backlog with Developer so debt decisions are strategic, not reactive.
- Contribute to commercial performance review with the Sponsor — architectural cost is commercial cost.
- Contribute to benefits shortfall root-cause with Business Analyst when architecture is the constraint.

## Artifacts you own

- [updated architecture roadmap](artifacts/updated-architecture-roadmap.md) — the refreshed architectural direction.
- [modernization plan](artifacts/modernization-plan.md) — the planned modernization/refresh work.
- [prioritized tech-debt list](artifacts/prioritized-tech-debt-list.md) — the tech-debt backlog, with Developer.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [profitability assessment vs. business case](artifacts/profitability-assessment-vs-business-case.md) — Product Sponsor owns; you contribute architectural cost drivers.
- [benefits-shortfall report](artifacts/benefits-shortfall-report.md) — Business Analyst owns; you contribute architectural root-cause.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on modernization strategy and commercial trade-offs.
- **Business Analyst** — bi-directional on benefits shortfall and cost drivers.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on tech-debt prioritization and modernization sequencing.
- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on roadmap architectural implications.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with an evidence-backed architecture roadmap and a pressure-tested modernization plan. Carry that into the next Discover cycle's feasibility, ROI technical inputs, and strategic alignment — the architecture that was fit-for-last-cycle rarely is fit-for-next, and your job is to make that transition deliberate.
