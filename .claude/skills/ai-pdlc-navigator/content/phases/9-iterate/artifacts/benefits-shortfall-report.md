# benefits-shortfall report

_Produced by: Benefits shortfall root-cause analysis_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Business Analyst

**Stakeholders:** Architect

## What this is

The root-cause analysis when promised benefits did not realize — what went wrong, which assumption failed, and what it would take to close the gap or accept the new reality. BA-led, Architect-consulted.

## Why it matters for the Business Analyst

Shortfalls are where the PDLC earns its integrity or loses it. A disciplined shortfall analysis distinguishes execution problems from assumption errors, protects future business cases from making the same mistakes, and turns a failure into an improvement to methodology.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Shortfall magnitude is quantified per benefit
- [ ] Contributing causes are categorized (discovery, execution, market, architecture)
- [ ] Specific business-case assumptions that failed are named
- [ ] Options to close the gap are listed with trade-offs
- [ ] A clear recommended posture (continue, pivot, sunset) is stated

## What it contains

- Shortfall magnitude per benefit
- Contributing causes (discovery, execution, market, architecture)
- Failed assumptions from the business case
- Options to close the gap
- Recommended posture (continue, pivot, sunset)
- Lessons for future forecasting

## Inputs you rely on

- Benefits-realization report and ongoing benefits tracking
- Profitability assessment vs. business case
- Adoption and KPI performance reports
- Win-loss insights and feedback insights
- Architectural and security posture reports

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — input to portfolio investment decision
- Business Analyst — updated business case and reforecast
- Next Discover cycle — calibrated assumptions
- PMO and lessons-learned repository — methodology improvement

## Common pitfalls

- Explaining away shortfall rather than owning it
- Root cause stops at "adoption was low" without asking why
- No link to business-case assumption that failed
- Recommendation hedges; Sponsor can't make a decision
