# deprecation plan

_Produced by: Sunset / deprecation_

**Business outcome supported:** close the loop — measure actual performance against the business case, decide continue/pivot/sunset, and feed validated learning back into Discover for the next cycle.

**Primary owner:** Product Owner

**Stakeholders:** Product Sponsor

## What this is

The formal plan to sunset a feature, API, tier, or entire product — announcement schedule, migration support, end-of-life date, and legal/contract implications.

## Why it matters for the Product Owner

Sunsetting is hard but necessary; carrying every feature forever erodes margin, velocity, and product coherence. A disciplined deprecation plan makes the decision transparent, gives customers a fair migration path, and protects the company from customer-contract liability.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Scope of what is being deprecated is explicitly defined
- [ ] Rationale ties to adoption, margin, or strategy evidence
- [ ] Timeline specifies announce, soft sunset, and end-of-life dates
- [ ] Migration path and tooling offered to customers are described
- [ ] Legal, compliance, and contract implications plus exception policy are addressed

## What it contains

- Scope of what is being deprecated
- Rationale tied to adoption/margin/strategy
- Timeline (announce, soft sunset, end-of-life)
- Migration path and tooling offered
- Affected customers and comms plan
- Legal, compliance, and contract implications
- Exception policy

## Inputs you rely on

- Adoption metrics (low usage evidence)
- Margin & CAC/LTV report (cost-to-serve)
- Customer feedback and support/VoC analysis
- Contract terms and service commitments
- Architecture roadmap (what replaces it, if anything)

## Who consumes it

- Affected customers — migrate
- Customer Success and Support — save-plays and migration support
- Sales — stops selling the deprecated item
- Legal — validates contract compliance
- Engineering — plans removal

## Common pitfalls

- Announcing without a credible migration path
- Timeline too aggressive for enterprise contracts
- Back-channel exceptions that never end
- Communication to customers too late; pipeline and churn impact
