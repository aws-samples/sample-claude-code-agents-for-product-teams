# Security & Compliance in Iterate

Iterate is where you close the loop on posture and pre-empt what's next. You deliver the security posture review based on a cycle's worth of operating evidence, monitor regulatory change, and translate both into the next-cycle controls and compliance work.

## What you do

- Deliver the security posture review — vulnerability classes, incident trends, control effectiveness.
- Monitor regulatory change so the next cycle starts with current obligations.
- Translate posture findings into concrete next-cycle controls work.
- Feed regulatory and posture insights into the Iterate portfolio decision.

## Artifacts you own

- [security posture report](artifacts/security-posture-report.md) — the cycle-end posture readout.
- [regulatory change impact](artifacts/regulatory-change-impact.md) — the scan for new or changed obligations.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into others'.

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional on posture findings that affect portfolio decisions.
- **Architect** — bi-directional on security roadmap and modernization.
- **Developer** — bi-directional on posture-driven remediation and secure pattern adoption.
- **Project Manager** — bi-directional on lessons-learned capture for controls and response.

## Handoff into [the next Discover cycle](../1-discover/README.md)

You exit Iterate with a calibrated posture view and a regulatory-change impact summary. Carry that into the next Discover cycle's regulatory risk flag and data-sensitivity early read — knowing what's changed is how each cycle avoids re-learning expensive compliance lessons.
