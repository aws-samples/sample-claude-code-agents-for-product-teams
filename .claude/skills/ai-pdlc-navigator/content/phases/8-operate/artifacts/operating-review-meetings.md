## What this is

The cadence of steady-state review meetings the PM coordinates — operational performance review, business review, renewal forecast, security posture — with agendas, inputs, attendees, and decision logs.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

After hypercare, project governance gives way to operating rhythm. Without a PM-owned cadence, reviews drift, get skipped, or become unstructured status readouts. A tight cadence keeps decisions flowing and prevents issues from quietly accumulating until the next reforecast.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] The meeting calendar lists purpose, frequency, and attendees per meeting.
- [ ] Each meeting has a standard agenda and named required inputs.
- [ ] A decision-log template and archive are in place.
- [ ] Action items are tracked across meetings with owners.
- [ ] Escalation triggers between meetings and links to performance reports are documented.

## What it contains

- Meeting calendar with purpose, frequency, and attendees
- Standard agendas and required inputs per meeting
- Decision log template and archive
- Action-item tracking across meetings
- Cross-linkage to performance reports
- Escalation triggers between meetings

## Inputs you rely on

- Operational performance review and business performance report
- Margin & CAC/LTV report and revenue report
- Architectural health and security posture reports
- Governance calendar carried forward from Plan
- Steady-state ownership from the operating handoff

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor, PO, and role leads — participate and decide
- New team members — understand the rhythm
- Iterate phase — retrospective inputs
- Auditors — evidence of governance cadence

## Common pitfalls

- Meetings held but no decisions logged
- Attendee list bloats; no one can speak candidly
- Same status every meeting; no action-item follow-through
- No escalation path when a meeting uncovers a Sponsor-level issue
