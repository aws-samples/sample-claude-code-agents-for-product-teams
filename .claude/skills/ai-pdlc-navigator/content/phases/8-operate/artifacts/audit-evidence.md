## What this is

The continuously-collected pool of control evidence that demonstrates the product is operated in line with regulatory and contractual commitments — access reviews, change approvals, backup tests, vulnerability scans, training completion, and more. Organized by control and framework.

## Why it matters for the Security & Compliance team

Audits happen; gathering evidence in a fire drill is expensive and risky. A clean, continuous evidence pool lets attestations (SOC 2, ISO, HIPAA, PCI, GDPR etc.) pass with minimal engineering drag and keeps the company shippable under its obligations.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Each evidence item is mapped to at least one control and framework.
- [ ] Access reviews, change approvals, and patch records are captured for the period.
- [ ] Backup/DR test results and vulnerability scan/remediation records are included.
- [ ] Training and awareness completion rates are recorded.
- [ ] Incident response documentation is linked for every in-scope incident.

## What it contains

- Evidence mapped to controls mapped to frameworks
- Access review results, change approvals, patch records
- Backup/DR test results
- Vulnerability scan and remediation records
- Training and awareness completion
- Incident response documentation

## Inputs you rely on

- Evidence collection plan from Plan phase
- Access review results and patched dependencies
- DR test results and security monitoring alerts
- Security incident postmortems
- Applicable-regulations list and control-to-test coverage map

## Who consumes it

- External auditors — validate control operation
- Legal/Privacy and enterprise sales — customer security questionnaires
- Audit report / attestation authors
- Sponsor — risk posture readouts

## Common pitfalls

- Evidence collected only before audit — stale, inconsistent, or incomplete
- Screenshots without timestamps or system context
- Controls mapped to one framework, forcing rework for every other audit
- Evidence stored where future team members can't find it
