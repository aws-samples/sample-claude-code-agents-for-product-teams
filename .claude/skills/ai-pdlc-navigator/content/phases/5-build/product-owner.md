# Product Owner in Build

You are here to accept what the team builds, in the order they build it, and to keep the backlog honest as the product meets reality. Build is where your Plan-phase backlog gets tested against what's actually possible — and where stakeholder pressure to change scope has to be filtered through a single, visible decision-maker: you.

## What you do

- Review progress and accept increments with the Project Manager so every delivered slice has a clear "done" and "not done."
- Co-own scope-change decisions with the Sponsor so emergent asks don't quietly bloat the build.
- Keep the backlog refined ahead of the team — the next two iterations always delivery-ready.
- Stay close to Business Analyst clarifications and Architect conformance findings so the product you're accepting is the one you planned.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own standalone Build-phase artifacts — your output is accepted increments.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [scope-change decisions](outcomes/scope-change-decisions.md) — Product Sponsor owns the outcome; you frame the scope trade-off.

## Outcomes you drive

- [accepted increments](outcomes/accepted-increments.md) — co-owned with Project Manager.

## Who you work with

- **Project Manager** — co-owns increment acceptance; they run the cadence, you make the calls.
- **Product Sponsor** — arbitrates scope changes you surface; you do the analysis, they pay the political cost.
- **Business Analyst** — keeps requirements clarified mid-flight so acceptance is unambiguous.
- **Architect** — flags conformance or technical-impact concerns on what you're about to accept.
- **QA/Tester** — confirms "works" with evidence, not assertion.

## Handoff into [Verify](../6-verify/README.md)

In Verify you co-sign UAT/beta, approve the launch cohort with the Sponsor, and help commit to the launch window. You know Build is done when all in-scope increments are accepted, the business case is still intact, and you're ready to start approving risk (not just features). Your backlog discipline in Build determines whether Verify is validation or rescue.
