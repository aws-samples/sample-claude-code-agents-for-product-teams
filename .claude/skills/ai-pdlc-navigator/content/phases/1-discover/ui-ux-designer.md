# UI/UX Designer in Discover

You are here to make sure the team is solving a real problem for real people before anyone draws a screen. Discover is almost entirely research for you: pair with the BA on problem validation, and own the persona definition that the rest of the lifecycle will keep returning to. Your output this phase is thin in volume but load-bearing — get the users wrong here and everything downstream bends toward the wrong thing.

## What you do

- Persona / target user definition — own the [user personas](artifacts/user-personas.md), with the BA supplying quantitative grounding
- Partner on user / problem validation research — contribute qualitative depth to the [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md)

## Artifacts you own

- [user personas](artifacts/user-personas.md) — the named segments the product is for, with pains, jobs, and context (co-owned with Business Analyst)

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md) — owned by the Business Analyst, you run the interviews and synthesize behavioral evidence

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md) through persona credibility and problem validation evidence.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — your primary partner; they bring market and numbers, you bring interviews and behavior
- **Product Owner** — consumes personas and the validated problem statement into the vision and business case
- **Sales & Marketing** — shares competitive and segmentation signal you can use to prioritize personas

## Handoff into Define

Your personas become the anchor for every scope and UX decision in [Define](../2-define/README.md) and especially for your own journey mapping and IA work in Design. The quality bar is that a stranger reading the personas could tell the team to drop a proposed feature because it doesn't serve a primary persona. You're done when the team argues about priorities through the lens of the personas rather than around them.
