# Business Analyst in Discover

You are the evidence function in Discover. While the PO frames the bet and the Sponsor lines up funding, you make sure the claims underneath have real data, real users, and a real market behind them. If Discover ships without your work, the stage-gate becomes a vibes check; with your work, it becomes a defensible investment decision.

## What you do

- Market & customer research — publish the [market analysis report](artifacts/market-analysis-report.md)
- User / problem validation — run the research, co-produce the [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md) with the UI/UX Designer
- Problem-opportunity analysis — size the prize in the [opportunity sizing](artifacts/opportunity-sizing.md)
- Cost-benefit / ROI analysis — build the [ROI model](artifacts/roi-model.md) that anchors the business case
- Feed personas work — contribute to [user personas](artifacts/user-personas.md) so the segments trace back to evidence

## Artifacts you own

- [market analysis report](artifacts/market-analysis-report.md) — external view of the space, trends, and competitive posture
- [validated problem statement](artifacts/validated-problem-statement.md) — the problem as real users describe it (co-owned with UI/UX Designer)
- [opportunity sizing](artifacts/opportunity-sizing.md) — TAM/SAM/SOM with assumptions named
- [ROI model](artifacts/roi-model.md) — the numbers the business case rests on

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [user personas](artifacts/user-personas.md) — owned by the UI/UX Designer, you supply the segmentation and quantitative anchors

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's [approved budget & investment commitment](outcomes/approved-budget-investment-commitment.md) and [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md) through the ROI model and validated problem evidence.

## Who you work with

- **UI/UX Designer** — partner for user validation and personas; you bring the analytical frame, they bring the qualitative depth
- **Product Owner** — consumes your ROI model, opportunity sizing, and market read into the business case and vision
- **Product Sponsor** — scrutinizes your numbers in the stage-gate review; you need to know the corporate hurdle rates and portfolio lens
- **Sales & Marketing** — their competitive landscape and pricing benchmarks feed your market analysis and ROI model
- **Architect** — their technical cost inputs plug into your ROI model

## Handoff into Define

Your Discover work becomes the floor for [Define](../2-define/README.md). The ROI model is refined into the ratified business case; the validated problem statement drives the requirements document; opportunity sizing feeds MVP scope decisions. The quality bar is traceability — every number sourced, every user quote tagged, every assumption flagged so sensitivity can be tested in Define. You're done when the Sponsor can defend your numbers without you in the room.
