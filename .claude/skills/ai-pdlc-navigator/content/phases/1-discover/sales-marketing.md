# Sales & Marketing in Discover

You are here to make sure the opportunity being framed is actually commercially viable — that it plays in a real market at a defensible price, against competitors you already know how to sell against. Discover is where you bring the outside-in revenue view into what would otherwise be an inside-out product conversation.

## What you do

- Competitor analysis — publish the [competitive landscape report](artifacts/competitive-landscape-report.md)
- Pricing research — publish the [pricing benchmark report](artifacts/pricing-benchmark-report.md)
- Feed the business case — your competitive and pricing views plug directly into the PO/Sponsor ROI model and positioning

## Artifacts you own

- [competitive landscape report](artifacts/competitive-landscape-report.md) — who else plays here, how they win, where the gap is
- [pricing benchmark report](artifacts/pricing-benchmark-report.md) — what the market pays and how competitors package

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's [Discover exit decision](outcomes/discover-exit-decision.md) through the market and pricing evidence that anchors the business case.

## Who you work with

- **Business Analyst** — your competitive and pricing data plug into their ROI model and market analysis
- **Product Owner** — consumes your reports into the vision and business case; they need to know which competitor segments they're targeting
- **Product Sponsor** — will ask hard questions about positioning and price realism at the stage-gate

## Handoff into Define

Your reports become the foundation for the draft GTM plan you'll own in [Define](../2-define/README.md) and the pricing and packaging decisions that follow. The quality bar is that the PO can defend pricing assumptions in the business case by pointing to your benchmark rather than to gut feel. You're done when the team can articulate not just "what this is" but "who we beat and how we're priced" — the revenue shape of the bet is visible.
