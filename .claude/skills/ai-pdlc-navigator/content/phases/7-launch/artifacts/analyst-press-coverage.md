# analyst/press coverage

_Produced by: External analyst & press engagement_

**Business outcome supported:** product live, teams enabled, customers notified, baseline metrics captured, and launch stabilized through hypercare.

**Primary owner:** Product Sponsor

**Stakeholders:** Sales & Marketing

## What this is

The captured set of external analyst notes, press coverage, and influencer commentary produced around launch — quotes, briefing summaries, published articles, and any embargoed pieces releasing on launch day. It is the evidence that the market heard the message you intended.

## Why it matters for the Product Sponsor

Analyst and press coverage moves pipeline, deal velocity, and category perception — levers you cannot pull again cheaply. Good coverage gives you quotable proof for the board; bad coverage or silence tells you positioning and readiness need correction before you scale spend.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Analyst briefing attendee list names every participant and lists the takeaway themes recorded from each briefing.
- [ ] Every quote carries explicit clearance status for reuse in sales and marketing assets.
- [ ] Press hits are logged with outlet, reach figure, and sentiment assessment.
- [ ] Embargo status and scheduled publication date are stated for each piece.
- [ ] Correction log captures every inaccurate coverage item and the correction status.

## What it contains

- Analyst briefing attendee list and takeaway themes
- Quotes cleared for use in sales and marketing assets
- Press hits with outlet, reach, and sentiment
- Embargo status and scheduled publication dates
- Correction log for any inaccurate coverage
- Follow-up opportunities (podcasts, deeper briefings)

## Inputs you rely on

- Warmed market & analyst briefings from Verify
- Launch announcement and marketing collateral
- Pricing & packaging plan (analysts always ask)
- Sales & Marketing spokesperson availability
- Legal review of public statements

## Who consumes it

- Sales & Marketing — weave quotes into decks, battlecards, and website
- Product Owner — calibrate roadmap messaging against analyst takes
- Board/investor narrative — cites coverage as external validation
- Customer Support and Success — anticipate customer questions sourced from articles

## Common pitfalls

- Briefing analysts without a crisp one-pager and demo — coverage goes generic
- Ignoring lukewarm coverage instead of feeding it back to Product and PMM
- No process to clear quotes for reuse, so sales loses leverage
- Treating coverage as a launch-day event instead of a 2–4 week rollout
