## What this is

The record of launch-day communications that actually went out — internal all-hands, customer notices, status page updates, partner alerts, press releases, and social posts — with timestamps, audiences, and sign-off trail.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

Comms are promises. Missing or delayed ones erode trust (customers, partners, support reps waiting for guidance); off-script ones expose legal and PR risk. A tracked execution log makes it auditable who said what, when, to whom, and catches drift against the plan in real time.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Comms schedule lists owner, channel, audience, and send time for every planned item.
- [ ] Approval status and legal or PMM sign-off are recorded per item.
- [ ] Actual send timestamps are captured and any delta from plan is noted.
- [ ] Content snapshots or links to canonical versions are attached for every sent message.
- [ ] Amendments and corrections shipped during the day are logged.

## What it contains

- Comms schedule with owner, channel, audience, and send time
- Approval status and legal/PMM sign-off per item
- Actual send timestamps and any deltas from plan
- Content snapshots (or links to canonical versions)
- Reactions and response needs captured during the day
- Amendments and corrections shipped

## Inputs you rely on

- Stakeholder comms plan and cadence from Plan phase
- Launch run-of-show with comms triggers
- Internal launch announcement and launch announcement emails/notices
- Board/investor launch narrative for exec-level alignment
- Legal approval on external claims

## Who consumes it

- Sponsor — confirms message discipline for the board
- Sales & Marketing — mirrors comms into pipeline touches
- Customer Support — anticipates inbound triggered by each message
- Retrospective — post-launch audit of comms effectiveness

## Common pitfalls

- Untracked ad-hoc comms that contradict the planned message
- Internal comms going out before external — leaks and confusion
- No timestamp trail, so postmortems can't reconstruct the sequence
- Forgetting quiet audiences (partners, resellers, regulated customers)
