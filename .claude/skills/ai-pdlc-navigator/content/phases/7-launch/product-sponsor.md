# Product Sponsor in Launch

Launch is your moment to put portfolio weight behind the bet — briefing the board, rallying the company, and holding the authority to stop the release if the numbers demand it. You narrate the "why now" externally and internally, and you are the single accountable voice for continue-or-rollback while the product is live.

## What you do

- Brief the board and investors on the launch so the narrative matches the business case you committed to.
- Deliver the internal all-hands launch communication that lines the company up behind the release.
- Monitor real-time launch metrics so the portfolio view stays tied to ground truth on launch day.
- Hold rollback decision authority with the Release Manager — you own the final continue-or-rollback call.
- Engage external analysts and press with Sales & Marketing to shape third-party coverage.
- Recognize the team and capture wins so the organization learns what good looks like.

## Artifacts you own

- [board/investor launch narrative](artifacts/board-investor-launch-narrative.md) — the executive-level story of what shipped and why it matters.
- [internal launch announcement](artifacts/internal-launch-announcement.md) — the company-wide message that anchors the launch.
- [analyst/press coverage](artifacts/analyst-press-coverage.md) — the curated external coverage you and PMM land.

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [pricing & packaging plan](artifacts/pricing-packaging-plan.md) — Sales & Marketing owns; you ratify the commercial posture.

## Outcomes you drive

- [live launch-day status](outcomes/live-launch-day-status.md) — the authoritative read on how launch is going.
- [continue/rollback decision](outcomes/continue-rollback-decision.md) — your call, made with the Release Manager.
- [recognized team & captured wins](outcomes/recognized-team-captured-wins.md) — the wins and recognition that stick.
- [go/no-go decision](outcomes/go-no-go-decision.md) — Product Owner owns; you ratify as the accountable exec.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on go/no-go, launch narrative, and pricing posture.
- **Release Manager** — bi-directional on rollback authority and launch-day status.
- **Sales & Marketing** — bi-directional on analyst/press engagement and external story.
- **Project Manager** — bi-directional on command-center status and executive updates.

## Handoff into [Operate](../8-operate/README.md)

You exit Launch with the product live, an on-record narrative, and a team that knows it shipped. Carry the business-case expectations into Operate so the steady-state performance review you chair has a credible bar to measure against. Your posture shifts from launch-day authority to commercial accountability for revenue, margin, and SLO outcomes.
