# Security & Compliance in Launch

Launch is the last moment your controls and evidence have to stand up without qualification. You deliver the final legal & compliance sign-off, make sure approved risk posture is honored at go-live, and ensure nothing ships that puts the company or customers in an untenable regulatory position.

## What you do

- Deliver the final legal & compliance sign-off with the Product Owner before release executes.
- Confirm approved known-issues and risk-acceptance posture match what actually ships.
- Keep audit evidence collection running through the launch window.
- Stay reachable on launch day for any security or compliance anomaly that surfaces.

## Artifacts you own

You don't own any artifacts this phase — you contribute to others'.

## Artifacts you contribute to

You don't contribute to others' artifacts this phase.

## Outcomes you drive

- [legal approval](outcomes/legal-approval.md) — your final sign-off, delivered with the Product Owner.

## Who you work with

- **Product Owner** — bi-directional on legal sign-off and residual-risk acceptance.
- **Product Sponsor** — bi-directional when risk posture intersects the continue/rollback call.
- **Release Manager** — bi-directional on the readiness gate and compliance go/no-go inputs.
- **Architect** — bi-directional on any late security or privacy architectural questions.

## Handoff into [Operate](../8-operate/README.md)

You exit Launch with controls live and approvals on record. Carry that forward into Operate's audit evidence collection, periodic compliance audit, security monitoring, and patch cadence — controls only hold up over time if they're exercised, and Operate is where that happens.
