# Sales & Marketing in Define

You are here to get GTM planning started before the product is real — because sales enablement, positioning, and launch plans take months and you don't want them to begin at Verify. Your Discover pricing and competitive work plugs into the Sponsor's commercial model decision, and your draft GTM plan tells the org what kind of launch this is going to be.

## What you do

- GTM planning — initial — publish the [draft GTM plan](artifacts/draft-gtm-plan.md)
- Feed the [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md) with pricing, packaging, and competitive reads
- Stay in sync with the PO and Sponsor on the [signed product charter](artifacts/signed-product-charter.md) — positioning starts with the charter

## Artifacts you own

- [draft GTM plan](artifacts/draft-gtm-plan.md) — launch shape, channels, segments, early positioning

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input into the Sponsor's [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md) and [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md).

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — commercial model decision rides on your pricing and market reads
- **Product Owner** — consumes your GTM plan into roadmap sequencing and MVP scope
- **Customer Support** — your GTM plan shapes the support process model they're writing

## Handoff into Design

You don't own artifacts in [Design](../3-design/README.md), but your draft GTM plan becomes the foundation for the marketing collateral, launch announcement, and pricing/packaging plan you'll produce in Launch. The quality bar is that the GTM plan is specific enough to brief an analyst on, not so generic it could apply to any product. You're done when the commercial model is committed and the team knows what kind of launch this is.
