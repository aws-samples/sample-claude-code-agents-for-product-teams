# Product Owner in Define

Define is where the vision becomes a charter and the bet becomes a bounded scope. You own the roadmap shape, the KPIs, and the MVP line — and you co-sign the charter with the Sponsor. Your job is to push hard for clarity on "what does success look like" and "what is explicitly not MVP" so the rest of the org can plan against a commitment instead of an aspiration.

## What you do

- Create & manage product roadmap — publish the [product roadmap](artifacts/product-roadmap.md)
- Success metrics / KPI definition — define [KPI definitions](artifacts/kpi-definitions.md) with the Sponsor
- MVP / scope decision — draw the [MVP scope statement](artifacts/mvp-scope-statement.md) with the Sponsor
- Co-sign the product charter — own the Product Owner side of the [signed product charter](artifacts/signed-product-charter.md)
- Input on the commercial / delivery model — contribute to the [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md)

## Artifacts you own

- [product roadmap](artifacts/product-roadmap.md) — the sequenced view of what ships when
- [KPI definitions](artifacts/kpi-definitions.md) — success metrics with measurement method, not just names (co-owned with Sponsor)
- [MVP scope statement](artifacts/mvp-scope-statement.md) — what's in, what's out, what's conditional (co-owned with Sponsor)

## Artifacts you contribute to

- [signed product charter](artifacts/signed-product-charter.md) — owned by the Sponsor, you co-sign and defend the Product Owner side
- [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md) — owned by the Sponsor, you input on pricing and packaging implications for the backlog

## Outcomes you drive

You don't drive outcomes this phase — you input heavily into the Sponsor's [approved business case](outcomes/approved-business-case.md), [commercial model decision](outcomes/commercial-model-decision.md), and [Define exit decision](outcomes/define-exit-decision.md).

## Who you work with

- **Product Sponsor** — co-owns the charter, KPIs, MVP scope, and commercial model with you
- **Business Analyst** — their requirements document, acceptance criteria, and traceability matrix give your backlog real shape
- **Architect** — their technical feasibility findings, NFRs, and solution recommendation shape what's possible in MVP
- **Project Manager** — runs the workshops and drafts the charter; your decisions land in their schedule and RAID

## Handoff into Design

Your roadmap, KPIs, MVP scope, and the signed charter anchor every [Design](../3-design/README.md) decision — what to prototype, what to threat-model, what to defer. The quality bar is that the scope line is sharp enough that when Architect and UI/UX propose options, you can choose between them in minutes instead of weeks. You're done when the charter is signed, the MVP line is defended, and Design has a prioritized roadmap they can plan against.
