# draft project schedule

_Produced by: Baseline schedule draft_

**Business outcome supported:** turn the validated opportunity into approved requirements, scope, NFRs, KPIs, regulatory scope, and a commercial model, with sized estimates ready to plan.

**Primary owner:** Project Manager

**Stakeholders:** _(none listed)_

## What this is

The first pass at a project schedule — phases, major milestones, critical path, and handoffs — based on current effort estimates and dependencies. It is the input to the Plan-phase baselined schedule, not yet the commitment.

## Why it matters for the Project Manager

You produce this so governance has a time-shape of the initiative before it signs the charter. A draft schedule shows where estimates are too optimistic for the gate dates people want, and exposes dependencies that need to start moving in Plan.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Schedule lists phases with expected durations
- [ ] Major milestones have rough target dates
- [ ] Critical path through the work is identified
- [ ] Dependency-driven handoffs name owners
- [ ] Schedule risks and their drivers are listed

## What it contains

- Phases and their expected duration
- Major milestones with rough target dates
- Critical path through the work
- Dependency-driven handoffs with owners
- Resource peaks and conflicts
- Key assumptions driving the schedule
- Known schedule risks and their drivers

## Inputs you rely on

- [Effort estimates](effort-estimates.md) and [capability gap report](capability-gap-report.md)
- [Dependency list](dependency-list.md)
- Portfolio calendar and resource availability
- Sponsor's target windows (launch events, regulatory deadlines)

## Who consumes it

- Product Sponsor — tests "is this achievable in the desired window?"
- Architect — sees where technical sequencing will bottleneck
- Business Analyst — sequences requirements refinement around it
- Project Manager (future you) — baselines it into the [iteration plan](../../4-plan/artifacts/iteration-plan.md) and the Plan-phase [baselined schedule](../../4-plan/outcomes/baselined-schedule-budget-commitment.md)

## Common pitfalls

- Gantt theater — overly detailed schedule built on fuzzy estimates
- Ignoring non-engineering work (legal, enablement, comms) on the critical path
- One-point duration estimates treated as facts
- No re-plan trigger criteria, so slippage is absorbed silently
